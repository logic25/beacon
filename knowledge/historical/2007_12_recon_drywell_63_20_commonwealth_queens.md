## Objections #1 and #2 — Drywell Requirements for New Roof Addition

**DOB Position:** New building addition requires drywells for storm water management.

**Argument:** The new roof of the addition is just 2% of the total roof area. The total developed storm water flow from the building roof including the new addition is 3.68 CFS. The net storm water flow change after adding the new addition is 0.077 CFS, approximately 2% of total developed flow. The existing storm drainage system had no problems. A 2% change will not affect it. Therefore new drywells are not required.

**Engineering Calculations (Wesler-Cohen Associates, calc by MC, checked NW, 12/5/07):**

Using the Rational Method: Q = A × C × I / 43,560. Where A = area (SF), C = runoff coefficient, I = rainfall intensity (inches/hr), 43,560 = SF-to-acres conversion. Design storm: 5-year return, 6-minute time of concentration, I = 5.95 in/hr.

A_add = 700 SF (addition roof). A_T = 27,000 SF (total roof). C_roof = 1.0 (100% runoff from roof). C_grass = 0.2 (existing grass area where addition is built).

Q_Dadd = 700 × 1.0 × 5.95 / 43,560 = 0.096 CFS (developed flow on added roof). Q_Dgrass = 700 × 0.2 × 5.95 / 43,560 = 0.019 CFS (prior developed flow on grass area). Q_T = 27,000 × 1.0 × 5.95 / 43,560 = 3.688 CFS (total developed flow on entire roof).

Net change: ΔQ = 0.096 − 0.019 = 0.077 CFS. Percentage change: 0.077 / 3.688 = 2.09%.

**DOB Reviewer Notes:** For objection on storm discharge — OK to accept engineer's calculation. See attached signed/sealed calculation.

## Practical Impact

**De minimis storm water argument works.** When a building addition adds only a small percentage to total roof area (here 2.09%), the DOB will accept an engineer's calculation showing drywells are not required. The key is providing signed/sealed calcs expressing the change as a percentage of total developed flow, not just the absolute CFS number.

**Rational Method formula is the standard:** Q = A × C × I / 43,560. For drywell arguments, calculate both the addition's flow and the prior condition (grass/pervious) to show the net delta. Design storm parameters: 5-year return frequency, 6-minute time of concentration.

**Vibration isolator pragmatism:** Even when manufacturer specs prove internal isolation is sufficient, the DOB may still require external isolators. The smart move (shown here) is to argue the technical position but offer to add isolators as a concession. This resolves the objection without extended dispute.

**Pricing note:** Storm water engineering calculations and recon preparation are additional services. If a project involves any roof addition in Queens (or any borough with drainage sensitivity), flag potential drywell objections in your scope and price accordingly.
