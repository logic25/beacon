# Reconsideration: Factory to Residential Conversion — 305 East 140th Street, Bronx

**Location:** 305 East 140th Street, Bronx | **Block:** 2315 | **Lot:** 22 | **Zoning District:** R6 | **Applicant:** Otte Assemblage — Michael Lisowski, R.A. (License #028026) | **Job Number:** 201008603 | **Examiner Who Issued Objections:** Joe Dellutri | **Objection Date:** February 17, 2006

This job generated three separate reconsiderations addressing zoning, building code compliance, and layout objections during a factory-to-residential conversion.

---

## Recon #1 — Zoning Use Group Objection (Objection #5)

**Recon Date:** April 6, 2006 | **Reviewer:** Kalyan K. Bhattacharyya | **Outcome:** ACCEPTED as stated. Use of 5th floor may be moved to 1st floor, provided FA is not increased and use from 2nd floor up will be conforming/complying use.

**DOB Objection #5 (per 52-61 Z.R.):** A new use group 6 is only permitted in an R6 zone if the previous use was a use group 6 or higher that was not discontinued for more than two years. Indicate in zoning calculations that the factory was not discontinued for more than two years.

**Argument:** The examiner cited ZR 52-61 too narrowly. Per ZR sections 52-332 (Other buildings or structures in residence districts), a non-conforming use listed in use group 11A, 16, 17 or 18 which is not subject to provisions of Sections 52-32 or 52-331, may be changed either to a conforming use or to any use listed in Use Group 6, 7B, 7C, 7D, 8, 9, 10, 11B or 14. Any subsequent change of use shall conform to Section 52-34 (Commercial Uses in Residence Districts).

The existing use was a factory per alteration number 631/50 with a current factory tenant on the 5th floor. The applicant also complies with ZR 52-34 requirements which state that a non-conforming use listed in use group 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11B, 12, 13, 14 or 15 may be changed only to a conforming use or to a use listed in Use Group 6.

The applicant also requested moving the UG 6 designation from the 5th floor to the 1st floor, allowing floors 2-5 to convert to conforming residential uses, providing more housing opportunities and benefitting the community.

**Key Citations:** ZR 52-61, ZR 52-332, ZR 52-32, ZR 52-331, ZR 52-34, Alteration 631/50.

## Recon #2 — Multiple Code Objections and MDL Article 7B (Objections #3, 4, 6, 9, 10, 12, 13, 15, 16)

**Recon Date:** March 20, 2006 | **Reviewer:** Kalyan K. Bhattacharyya | **Outcome:** Accepted on structure. Re-reviewed in application with TPPN 57. Show density, coverage. Must be for layout. Article 7B of MDL accepted for residential purposes.

**DOB Objections:** #3 — Must review under new code (not old code) since change in use from factory to residential. #4 — Change Schedule A to new code nomenclature. #6 — Show proposed building per new building code on T-001. #9 — Provide window for dining room (habitable room). #10 — Provide at least two exit door openings per apartment. #12 — Remove reference to Article 7B of MDL; this is not JLWQA. #13 — Provide 30' minimum rear yard. #15 — Every room including kitchens and bathrooms shall have window opening upon street or lawful yard/court. #16 — No room in non-fireproof multiple dwelling shall extend more than 30' in depth without window on lawful court.

**Argument:** The proposed conversion is filed under the general provisions of Article 7B of the Multiple Dwelling Law. The general provisions of Article 7B are not solely for the occupation of Joint Living Quarters for Artists (JLWQA) but also for general residential purposes. The objections cited were issued without regard to the method in which Article 7B of the MDL is being utilized.

Furthermore, the existing bulk conditions of this building do not allow compliance with district bulk regulations. However, under ZR 52-30 (Change of Non-Conforming Use) and ZR 52-31 (General Provisions), the applicable district bulk regulations shall not apply when a change from a non-conforming use to a conforming use is made unless such change includes any enlargement.

**Key Citations:** MDL Article 7B (general provisions, not limited to JLWQA), ZR 52-30, ZR 52-31, TPPN 57, PCF.PW-1 form.

## Recon #3 — Layout Objection, JLWQA Appearance (Objection #2)

**Recon Date:** March 3, 2006 | **Reviewer:** Kalyan K. Bhattacharyya (March 15, 2006) | **Outcome:** ACCEPTED. Reviewer noted: "The layout represents a one room apartment with kitchenette, 3 fixture bathroom, etc."

**DOB Objection #2:** THE LAYOUT STILL APPEARS TO BE THAT OF A JOINT LIVING-WORK QUARTERS FOR ARTISTS. REVISE LAYOUT SO THAT IT CANNOT POSSIBLY BE USED FOR SAME.

**Argument:** These are not JLWQA. The layout is an open plan layout since these apartments will be sold as condos. There is no set code requirement to that of a large studio apartment.

## Practical Impact

This is one of the most instructive reconsideration packages in GLE's files because it establishes three critical precedents:

**MDL Article 7B is not limited to JLWQA.** The examiner incorrectly assumed Article 7B only applied to Joint Living-Work Quarters for Artists. The recon established that Article 7B's general provisions apply to any residential conversion. This is a common DOB examiner error worth knowing.

**Bulk regulations don't apply for non-conforming to conforming use changes.** Under ZR 52-30 and 52-31, when converting from non-conforming (factory) to conforming (residential) use WITHOUT enlargement, district bulk regulations (rear yard, lot coverage, depth limits) do not apply. This is essential for older factory buildings that physically cannot meet current R6 bulk requirements.

**ZR 52-332 provides a broader pathway than 52-61.** When an examiner cites 52-61 to block a use group change in a residential district, check 52-332 for the exception path. It allows non-conforming UG 11A/16/17/18 uses to change to UG 6 or conforming without the two-year discontinuance requirement.

**Creative floor relocation strategy:** Moving the commercial use from the 5th floor to the 1st floor maximized the residential conversion potential. The DOB accepted this provided no increase in floor area.

When scoping factory conversion projects, flag these zoning arguments early — they determine whether the project is even feasible before you get into building code compliance details.
