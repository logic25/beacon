# DOB Acceptance: Voluntary Fire Alarm Certification for Temporary Certificate of Occupancy — 620 West 30th Street

**Project:** Verizon — Empire City Subway Relocation | **Location:** 620 West 30th Street, Manhattan | **WFC Job:** 07-7079 | **ARBPE Job:** 2664 | **BIS Job:** 104750976 | **Occupancy Group:** E, B-2 | **Engineer:** James B. (License #62089), ARBPE PLLC | **Date:** November 13, 2007 | **DOB Review Date:** November 28, 2007 | **Outcome:** ACCEPTED — PE certification plus manufacturer letter accepted in lieu of formal FDNY TCO letter.

---

## The Situation

The project needed a Temporary Certificate of Occupancy but the formal FDNY acceptance letter for the voluntary internal fire alarm system had not yet been issued. The engineer of record submitted a PE certification package as a substitute.

## The PE Certification

The FDNY-approved fire alarm system installed within occupancy group E, B-2 at 620 West 30th Street underwent inspection, testing and acceptance by three parties: (1) the licensed electrical contractor (James F. Volpe Electrical Contracting Corp), (2) the engineer of record (ARBPE PLLC), and (3) the system manufacturer's sales representative (Dynamic Fire Inc). The system was left in proper working order and on line.

Supporting documentation included a letter from Dynamic Fire Inc. (November 12, 2007) verifying that the fire alarm system at Empire City Subway at 620 West 30th Street is fully operational and accepted as per design.

## DOB Response

Handwritten notes: "OK — in lieu of FDNY TCO letter of acceptance." The DOB accepted the PE certification and manufacturer letter as a substitute for the formal FDNY sign-off, allowing the TCO to proceed.

---

## Practical Impact

**PE certification can substitute for FDNY letter on TCO.** When FDNY formal acceptance is delayed but the project needs to obtain a TCO, a PE certification package can be accepted in lieu of the FDNY letter. This is significant because FDNY acceptance timelines are unpredictable and can delay occupancy.

**The certification package must include three parties:** (1) Licensed electrical contractor confirmation (installer), (2) engineer of record PE certification, and (3) system manufacturer or manufacturer's representative acceptance letter. All three must confirm the system is operational and per design.

**Occupancy group matters.** This precedent applies to occupancy group E, B-2 (commercial/office). Residential occupancy groups may have different requirements — confirm before assuming this approach transfers.

**For project management,** when a TCO timeline is tight and FDNY acceptance is pending, prepare the PE certification package proactively. Have the electrical contractor, PE, and manufacturer representative ready to issue their letters simultaneously once testing is complete. Don't wait for FDNY — submit the PE package to DOB as an alternative path.

**Pricing note:** Coordinating TCO documentation across DOB, FDNY, contractor, PE, and manufacturer is exactly the kind of multi-agency work that justifies Managed Service or Full Concierge pricing tiers. This is not a simple filing.
