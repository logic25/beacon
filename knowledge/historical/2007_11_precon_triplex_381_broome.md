# Pre-Consideration: Triplex Apartment Combination Under TPPN 3/97 — 381 Broome Street, Manhattan

**Location:** 381 Broome Street, Manhattan | **Block:** 471 | **Lot:** 7501 | **BIN:** 1085557 | **CB:** 102 | **Applicant:** Beyhan Karahan, R.A. (License #017027), Beyhan Karahan & Associates Architects | **Filing Type:** Pre-Consideration | **Date:** November 27, 2007 | **Reviewer:** Chris Jnofli, Borough Commissioner | **Review Date:** November 28, 2007 | **Outcome:** APPROVED — "OK to accept combination under TPPN 3/97. Duplex unit of lease."

**Attachments:** Sketches, Certificate of Occupancy.

---

## The Issue

Client wanted to combine an existing duplex apartment (6th & 7th floors) with the apartment below (5th floor) to create a triplex. TPPN 3/97 states that not more than two stories may be connected in an apartment combination.

## The Argument

Respectfully request acceptance to combine two apartments under TPPN 3/97. While the TPPN states that not more than two stories may be connected, the space on the 6th floor is a small fraction of the overall floor. The combination is effectively the 5th floor apartment joining the existing duplex, where the 6th floor component is de minimis.

## DOB Response

Approved next day (submitted 11/27, approved 11/28). Reviewer accepted the de minimis argument for the 6th floor component.

---

## Practical Impact

**TPPN 3/97 two-story limit is not absolute.** The DOB has discretion to approve combinations that technically span three floors when the third floor component is de minimis (small fraction of total floor area). This precedent is valuable for luxury apartment combination projects in Manhattan.

**Pre-consideration is the right strategy** when pushing TPPN boundaries. Getting DOB buy-in before filing avoids objections during plan examination that could delay the project by months. Bring sketches and the existing CO — the reviewer needs to see actual floor areas to assess the de minimis argument.

**Framing matters.** Rather than requesting a "three-story combination" (which triggers automatic denial), frame it as combining a duplex with an adjacent apartment where the third floor is incidental. The language "small fraction of the overall floor" was the winning phrase.

**Quick turnaround is possible.** This was approved in one day, suggesting pre-considerations for apartment combinations can resolve rapidly when documentation is clean and the argument is straightforward.

**Related considerations for apartment combinations:** Fire separation requirements between combined units, egress requirements for multi-floor apartments, building code requirements for internal stairs connecting floors, and whether the building's CO allows the combined configuration.
