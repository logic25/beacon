# 1 RCNY Chapter 15: Maintenance, Repair and Replacement of Fire Escapes

## §15-01: Scope and Applicability

### General Scope
These rules establish mandatory standards for maintenance, repair, examination, and replacement of fire escapes in all buildings within the City of New York that contain one or more fire escapes.

### Buildings Subject to Rules
- All building types with existing fire escape installations
- Applies regardless of original construction date or building code vintage
- Applies whether fire escape serves as required or supplemental exit
- Applies to both public and private buildings
- Applies to both occupied and vacancy buildings

### Mandatory Compliance
- Building owners responsible for ensuring compliance
- Property managers designated as responsible parties
- Tenants required to permit access for inspections and maintenance
- No waivers or exemptions permitted except as specified in MDL §53

## §15-02: Definitions

### Fire Escape
A structure attached to a building consisting of stairs, platforms, railings, and supporting framework serving as a means of egress or emergency exit.

### Owner
The person or entity holding title to the property, or if property is in lease, the leaseholder with maintenance responsibilities.

### Responsible Person
The owner or owner's agent designated responsible for compliance with these rules.

### Qualified Inspector
A professional engineer licensed in New York State with experience in structural evaluation or a DOB-approved fire escape examiner.

### Maintenance
Regular upkeep and minor repairs required to maintain fire escape in safe and operational condition.

### Repair
Work required to restore fire escape components to functional and safe condition, not constituting full replacement.

### Replacement
Complete reconstruction or installation of new fire escape to modern standards.

### Load Test
Structural test applying specified weight loads to determine bearing capacity and structural integrity.

### Certification
Professional documentation prepared by qualified inspector attesting to fire escape condition and safety.

## §15-03: General Requirements for Fire Escapes

### Structural Integrity
- All fire escape components must be maintained in safe and secure condition
- No deterioration, corrosion, or damage that compromises structural strength
- Connections and fasteners must be secure and properly torqued
- Frame alignment must be plumb and level within acceptable tolerances

### Material Conditions
- Metal components must not show more than surface rust
- Deep corrosion, pitting, or material loss must be repaired or replaced
- Welds must be continuous with no cracks or separation
- Fasteners must not be loose, missing, or deteriorated

### Functional Requirements
- All stairs must be passable without obstruction
- Handrails must be firmly attached and capable of supporting body weight
- Guard railings must be present and in good condition
- Platforms must be level and capable of supporting specified loads
- Doors and windows providing access must open freely

### Load Capacity
- Minimum load capacity: 100 pounds per square foot distributed load
- Stair treads: 40 pounds per tread when concentrated load applied at edge
- Platform railings: 200 pounds applied horizontally at rail height
- Handrails: 200 pounds applied horizontally at rail height
- Structural testing required to verify capacity

### Fire Escape Dimensions

**Stairs:**
- Minimum width: 16 inches clear width
- Minimum tread: 8 inches
- Maximum rise: 12 inches
- Landings: Maximum 6 inches between landings
- Inclination: Not steeper than 50 degrees

**Platforms:**
- Minimum size: 5 feet by 5 feet
- Landing platforms required at each story
- Offset platforms require connecting stairs or ramps
- Slope: Maximum 1/4 inch per foot

**Railings and Guards:**
- Guard railings: Minimum 42 inches measured from stair nosing
- Handrails: Minimum 34 inches to maximum 38 inches height
- Handrail clearance: Minimum 1.5 inches from wall
- Guard spacing: Balusters not more than 4 inches on center
- Handrail diameter: 1.25 inches to 2.0 inches for standard graspability

### Access and Egress
- Doors providing access must be easily opened
- Window sills: Maximum 42 inches from floor
- Ladder access from lowest platform to ground required
- Clear path from building to fire escape opening
- No permanent obstruction of egress route

## §15-04: Periodic Examination Requirements

### Examination Schedule
- **Annual Examination**: Required minimum once per calendar year
- **Biennial Certification**: Professional certification required every two years
- **Expedited Examination**: Required within 30 days of visible damage or incident

### Examination Scope
Complete visual and physical inspection of all components including:

**Structural Elements:**
- All connections and welds
- Fasteners and bolts
- Main beams and support members
- Attachment points to building

**Stairs and Platforms:**
- Tread and riser conditions
- Platform structure and attachment
- Level and alignment verification
- Load-bearing capacity indicators

**Safety Components:**
- Handrails for firmness and attachment
- Guard railings for completeness and condition
- Access doors and windows
- Ladder conditions and attachment

**Environmental Damage:**
- Corrosion assessment
- Rust and deterioration evaluation
- Paint condition and protective coating integrity
- Cracks, separation, or material loss

### Load Testing Requirements
- Load tests required every two years for fire escapes used as required exit
- Load tests required every four years for supplemental fire escapes
- Static load test: Application of distributed 100 pounds per square foot
- Concentrated load test on stairs: 300 pounds per tread edge
- Railing test: 200 pounds horizontal force applied at handrail height
- Structural deflection must not exceed L/240 (where L is span length)

### Qualified Examiner Requirements
- Professional engineer licensed in New York State, or
- Architect licensed in New York State with structural experience, or
- DOB-approved fire escape examiner with certification
- Examiner must have liability insurance minimum $1,000,000
- Conflict of interest disclosure required

## §15-05: Examination Reports

### Required Documentation
Each examination must produce comprehensive written report including:

**Property Information:**
- Building address and block/lot number
- Property owner name and contact information
- Responsible person designated
- Fire escape identification (location, stories served)

**Examination Details:**
- Examination date and examiner credentials
- Weather conditions at time of inspection
- Overall condition rating and findings summary
- Detailed component-by-component assessment
- Photographs of significant conditions
- Load test results with data

**Condition Rating System:**
- **Acceptable**: Performs required function, minor maintenance only
- **Acceptable with Repairs Required**: Functional but repairs needed within specified timeline
- **Not Acceptable**: Unsafe for intended use, immediate repair/replacement required
- **Critical**: Imminent danger to occupants, use prohibited until corrected

### Reporting Timeline
- Written report must be submitted within 15 days of examination
- Report must be submitted to DOB and property owner simultaneously
- Copies maintained by building owner for minimum 10 years
- Electronic filing through DOB online portal required

### Examination Record Maintenance
- Building owner must maintain copies of all reports
- Reports must be made available to DOB upon inspection
- Reports must be provided to tenants upon request
- Examination records must be posted in building common area

## §15-06: Repair Requirements

### Repair Obligation
- Owner responsible for all repairs identified in examination
- Repairs must be completed within specified timeframes
- Non-emergency repairs: Within 90 days of examination
- Emergency repairs: Immediate or within 7 days as specified

### Repair Standards
All repairs must:
- Restore component to safe and functional condition
- Meet or exceed original material and design specifications
- Be performed by qualified tradesperson or engineer
- Receive inspection and certification upon completion
- Comply with applicable building code standards

### Repair Categories and Timelines

**Immediate Repairs (7 days):**
- Loose or missing treads presenting trip hazard
- Broken or separated railings creating fall hazard
- Hanging or separated members threatening failure
- Blocked egress or access preventing use
- Structural deterioration threatening integrity

**Priority Repairs (30 days):**
- Handrail loose or cracked but still functional
- Guard railing balusters loose but structure intact
- Moderate corrosion requiring surface treatment
- Hardware deterioration affecting operation
- Platform settling creating minor misalignment

**Standard Repairs (90 days):**
- Surface rust and minor corrosion
- Paint deterioration without structural impact
- Fastener looseness not affecting safety
- Minor platform slope beyond tolerance
- Cosmetic damage to non-load-bearing components

### Repair Certification
- Professional engineer or qualified inspector must certify repairs
- Certification confirms repairs meet code standards
- Certification submitted to DOB with examination report
- Photo documentation of repair work required
- Warranty minimum one year for repair work

## §15-07: Replacement Requirements

### Replacement Triggering Events
Fire escape replacement required when:

- **Structural Failure Risk**: Load test results show capacity below minimum standards
- **Extensive Deterioration**: More than 30% of area affected by deep corrosion or material loss
- **Repair Infeasibility**: Required repairs exceed 60% of replacement cost
- **Code Non-Compliance**: Cannot be economically repaired to current code standards
- **Safety Risk**: Multiple critical defects cannot be resolved by repair

### Replacement Standards
All replacement fire escapes must be designed and constructed to:
- Current NYC Building Code requirements
- New York State Building Construction Code standards
- Professional engineering design standards
- Load capacity minimum 100 pounds per square foot
- All dimensional and safety requirements per these rules

### Replacement Scope Options

**In-Kind Replacement**:
- Identical configuration to original fire escape
- Modern materials and construction methods
- Meets current code requirements for load and dimensions
- Recommended when building configuration unchanged

**Modified Replacement**:
- Different configuration to improve function or access
- Must maintain same number of exit stories served
- Cannot reduce accessibility compared to original
- Requires owner and tenant coordination

**Modernized Replacement**:
- Upgraded design for improved occupant safety
- Enhanced visibility and ease of use
- May include modifications for improved egress paths
- Requires coordination with other building modifications

### Replacement Timeline
- Design and engineering: 60 days
- DOB permit application: 15 days
- Construction: 45-90 days depending on scope
- Inspection and certification: 10 days
- Total: Minimum 130 days from replacement notice

### Replacement Financing
- Owner responsible for all replacement costs
- Limited subsidy programs available for low-income buildings
- Payment plans available through DOB for qualifying properties
- Tax abatement programs may apply for affordable housing

## §15-08: Emergency Repairs

### Emergency Repair Procedures
When fire escape poses imminent danger, following procedures required:

**Notification:**
- Owner must immediately notify DOB
- Occupants must be notified of restricted use
- Emergency repair notice posted at building entrance

**Initial Action:**
- Fire escape use prohibited immediately if imminent danger
- Temporary bracing or supports permitted only as emergency measure
- Temporary posting and warning signs required
- Alternative means of egress must be provided

**Repair Timeline:**
- Emergency repair work must commence within 3 days
- Work must proceed continuously until completion
- Completion required within 14 days of emergency notice
- Expedited DOB inspection available upon request

**Documentation:**
- Emergency repair report submitted within 3 days
- Professional engineer must certify emergency situation
- Documentation of temporary measures required
- Certification of repair completion required upon finish

## §15-09: Penalties

### Violation Penalties
Building owners in violation of fire escape maintenance requirements subject to:

**Civil Penalties:**
- Initial violation: $1,000 to $2,500 per fire escape
- Continuing violation: $500 per day per fire escape
- Failure to examine: $2,500 per examination period per fire escape
- Failure to repair: $1,000 per 30-day period per repair item

**Administrative Penalties:**
- Commissioner may issue violations and direct compliance
- Work orders issued for required repairs and replacements
- Building sealing authority exercised if imminent danger exists
- Registered complaints issued to tenants

**Criminal Penalties:**
- Felony charges for reckless endangerment if fire escape failure causes injury
- Misdemeanor charges for violation causing occupancy restrictions
- Criminal negligence charges for willful non-compliance

### Violation Resolution
- Owners must respond to violations within 5 days
- Cure period: 7 days for emergency conditions, 30 days standard
- Failure to cure results in increased penalties and enforcement action
- DOB may perform work and charge costs against property owner

## §15-10: Special Provisions for Article 7-B Buildings

### Scope of Article 7-B Provisions
These special provisions apply specifically to:
- Buildings undergoing conversion under MDL Article 7-B (Loft Board Law)
- Buildings converted to residential use from commercial/industrial use
- Buildings with fire escapes serving as means of egress under conversion plans
- Buildings permitted under 1968 Building Code fire escape provisions

### Fire Escape Acceptability in 7-B Buildings

**Secondary Means of Egress:**
Fire escapes may serve as secondary (second) required means of egress in Article 7-B buildings if:
- Building originally constructed under 1968 Building Code or earlier
- Fire escape was existing at time of conversion plan approval
- Primary egress via interior stairwell meets current code
- Fire escape meets all maintenance and load testing requirements
- Fire escape provides unobstructed egress to ground level

**Primary Egress Prohibited:**
Fire escapes may NOT serve as primary (only) means of egress in Article 7-B buildings:
- Interior stairwell required for primary exit
- Fire escape serves supplemental egress function only
- Exception: Historic preservation buildings with hardship waiver from DOB

**Conversion Plan Integration:**
- Fire escape acceptability determined at time of conversion plan filing
- Loft Board or DOB must approve fire escape in conversion plan
- Subsequent conversion to alternative egress requires plan modification
- Fire escape removal permitted if alternative egress installed

### Fire Escape Maintenance Standards for 7-B Buildings

**Heightened Inspection Requirements:**
- Annual examination by qualified inspector required (more frequent than standard)
- Biennial professional certification required
- Load testing required every 24 months (not 48 months)
- Building owner must maintain examination records in accessible location

**Performance Standards:**
- Load capacity testing at 110% of standard requirement (110 pounds per square foot)
- Structural safety factor of 3.0 minimum (vs. standard 2.5)
- Enhanced corrosion protection with specific coating systems
- Annual moisture and rust assessment required

**Documentation and Reporting:**
- Quarterly inspection reports (abbreviated) submitted to DOB
- Annual comprehensive examination submitted to DOB and Loft Board
- Photos of fire escape condition in each report
- Any deterioration noted in immediately preceding examination must be addressed

### Load Testing Requirements for 7-B Fire Escapes

**Test Frequency:**
- Initial load test required before conversion occupancy approval
- Biennial (24-month) load tests required thereafter
- Load test performed by qualified structural engineer
- Testing results documented and certified

**Test Specifications:**
- Distributed load test: 110 pounds per square foot on platforms
- Stair concentration test: 400 pounds per tread edge (vs. standard 300 pounds)
- Handrail test: 250 pounds horizontal force (vs. standard 200 pounds)
- Deflection limits: L/300 (more stringent than standard L/240)

**Test Certification:**
- Professional engineer must certify test results
- Certification submitted to DOB within 10 days of test
- Failed test triggers emergency repair notice
- Repair or replacement required within 7 days of test failure

### Integration with MDL §53 (Fire-Escapes)

**MDL §53 Framework:**
- MDL §53 establishes owner responsibility for fire escape safety
- §53 applies generally to all buildings with fire escapes citywide
- 1 RCNY Chapter 15 implements and expands MDL §53 requirements
- Article 7-B buildings subject to MDL §53 plus additional provisions

**Coordination of Standards:**
- Most stringent applicable standard governs
- Article 7-B buildings follow Chapter 15 §15-10 enhanced standards
- DOB enforces MDL §53 baseline and Chapter 15 specific requirements
- Loft Board has concurrent authority for 7-B buildings

**Enforcement Authority:**
- DOB enforces all fire escape requirements
- Loft Board coordinates on 7-B building compliance
- Joint inspections may be conducted for compliance verification
- Violation of MDL §53 can trigger both DOB and Loft Board enforcement

### Integration with MDL §278 (Applicable Provisions)

**Applicability Framework:**
MDL §278 establishes which building code provisions apply to converted buildings:

- Converted buildings must meet fire safety provisions of applicable code
- 1968 Building Code provisions apply to buildings converted after initial 1968 code adoption
- More recent code provisions may apply when substantially safer
- Fire escape provisions subject to §278 equivalency analysis

**Code Equivalency in Fire Escape Context:**
- Fire escape acceptability per 1968 code (C26-273.0) recognized as equivalent
- 1 RCNY Chapter 15 §15-10 represents modern equivalent safety level
- Load testing and maintenance requirements ensure ongoing safety
- Combination of historical provision plus modern maintenance deemed equivalent to new construction standards

**Alternative Compliance Routes:**
- Building owner may choose complete fire escape replacement to new standards
- Owner may choose installation of additional interior stairwell
- Owner may pursue variance from DOB for alternative means of egress
- Hard-to-comply buildings may request equivalency determination

### Requirements for Conversion Approval

**Conversion Plan Components:**
- Fire escape assessment by structural engineer required
- Load testing results demonstrating adequate capacity
- Maintenance plan outlining inspection and repair protocols
- Phased repair schedule if repairs needed to achieve compliance
- Professional engineer certification of egress adequacy

**Loft Board or DOB Review:**
- Fire escape acceptability determined during plan review
- Inspector site visit required to assess fire escape condition
- Examination of any previous repair or modification work
- Determination of whether repair or replacement required before occupancy

**Occupancy Approval Conditions:**
- Fire escape in acceptable condition or repairs scheduled
- Maintenance plan approved and adopted by building
- Initial load test completed before first certificate of occupancy
- Owner acknowledgment of ongoing maintenance obligations

## §15-11: Reporting and Recordkeeping

### Annual Reporting Requirements
- Building owners must file annual report of fire escape examination status with DOB
- Report due March 31 of each year for previous calendar year
- Report must list all fire escapes on property and examination status
- Defects and repair status must be documented in report

### Record Retention
- All examination reports maintained for minimum 10 years
- Load test results retained for minimum 10 years
- Repair records retained for minimum 10 years
- Owner transition: Records must be provided to subsequent owner

### Record Accessibility
- Records must be provided to DOB upon inspection
- Tenants have right to inspect building fire escape records
- Records may be requested under FOIL (Freedom of Information Law)
- Electronic records preferred but paper records acceptable

### Recordkeeping Standards
- Records organized by fire escape location and building address
- Chronological organization within each fire escape file
- Examination reports clearly dated and signed
- Photos included with examination documentation
- Load test results with engineer calculations included

### Database and Compliance Tracking
- DOB maintains centralized fire escape compliance database
- Property owners may access their building record online
- Compliance status tracked by last examination date
- Overdue examinations flagged for enforcement action
- Compliance reports generated quarterly by DOB
