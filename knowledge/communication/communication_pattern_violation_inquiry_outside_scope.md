# How GLE Handles: Violations from Past Client Filings Outside Our Scope

## Situation Overview

**Context:** A property management company (Clinton Management for Edge Community Apartments) contacts GLE about an ECB violation (#014201723K) related to DOB Job #320578086. The job was filed 1/24/2025. GLE's name appears in DOB records as having filed paperwork, but the actual work (FDNY fire alarm inspection and sign-off) was contracted to a third-party installer.

**Red Flags in the Inquiry:**
- Client says "paperwork filing processes is incomplete"
- GLE is listed as "respondent" in city records
- Violation issued for incomplete submission
- Client's violation consultant pulled GLE's info from DOB

**Actual Situation:** GLE was contracted by the building owner (Eleyyan Munzer of MA Development & Maintenance) to **amend an FDNY application** so the fire alarm installer could complete inspections. GLE's scope was limited to the amendment filing. The installer (Fireresponse Services) was responsible for the actual inspection and sign-off/LOA.

## How Sheri Handled It (Best Practice)

### 1. Immediate Response - Clarify Scope Boundaries

**What Sheri Did:**
> "Green Light was contracted by Eleyyan Munzer of MA Development & Maintenance to **amend the application** for the installer to complete the Fire Alarm inspections for Sign off/LOA."

**Why This Works:**
- Immediately establishes that GLE's scope was limited to **amendment filing only**
- Names the actual client (Eleyyan Munzer) to redirect accountability
- Does NOT take ownership of the violation or incomplete filing
- Polite but firm boundary-setting

### 2. Provide Documentation Trail

**What Sheri Did:**
- Attached FDNY portal approval: `#2024-TMFALM-005842-AMND`
- Referenced the inspection date: 11/14/2025
- Directed follow-up to the installer: Travis at Fireresponse Services (`services@fireresponse.net`)

**Why This Works:**
- Shows GLE completed its contracted scope (amendment was approved)
- Shifts responsibility to the party who failed to complete the inspection/sign-off
- Provides the property manager with actionable next steps without GLE becoming the middleman

### 3. CC the Actual Client

**What Sheri Did:**
CC'd Manny, Chris, and **Fouad Elayyan** (the actual client who hired GLE)

**Why This Works:**
- Ensures the actual client sees that GLE is being contacted about a violation outside our scope
- Creates transparency if the property manager escalates
- Protects GLE from he-said-she-said if the violation becomes a dispute

## Key Principles (Apply to All Similar Situations)

### When to Use This Pattern

Use this approach when:
- A non-client contacts GLE about a violation on a job GLE touched but didn't fully manage
- The violation stems from work **outside GLE's contracted scope**
- GLE's name appears in DOB/FDNY records but the failure is someone else's (installer, owner, GC, etc.)
- The inquiry implies GLE is responsible for resolving the issue

### Communication Framework

1. **Clarify scope immediately** - "GLE was contracted to [specific task]"
2. **Redirect responsibility** - Name the party who failed to complete their part
3. **Provide documentation** - Attach proof that GLE completed its scope
4. **Give them a path forward** - Direct them to the responsible party for resolution
5. **CC the actual client** - Transparency protects everyone

### What NOT to Do

❌ **Don't take ownership of someone else's failure:**
- "We'll look into the violation for you"
- "Let me follow up with DOB on this"
- "We can help resolve this"

❌ **Don't engage without looping in the client:**
- Responding to the property manager without CC'ing the owner creates liability exposure

❌ **Don't provide free consulting:**
- The property manager's violation consultant should be handling this, not GLE

## Practical Applications

### Scenario 1: Incomplete TCO/CO Process
**Inquiry:** "You filed for our TCO and we got a violation for missing inspections."

**Response Template:**
> "GLE was contracted to file the TCO application. Sign-off inspections were the responsibility of [contractor/owner]. Our scope was complete when the application was accepted by DOB on [date]. For inspection scheduling and sign-offs, please contact [responsible party]."

### Scenario 2: Post-Approval Construction Violations
**Inquiry:** "We got cited for work not matching approved plans. You filed the plans."

**Response Template:**
> "GLE prepared and filed construction documents that were approved by DOB on [date]. Field compliance with approved plans is the contractor's responsibility. The violation should be addressed by [GC name] or the site superintendent."

### Scenario 3: Elevator/Boiler Annual Inspections
**Inquiry:** "We got an ECB violation for a missed annual filing. You handle our DOB filings."

**Response Template:**
> "GLE was contracted for [specific project/filing]. Ongoing annual filings and periodic inspections are the building owner's or management company's responsibility unless separately contracted. We can provide a quote for managing annual compliance if you'd like that service going forward."

## Red Flags That Trigger This Pattern

Watch for these phrases in inquiries:
- "We received a violation and your name is on file"
- "The paperwork you submitted is incomplete"
- "Can you fix this for us?"
- "Our consultant found your contact info in DOB records"

**These all suggest scope creep risk.** Respond with the framework above.

## When to Escalate to Manny/Chris

Escalate immediately if:
- The violation amount is significant (>$5K)
- The property manager threatens legal action
- The actual client disputes that the scope was limited
- The violation could impact GLE's DOB standing or professional licenses

Otherwise, handle with the standard response and CC leadership for awareness.

## Follow-Up Actions (After Initial Response)

1. **Log the inquiry** in the project file with a note: "Violation inquiry outside scope - redirected to [responsible party]"
2. **Update the contract template** if this was a gray area that needs clearer language
3. **Bill for time** if the inquiry requires significant research or document review (>15 minutes)

## Outcome of This Specific Case

- Property manager was redirected to Fireresponse Services (the installer)
- GLE's documentation showed the amendment was approved, proving scope completion
- No further follow-up required from GLE
- Client (Eleyyan Munzer) was aware of the inquiry via CC

**Result:** Clean boundary-setting, no scope creep, no unpaid consulting work.
