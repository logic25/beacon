# Article III, Chapter 2: Use Regulations in Commercial Districts

## 32-00 GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Commercial Districts Overview

Commercial districts in the New York City Zoning Resolution are designed to provide opportunities for retail, office, service, and other commercial activities while maintaining neighborhood compatibility through varying intensity and use restrictions. The City's commercial zoning framework includes eight primary commercial district types (C1 through C8), each with distinct characteristics, density limitations, and permitted use profiles.

Commercial districts are mapped based on multiple planning factors including existing commercial concentrations, proximity to residential areas, transit access, infrastructure capacity, and waterfront conditions. The use regulations for commercial districts establish a hierarchical framework of permitted uses, conditional uses requiring special permits, and prohibited uses to ensure appropriate development patterns and neighborhood character protection.

### Adult Establishment Provisions

Adult establishments, including adult entertainment venues, adult retail stores, and adult theaters, are subject to specific restrictions and regulations within commercial districts. These uses are generally prohibited within certain distances of sensitive uses including schools, parks, playgrounds, day care facilities, and residential facilities. Certain commercial districts may restrict or prohibit adult establishments entirely through specific use provisions, and such establishments must comply with all applicable City regulations regarding operation and signage.

## 32-10 USE ALLOWANCES: USE GROUPS IN COMMERCIAL DISTRICTS

The New York City Zoning Resolution establishes eighteen use groups (Use Groups I through XVIII) that classify land uses into categories based on operational characteristics and land use compatibility. Use Groups I through VIII are permitted in various commercial districts. Use Groups IX through XVIII are primarily restricted to commercial and manufacturing districts with specific restrictions and conditional use requirements.

### Use Group I: Agriculture and Open Spaces

**Use Group I** encompasses agricultural uses, plant nurseries, outdoor recreation areas, and open space uses. This group includes community gardens, parks, playgrounds, and similar outdoor uses that maintain the character and accessibility of public open spaces.

Permitted Status: Generally permitted in all commercial districts (C1 through C8).

Use Group I uses are compatible with commercial districts and serve important neighborhood functions. These uses are allowed by right in all commercial zoning districts without conditional use requirements or special permits.

### Use Group II: Residential Uses

**Use Group II** includes all dwelling units and residential occupancies, including single-family homes, apartments, hotels that provide residential accommodation, and other residential facilities.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

- **C1 Districts**: Permitted. Residential uses including apartments and mixed-use buildings with residential components are permitted in C1-1 through C1-9 districts. The equivalency structure identifies residential equivalent districts for each C1 variant: C1-1 through C1-5 (equivalent to R1-R5), C1-6 (equivalent to R6), C1-7 (equivalent to R7), C1-8 (equivalent to R8), and C1-9 (equivalent to R9).

- **C2 Districts**: Permitted. Residential uses follow the same equivalency structure as C1 districts, allowing apartments and residential facilities subject to residential district regulations.

- **C3 Districts**: Not permitted. C3 districts are waterfront recreation zones with limited residential accommodation.

- **C4 Districts**: Permitted. C4 districts (C4-1 through C4-7) allow residential uses, typically in mixed-use development patterns. Residential components in C4 districts remain subject to applicable residential district density and building regulations.

- **C5 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. Residential uses are prohibited in C5-3, C5-4, and C5-5 districts due to their restricted central commercial focus. Residential uses are permitted in C5-1 and C5-2 districts subject to special permit approval.

- **C6 Districts**: Permitted. General central commercial districts (C6-1 through C6-9) allow residential uses broadly, supporting mixed-use development patterns characteristic of central business districts.

- **C7 Districts**: Not permitted. C7 districts are designated for commercial amusement uses with limited residential accommodation.

- **C8 Districts**: Not permitted. C8 general service districts are intended for non-residential commercial and service uses without residential components.

### Use Group III: Community Facilities

**Use Group III** encompasses community-serving facilities including schools, hospitals, medical offices, libraries, cultural institutions, recreation centers, religious institutions, and similar public and quasi-public facilities that serve neighborhood populations.

Permitted Status: Generally permitted in all commercial districts (C1 through C8) subject to specific dimensional and operational regulations.

Use Group III uses are encouraged in commercial districts to support neighborhood vitality and provide essential services. These uses are typically allowed by right in most commercial districts, though certain large facilities may require special permits or review under City environmental and planning procedures.

### Use Group IV: Public Service Uses and Infrastructure

**Use Group IV** includes government offices, police and fire stations, utilities, telecommunications facilities, public parking facilities, transfer stations, and similar public service infrastructure.

Permitted Status: Generally conditional use (special permit required) in most commercial districts.

Use Group IV uses serve essential infrastructure functions but may generate significant traffic, noise, or operational impacts. In C1-C3 districts, many Use Group IV uses require special permits to ensure compatibility with local retail and residential functions. In C4-C8 districts, some Use Group IV uses may be permitted by right depending on the specific facility and district characteristics.

### Use Group V: Transient Hotels and Guest Accommodations

**Use Group V** includes hotels, motels, bed and breakfast facilities, hostels, dormitories, and similar transient guest accommodations with room occupancies of less than 30 days (or equivalent).

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

- **C1 Districts**: Not permitted. C1 local retail districts restrict transient hotels to maintain neighborhood residential character.

- **C2 Districts**: Not permitted. C2 local retail districts similarly prohibit transient hotels.

- **C3 Districts**: Conditionally permitted with special permit. C3 waterfront districts may allow limited hotel development supporting waterfront recreation.

- **C4 Districts**: Permitted. General commercial districts (C4-1 through C4-7) allow hotels and transient accommodations subject to applicable building and operational regulations.

- **C5 Districts**: Permitted. Restricted central commercial districts (C5-1 through C5-5) allow transient hotels as important central business district uses.

- **C6 Districts**: Permitted. General central commercial districts allow transient hotels broadly, supporting central business and tourism functions.

- **C7 Districts**: Permitted. Commercial amusement districts allow transient accommodations related to amusement activities.

- **C8 Districts**: Not permitted. General service districts exclude transient hotels.

### Use Group VI: Retail Establishments and Personal Services

**Use Group VI** encompasses retail stores selling merchandise at retail, eating and drinking establishments, services to the person (barbers, salons, dry cleaners), and similar commercial service uses.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

**Local Retail Districts (C1 and C2):**

- **C1-1 through C1-9 Districts**: Permitted by right. C1 districts are designated for neighborhood retail serving local residents with essential goods and services. Permissible uses include grocery stores, delis, barber shops, dry cleaners, pharmacies, and small-scale service establishments up to 10,000 square feet per establishment.

- **C2-1 through C2-5 Districts**: Permitted by right. C2 districts provide broader local retail and service opportunities than C1 districts, allowing similar uses with higher square footage thresholds and expanded service categories.

**Waterfront and Intermediate Districts (C3 and C4):**

- **C3 Districts**: Limited retail permitted. C3 waterfront recreation districts restrict traditional retail but allow food and beverage service related to waterfront recreation activities.

- **C4-1 through C4-7 Districts**: Permitted by right. General commercial districts allow retail establishments broadly including department stores (with limitations), eating and drinking establishments, service businesses, and general merchandise retailers with minimal restrictions.

**Central Commercial Districts (C5 and C6):**

- **C5-1 through C5-5 Districts**: Permitted by right. Restricted central commercial districts allow retail establishments including major department stores and eating/drinking establishments as primary uses supporting central business concentrations.

- **C6-1 through C6-9 Districts**: Permitted by right. General central commercial districts (the broadest commercial zoning category) allow all retail categories including large-scale department stores, major eating and drinking establishments, entertainment retail, and comprehensive service businesses. Retail uses in C6 districts are not subject to square footage limitations for individual establishments.

**Special Purpose District (C7):**

- **C7 Districts**: Limited retail permitted. C7 commercial amusement districts restrict retail to uses directly related to amusement activities and food service supporting amusement venues.

**Service Districts (C8):**

- **C8 Districts**: Permitted by right. General service districts allow retail establishments including eating and drinking establishments, service businesses, and retail operations supporting the district's service orientation. Certain retail categories may be conditional depending on specific district provisions.

### Use Group VII: Offices and Professional Services

**Use Group VII** includes office buildings and office space for professional, business, governmental, and administrative functions, including doctor's offices, law offices, business offices, architectural and engineering offices, and similar professional service operations.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

- **C1 Districts**: Permitted. Professional offices are allowed in C1 districts with standard commercial space requirements.

- **C2 Districts**: Permitted. C2 districts allow professional offices subject to use limitations.

- **C3 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. C3 waterfront districts restrict offices but allow limited professional services related to waterfront management and activities.

- **C4 Districts**: Permitted by right. General commercial districts allow office uses broadly as important commercial functions.

- **C5 Districts**: Permitted by right. Restricted central commercial districts are designed to support office development as a primary commercial use, with minimal restrictions on office building size or floor area.

- **C6 Districts**: Permitted by right. General central commercial districts allow office uses without restriction, supporting major office concentrations in central business districts.

- **C7 Districts**: Not permitted. Commercial amusement districts do not include office uses.

- **C8 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. General service districts restrict office uses but allow limited office operations supporting service businesses.

### Use Group VIII: Recreation and Entertainment

**Use Group VIII** includes theaters, concert halls, museums, art galleries, recreation facilities, bowling alleys, game arcades, sports facilities, and similar recreation and entertainment uses providing public assembly and entertainment functions.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

- **C1 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. Local retail districts restrict recreation and entertainment uses to smaller facilities and may require special permits for theaters or major entertainment venues.

- **C2 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. C2 districts allow recreation uses with similar restrictions as C1 districts.

- **C3 Districts**: Permitted. Waterfront recreation districts specifically encourage recreation and entertainment uses related to waterfront activities and public access.

- **C4 Districts**: Permitted. General commercial districts allow recreation and entertainment uses including theaters and sports facilities.

- **C5 Districts**: Permitted. Restricted central commercial districts allow recreation and entertainment uses supporting cultural amenities in central business districts.

- **C6 Districts**: Permitted. General central commercial districts allow recreation and entertainment uses broadly.

- **C7 Districts**: Permitted by right. Commercial amusement districts are specifically designated for recreation and entertainment uses including outdoor amusement areas, recreational facilities, and entertainment venues.

- **C8 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. General service districts restrict some entertainment uses but allow recreation facilities.

### Use Group IX: Large-Scale Retail Stores and Home Furnishings

**Use Group IX** encompasses large-scale retail establishments including department stores, general merchandise retailers, furniture and home furnishing stores, and similar high-volume retail operations occupying substantial floor area.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

- **C1-C3 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. Neighborhood and waterfront districts restrict large-scale retail operations to maintain local character and prevent regional retail concentrations.

- **C4 Districts**: Permitted. General commercial districts allow department stores and large-scale retail with possible special permit requirements for the largest establishments in certain C4 variants.

- **C5 Districts**: Permitted by right. Restricted central commercial districts allow department stores and major retail establishments as important commercial uses.

- **C6 Districts**: Permitted by right without restriction. General central commercial districts are designed to accommodate the largest department stores and comprehensive retail operations.

- **C7 Districts**: Not permitted. Commercial amusement districts do not include traditional retail department stores.

- **C8 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. General service districts restrict large-scale retail but allow specialized large-format retailers related to service operations.

### Use Group X: Automotive Uses and Related Services

**Use Group X** includes gasoline service stations, automobile repair shops, automobile car washes, tire shops, automobile parts sales, and similar auto-related commercial services.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

- **C1-C3 Districts**: Not permitted. Neighborhood and waterfront districts exclude automotive service uses to maintain retail character and minimize traffic and pollution impacts.

- **C4 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. General commercial districts restrict auto service uses through special permit review due to operational impacts including traffic, noise, and air quality concerns.

- **C5 Districts**: Not permitted. Central commercial districts exclude automotive uses to preserve street-level retail and office functions.

- **C6 Districts**: Conditionally permitted. General central commercial districts restrict auto service uses, though limited auto-related retail may be permitted.

- **C7 Districts**: Not permitted. Commercial amusement districts do not include automotive uses.

- **C8 Districts**: Permitted by right. General service districts are specifically designed to accommodate automotive repair, service stations, car washes, and related uses as primary service functions. C8 districts serve regional automotive service markets.

### Use Group XI: Custom Manufacturing and Craft Operations

**Use Group XI** includes small-scale custom manufacturing, craft workshops, artisan operations, and light manufacturing operations that produce goods by hand and machine, including custom furniture making, jewelry manufacturing, printing, and similar skilled manufacturing operations.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

- **C1-C5 Districts**: Not permitted. Commercial districts through C5 exclude manufacturing uses to maintain retail and office character.

- **C6 Districts**: Conditionally permitted with special permit. Limited custom manufacturing may be allowed in C6 districts in specific locations where compatible with commercial uses.

- **C7 Districts**: Not permitted. Commercial amusement districts exclude manufacturing.

- **C8 Districts**: Permitted by right. General service districts allow custom manufacturing and craft operations as important commercial service activities.

### Use Group XII: Light Manufacturing and Production

**Use Group XII** encompasses light manufacturing, assembly, processing, and production operations including food processing, beverage bottling, apparel manufacturing, electronics assembly, and similar operations with minimal external effects.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts:

- **C1-C5 Districts**: Not permitted. Commercial retail and central commercial districts do not include manufacturing uses.

- **C6 Districts**: Not permitted. General central commercial districts exclude manufacturing.

- **C7 Districts**: Not permitted. Commercial amusement districts do not include manufacturing.

- **C8 Districts**: Permitted by right. General service districts allow light manufacturing operations compatible with service business locations.

### Use Groups XIII through XVIII: Heavy Commercial and Manufacturing Uses

**Use Groups XIII through XVIII** include heavy manufacturing, industrial processing, automobile assembly, printing facilities, warehousing, scrap processing, storage facilities, and similar heavy commercial and manufacturing operations with substantial external impacts including noise, traffic, and air emissions.

Permitted Status in Commercial Districts: Generally not permitted in any commercial district (C1 through C8). Use Groups XIII through XVIII are primarily regulated in Manufacturing (M) and Special Manufacturing (MX) zoning districts. These use groups represent heavy industrial operations appropriate only for manufacturing-designated areas with adequate infrastructure and separation from residential, retail, and office uses.

## 32-20 SIGN REGULATIONS

### Commercial Sign Standards

Commercial districts allow signage subject to specific design, size, and operational regulations to maintain neighborhood character and public safety while supporting business visibility. Sign regulations vary by commercial district type and specific location.

**General Commercial Sign Allowances:**

Permitted signage in commercial districts includes business identification signs, window display signage, and temporary promotional signage subject to size limitations, setback requirements, and illumination standards. The specific allowances for sign area and number of signs vary by commercial district and lot width.

**Sign Permit Requirements:**

Signs in commercial districts typically require Department of Buildings permits. Certain sign categories including painted wall signs, projecting signs, and illuminated signs require specific approvals and may require modifications to building facades.

**Design Standards:**

Commercial signage must meet City of New York design standards including character legibility, color coordination with building facades, and structural safety. Neon signage and other specialized lighting is subject to specific regulations regarding placement and visibility impacts.

## COMMERCIAL DISTRICT CHARACTERISTICS REFERENCE

### C1-1 through C1-9: Local Retail Districts

C1 districts are neighborhood-serving commercial districts mapped in and adjacent to residential areas. C1 districts accommodate local retail and service uses including groceries, pharmacies, barber shops, dry cleaners, and personal services serving neighborhood residents within walking distance.

**Characteristics:**
- Mapped within or adjacent to residential neighborhoods
- Limited to smaller retail establishments (maximum 10,000 square feet per establishment)
- Residential equivalencies: C1-1 through C1-5 (equivalent to R1-R5), C1-6 (equivalent to R6), C1-7 (equivalent to R7), C1-8 (equivalent to R8), C1-9 (equivalent to R9)
- Permits residential uses and community facilities
- Excludes major retail, hotels, and manufacturing uses

### C2-1 through C2-5: Local Service Districts

C2 districts are local commercial districts with broader service and retail allowances than C1. C2 districts serve neighborhood populations with retail and service establishments with higher square footage thresholds than C1 districts.

**Characteristics:**
- Mapped in residential neighborhoods with established commercial corridors
- Permits broader service uses than C1 districts
- Follows same residential equivalency structure as C1 districts
- Permits residential uses and community facilities
- Excludes major retail, manufacturing, and automotive service uses

### C3: Waterfront Recreation Districts

C3 districts are designated for waterfront recreation, boating, and limited commercial uses supporting waterfront public access and recreational activities.

**Characteristics:**
- Mapped along waterfront areas
- Permits recreation, waterfront facilities, and food service
- Limits residential uses and excludes traditional retail uses
- Designed for public waterfront access and recreational opportunities
- Generally excludes office uses and automotive services

### C4-1 through C4-7: General Commercial Districts

C4 districts are general commercial districts accommodating retail, office, hotels, and entertainment uses in areas appropriate for higher-density commercial development than C1-C3 districts.

**Characteristics:**
- Mapped in areas supporting mixed commercial uses and development
- Permits department stores, large retail, offices, and hotels
- Supports residential mixed-use development
- Accommodates entertainment and recreation facilities
- Seven variants (C4-1 through C4-7) with specific restrictions based on location and character

### C5-1 through C5-5: Restricted Central Commercial Districts

C5 districts are central business district zones restricted to office and retail uses with residential uses prohibited in C5-3, C5-4, and C5-5 variants. C5 districts support high-density office and retail concentrations in midtown and downtown areas.

**Characteristics:**
- Mapped in central business district areas
- Emphasizes office and retail uses as primary functions
- C5-3, C5-4, C5-5: Residential uses prohibited
- C5-1, C5-2: Residential uses permitted
- Permits hotels, department stores, and entertainment uses
- Excludes automotive services and manufacturing uses
- Supports tall building development and continuous street-level retail

### C6-1 through C6-9: General Central Commercial Districts

C6 districts are the broadest commercial zoning category, permitting comprehensive retail, office, entertainment, and service uses. C6 districts support the most intensive commercial development patterns including the largest department stores, major hotels, and office buildings.

**Characteristics:**
- Mapped in major central business districts and commercial corridors
- Permits all retail and service uses without square footage restriction
- Supports residential mixed-use development throughout all variants
- Permits the broadest range of commercial uses of any district type
- Nine variants (C6-1 through C6-9) with specific modifications based on location
- Excludes manufacturing and automotive service uses except conditionally

### C7: Commercial Amusement Districts

C7 districts are designated for commercial amusement and recreation facilities. C7 districts typically encompass outdoor amusement areas, boardwalk recreation areas, and similar regional amusement destinations.

**Characteristics:**
- Mapped in amusement area locations (notably Coney Island in Brooklyn)
- Primarily for amusement parks, rides, entertainment venues, and recreation facilities
- Limited retail to uses supporting amusement activities
- Permits transient hotels supporting amusement visitors
- Excludes residential uses and offices
- Designed for regional recreation destinations and tourism

### C8: General Service Districts

C8 districts are designated for service and automotive uses, heavy retail, and light manufacturing activities. C8 districts serve citywide and regional service markets including automobile repair, specialty retail, and service operations.

**Characteristics:**
- Mapped for automotive and service commercial uses
- Permits automotive repair, car washes, gas stations, and tire shops
- Allows light manufacturing and custom craft operations
- Permits heavy retail and specialized service businesses
- Excludes residential uses, offices, and hotels
- Designed for service functions and regional commercial markets
- Often located along transportation corridors and commercial strips

## References and Additional Information

This chapter of the Zoning Resolution is subject to periodic amendments and updates by the New York City Department of City Planning. Users should consult the official Zoning Resolution website at zr.planning.nyc.gov for the most current regulatory language, adopted amendments, and detailed use definitions. Specific projects and proposed uses should be reviewed with NYC Department of Buildings and DCP to determine precise applicability and conditional use requirements.

For questions regarding use applicability in specific commercial districts or properties, contact the NYC Department of Buildings or Department of City Planning for official determinations.
