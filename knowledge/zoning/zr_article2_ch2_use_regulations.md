# Zoning Resolution Article II, Chapter 2 - Use Regulations

## 22-00 GENERAL PROVISIONS

In order to carry out the purposes and provisions of this Resolution, the uses within buildings or other structures as well as the open uses of zoning lots, or portions thereof, have been classified and combined into 10 separate Use Groups with similar characteristics.

For the purposes of establishing permitted uses in this Resolution, references to permitted uses in the Use Groups, or any sub-categories therein, shall include all accessory uses thereto.

Use Groups I, II, III, IV, V, VI, VII and VIII, are permitted in Residence Districts subject to the provisions of the following Sections:

- Sections 22-11 through 22-18, inclusive, which establish general use allowances in Use Groups I through VIII, including each use listed separately therein, by Residence District, and additional provisions for certain uses where applicable.
- Section 22-20 (SIGN REGULATIONS), inclusive, which sets forth regulations for permitted signs.

The use provisions of this Chapter may be modified or superseded by special rules for certain areas in Article I (General Provisions), Article VI (Special Regulations Applicable to Certain Areas), and through Special Purpose Districts.

## 22-10 USE ALLOWANCES

The provisions of Sections 22-11 through 22-18 establish use allowances by Use Group. A brief statement is inserted at the start of each Section to describe and clarify the basic characteristics of that Use Group.

For Use Groups I through IV, use allowances by zoning district are summarized in Use Group tables. For each use and zoning district, the tables contain up to two levels of notation:

The top level will always contain a symbol and denotes:
- Permitted as-of-right (●)
- Permitted as-of-right in some instances, but with broad use limitations (♦)
- Allowed by special permit (○) of the BSA or CPC
- Not permitted in the district (–)

The second level may denote:
- Size restrictions (S)
- Additional conditions (P)

PRC letters A through G refer to the classification of permitted non-residential uses to determine required accessory off-street parking spaces as set forth in Section 25-31.

### 22-112 Use Group I – uses subject to additional conditions

- Sand, gravel or clay pits in all Residence Districts: BSA special permit per §73-112

### 22-122 Use Group II – uses subject to additional conditions

In R1 through R5 Districts, on qualifying residential sites, any type of residence shall be permitted.

In R1 through R5 Districts, for zoning lots that are NOT qualifying residential sites:
- R1 and R2: limited to single-family detached
- R3A: limited to single- or two-family detached and zero lot line
- R3-1: limited to single- or two-family detached or semi-detached
- R3X, R4A, R5A: limited to single- or two-family detached
- R4-1: limited to single- or two-family detached, semi-detached, or zero lot line
- R4B: limited to single- or two-family in detached, semi-detached, attached, or zero lot line

No rooming units permitted on zoning lots where residences limited to single- or two-family.

### 22-132 Use Group III – uses subject to size limitations

- Philanthropic/non-profit institutions: central office employees ≤ 50, floor area for such purposes ≤ 25% of total (or 25,000 SF in R8-R12, whichever greater)
- Ambulatory diagnostic/treatment in R3-1, R3A, R3X, R4-1, R4A, R4B: max 1,500 SF. In lower density growth management areas: 1,500 SF including cellar (except where zoning lot contains hospital/long-term care). BSA special permit per §73-134 for up to 10,000 SF.

### 22-133 Use Group III – uses subject to additional conditions

- Long-term care in R1/R2: only as-of-right on qualifying residential sites
- Nursing homes in high-risk flood zone or APPENDIX K areas: development not permitted; enlargement >15,000 SF not permitted
- Non-profit hospital staff dwellings: must be on same or immediately contiguous zoning lot as hospital. CPC special permit per §74-132 to modify.
- Ambulatory diagnostic/treatment in R3-R12: limited to licensed medical/health/mental health facilities; no veterinary or ophthalmic dispensing
- Non-commercial clubs in R3-R12: excludes clubs primarily carrying on business; excludes outdoor swimming pool clubs or clubs with outdoor pools <500 ft from lot line. CPC special permit per §74-133 for swimming pool clubs.

## 22-14 Use Group IV – Public Service Facilities and Infrastructure

Use Group IV consists of uses providing public services and infrastructure.

### 22-141 Use Group IV – general use allowances

**A. Public Service Facilities:**
- Court houses: not permitted in any Residence District
- Fire or police stations: ○ (special permit) all districts
- Prisons: not permitted in any Residence District

**B. Infrastructure:**
- Railroad right-of-way: ● all districts
- Most utility/infrastructure uses: ○ (special permit) all districts
- Power plants, dumps, incinerators, airports, bus stations, etc.: not permitted (–)

**C. Renewable Energy and Green Infrastructure:**
- Energy infrastructure equipment: ●SP all districts (max 10,000 SF aggregate lot area; BSA special permit per §73-147 to modify)

### 22-142 - 22-144 Additional provisions for Use Group IV

Size limitations, conditions, and special permit provisions for public service and infrastructure uses. Fire/police stations: CPC special permit per §74-141. Electric substations >40,000 SF: CPC per §74-142.

## 22-15 Use Group V – Transient Accommodations

In Residence Districts, limited to:
- Overnight camps: BSA special permit per §73-151
- Transient hotels in R10H Districts: CPC special permit per §74-151

## 22-17 Use Group VII – Offices and Laboratories

In Residence Districts, limited to:
- Laboratories: CPC special permit per §74-171
- Ground floor offices in flood zone: BSA special permit per §73-171

## 22-18 Use Group VIII – Recreation, Entertainment and Assembly Spaces

In Residence Districts, limited to:
- Outdoor day camps: BSA special permit per §73-184
- Riding academies or stables: BSA special permit per §73-185

## 22-20 SIGN REGULATIONS

### 22-21 Definitions
Words in italics defined in §12-10 or this Section.

### 22-22 Permitted Non-illuminated Accessory Signs

**22-221 Nameplates or identification signs:**
- Residences: one nameplate per unit, max 1 SF
- Multiple dwellings/non-residential: one identification sign max 12 SF; community facilities get bulletin board max 16 SF
- Hospitals: any number of ID/directional signs, total ≤ 25 SF per street frontage or 15% of frontage (whichever less); plus one directory/bulletin board ≤ 50 SF

**22-222 "For sale" or "for rent" signs:** max 12 SF. On vacant land: ≥ 15 ft from street line, ≥ 6 ft from other lot lines.

**22-223 Signs for parking areas:** one sign max 2 SF per entrance/exit, max 7 ft above curb. Car sharing: additional 2 SF aggregate.

### 22-23 Signs on Lots Containing Certain Community Facilities
- Hospitals: illuminated non-flashing signs permitted, same area limits as non-illuminated
- Flags/banners/pennants on civic/philanthropic/educational/religious community facility lots: permitted without limitation

### 22-24 Additional Regulations
- **22-241 Projecting signs:** max 12 inches across street line
- **22-242 Height of signs:** max ground floor ceiling or 20 ft above curb, whichever less

### 22-25 Number of Signs
Except as otherwise provided: max one sign per use/building/dwelling unit, two for professional offices. Corner/through lots: one sign per street.

### 22-26 Advertising Signs on Waterways
No moving or stationary advertising signs on vessels plying waterways adjacent to Residence Districts within view of arterial highways.
