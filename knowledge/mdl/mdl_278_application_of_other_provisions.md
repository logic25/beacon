# MDL §278 - Application of Other Provisions

## Full Text

The provisions of this article apply to buildings with residential, mixed or joint living-work quarters or artists' occupancy as herein provided and to such buildings only.

## 1. Applicable Articles and Sections

In addition to the provisions of this article, the following enumerated articles and sections of this chapter shall, to the extent required therein, apply to such buildings:

### Applicable Articles

| Article | Subject |
|---------|---------|
| Article 1 | Introductory provisions: definitions |
| Article 2 | Miscellaneous application provisions (except subdivision two of section nine) |
| Article 8 | Requirements and remedies |
| Article 9 | Registry of names and service of papers |
| Article 10 | Prostitution |
| Article 11 | Laws repealed; saving clause; effect |

### Applicable Sections

## 2. Certificate of Occupancy Protection

Failure to comply with any provision of this chapter other than this article and the above enumerated articles and sections shall not be grounds for refusal of a certificate of occupancy or compliance.

## Key Implications

- Only the specifically listed MDL articles and sections apply to Article 7-B conversions
- This is a LIMITED applicability provision — not all MDL requirements apply
- §53 (Fire-escapes) IS applicable to Article 7-B buildings
- The CO protection clause means DOB cannot refuse a CO based on non-listed MDL provisions
- This section is critical for arguing against examiner objections that cite inapplicable MDL sections
