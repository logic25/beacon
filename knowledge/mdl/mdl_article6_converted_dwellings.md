# MDL Article 6 - Converted Dwellings

## Overview

Article 6 of the Multiple Dwelling Law governs **converted dwellings** — buildings originally constructed for non-residential purposes that have been converted to residential use. This article works in conjunction with Article 7-B (§§275-278) for loft and manufacturing conversions, which provides specialized provisions for larger-scale conversion projects.

Converted dwellings represent an important category of residential buildings in New York City, particularly as older commercial and industrial structures are repurposed for residential occupancy.

## Scope

Article 6 applies to buildings that were not originally designed or constructed as multiple dwellings but have been converted to contain residential units. The regulatory framework ensures that such conversions meet applicable safety and habitability standards.

## Relationship to Article 7-B

**Article 7-B (§§275-278)** provides **specialized provisions for loft conversions and manufacturing building conversions**, allowing for:
- Live-work arrangements
- Joint occupancy scenarios
- Special occupancy types (artists' occupancy)
- Modified compliance pathways

**Article 6** provides the **general framework** for all other converted dwelling scenarios not addressed by Article 7-B.

### Key Distinction

While Article 7-B has a specific list of applicable MDL provisions (per §278), Article 6 conversions must comply with the full range of applicable MDL requirements unless specifically excepted.
