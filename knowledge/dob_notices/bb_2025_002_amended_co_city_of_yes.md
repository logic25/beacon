# BB 2025-002: Amended CO Requirements Under City of Yes Zoning Changes

## Overview

This bulletin clarifies Certificate of Occupancy (CO) requirements for existing buildings affected by the June 6, 2024 Zoning Resolution amendment enacted as part of the "City of Yes" initiative. The amendment fundamentally reorganized NYC's 18 zoning use groups into a new Roman numeral system (I–X) and created new permitted uses and density allowances in certain districts. This bulletin provides expeditors with guidance on when existing buildings require amended CO filings and when occupancy can continue under legacy use classifications.

## Background: Zoning Use Group Reorganization

### Previous System (Pre-June 6, 2024): Numbered Use Groups (1–18)

The former Zoning Resolution organized permitted uses into 18 numbered use groups:
- **Group 1:** Residential
- **Group 2:** Office/Professional Services/Government
- **Group 3–6:** Retail/Commercial (various types)
- **Group 7–10:** Automotive, Wholesale, Manufacturing (declining intensity)
- **Group 11–18:** Industrial, Storage, Utility Services (highest impact)

Each use group had strict district restrictions, and cross-use was generally prohibited unless explicitly permitted.

### New System (Post-June 6, 2024): Roman Numeral Use Groups (I–X)

The City of Yes amendment consolidated and reorganized into 10 new use groups:
- **Group I:** Residential (includes new ADU, accessory dwelling unit; boarding house provisions)
- **Group II:** Office and Professional Services
- **Group III:** Retail and Food Service (expanded categories)
- **Group IV:** Automotive Services (reformed; parking; charging stations)
- **Group V:** Warehousing, Wholesale, Logistics [VERIFY: exact definition]
- **Group VI:** Manufacturing and Industrial Processing (lighter industrial)
- **Group VII:** Heavy Industrial and Production [VERIFY: definition]

## When Amended CO IS Required

### Scenario 1: New Use Not Permitted Under Old Zoning, Now Permitted Under City of Yes

**Fact Pattern:** Commercial building in old R6B district. June 6, 2024, district rezoned to R6X (new district under City of Yes). Building currently has vacant retail space with dormant Group 3 (retail) CO.

Owner wants to open a small restaurant (Group III.B, food service).

**Old Rule (Pre-June 6):**
- Restaurants in retail (Group 5/6) prohibited in R6B residential district
- Food service would require variance or special permit

**New Rule (Post-June 6):**
- Group III.B (small food service, cafes) now permitted in R6X as "small establishment"
- Permitted uses expanded

**Analysis:**
- Building's zoning changed (R6B → R6X)
- Restaurant use now permitted under City of Yes
- However, CO may need amendment because:
  - Food service was not permitted under old zoning
  - CO history shows "Retail/Commercial" but not "Food Service"

Under old zoning, this use was grandfathered in (allowed, but not conforming). Under City of Yes, light manufacturing (Group VI) is now a permitted/conforming use in the M1X district.

**Analysis:**
- Building's use goes from "grandfathered non-conforming" to "conforming" due to zoning change

## When Amended CO Is NOT Required

### Scenario 1: Use Change Within Same Use Group, Both Permitted in Zone

**Fact Pattern:** Retail space (Group III) currently occupied by apparel store (previously Group 3 — old code). Tenant leaves; new tenant opens a bookstore (still Group III.A).

**Analysis:**
- Both uses are Group III (general retail)
- Both permitted in zone (no zoning change)
- Use remains "Retail and Commercial"
- CO occupancy class likely unchanged

**Amended CO Required?** NO, generally not required if change is within same use group and zone permits both.

---

### Scenario 2: Use Group Reorganization with No Change to Permitted Status

**Fact Pattern:** Office building (Group 2, old code) → Group II (new code). Office use remains permitted and conforming in zone. No change to occupied area or use intensity.

**Analysis:**
- Pure use group renumbering
- Use remains permitted and conforming
- Use character unchanged (office is office)
- No change to building zoning or occupancy classification

## Practical Guidance for Expeditors: CO Filing Strategy Under City of Yes

### Intake Checklist for Existing Buildings/Tenants

When representing client with existing CO in City of Yes-affected area:

- [ ] Confirm building's zoning district (before and after June 6, 2024 if rezoned)
- [ ] Identify current use and old use group (1-18) and new use group (I-X)
- [ ] Confirm existing CO occupancy class (e.g., Group B — Office)
- [ ] Determine if client's use changed (new tenant, expansion, etc.)
- [ ] Determine if City of Yes created new permitted status (e.g., ADU, small food service)
- [ ] Consult DOB C-Memo or DCP Zoning Handbook for specific use classification
- [ ] Determine if amended CO is required (see decision tree above)

### Filing Decision Framework

**Step 1:** Is the client's use permitted in the current zoning?
- YES → Proceed to Step 2
- NO → Building is non-conforming; grandfathering may apply. Consult attorney. May require amended CO to reflect non-conforming status [VERIFY].

**Step 2:** Has the use group changed (e.g., old 3 → new III.A)?
- YES → Proceed to Step 3
- NO → Likely no amended CO required. Verify with DOB if uncertain.

**Step 3:** Has the occupancy class changed under the new use group?
- YES (e.g., occupancy class changed) → Amended CO required
- NO (e.g., same occupancy classification) → Amended CO likely not required [VERIFY DOB guidance]

**Step 4:** Is there a new use not previously permitted (e.g., ADU added)?
- YES → Amended CO required
- NO → Proceed to Step 5

**Step 5:** Has the permitted area or scope of use expanded?
- YES → Amended CO likely required (new spaces, higher density, etc.)
- NO → Amended CO likely not required [VERIFY DOB guidance]

### Common Scenarios and Recommended Actions

| **Scenario** | **Amended CO Required?** | **Action** |
|---|---|---|
| Office building, no zoning change, no use change | NO | Keep existing CO; notify tenant of zoning update if helpful |
| Retail space, use change within Group III | NO (likely) | File change of occupancy form, or no filing if no occupancy class change [VERIFY DOB form] |
| Building rezoned under City of Yes, use now conforming (was non-conforming) | YES (likely) | Amended CO to reflect conforming status |
| Existing building adding ADU under City of Yes | YES | Amended CO or new CO for ADU unit [VERIFY DOB procedure] |
| Small restaurant in new "small establishment" zone | [VERIFY] | File amended CO if restaurant was previously non-permitted [VERIFY DOB guidance] |
| Parking garage modified with ground-floor retail (City of Yes requirement) | YES (likely) | Amended CO to reflect mixed retail/parking occupancy |
| Manufacturing use, shifted use groups but same zone, same intensity | NO (likely) | File informational amended CO or none [VERIFY DOB position] |
| Building in non-City-of-Yes district, no changes | NO | No amended CO needed |

## Expediting Tips and Common Pitfalls

### Tip 1: Distinguish Use Group Reorganization from Actual Use Change

The City of Yes renumbered use groups. Don't assume every change to a CO occupancy reference requires amended filing. If the use is the same (office is office), often no amendment is needed.

### Tip 2: Leverage City of Yes for Previously Non-Permitted Uses

If your client's use is now permitted under City of Yes (e.g., small restaurant, ADU) where it was not before, file amended CO promptly to legalize use. This removes uncertainty and prevents violation.

### Tip 3: Coordinate with Zoning and Building Code Changes

City of Yes zoning changes do not automatically trigger NYC Building Code changes. However, some uses may shift occupancy classes under the 2022 BC. Confirm with DOB Plan Examiner during pre-filing.

### Tip 4: Be Conservative with Non-Conforming Uses

If a use becomes non-conforming under new zoning (e.g., heavy industrial in new light-industrial zone), file amended CO or get DOB ruling on grandfathering. Don't assume grandfathering is automatic.

### Tip 5: ADU is a New CO Issue

ADUs are a new permitted use in many R-districts under City of Yes. Filing amended CO for ADU should explicitly identify ADU as new separate unit, with separate egress, utilities, etc. [VERIFY: DOB CO form for ADU]

## FAQs for Expeditors and Building Owners

**Q: Our building has a CO for "Group 3 — Retail." Under City of Yes, it's now Group III.A. Do we need an amended CO?**
A: Probably not, if the use remains "retail" and the zoning permits it. However, if the occupancy classification changed (e.g., from Group B to Group M [VERIFY]), then yes, amended CO is required. Consult DOB to confirm whether occupancy class changed.

**Q: We want to open a small restaurant in a space that had a retail CO. City of Yes now permits restaurants in this zone. Do we need amended CO?**
A: Yes, likely. The new use (restaurant, Group III.B) is different from the old use (retail, Group III.A), and it may have a different occupancy classification (e.g., Group F for food service [VERIFY]). Amended CO is recommended to reflect new food service use and occupancy classification.

**Q: Our building is non-conforming under old zoning. City of Yes rezoned our district, and now our use is conforming. Do we need an amended CO?**
A: Yes, recommended. An amended CO can remove the non-conforming notation and reflect the new conforming status. This clarifies the building's legal standing and may help with future work or transactions.

**Q: Can we add an ADU to our residential building under City of Yes?**
A: Yes, if your zoning district allows it (most R-districts do). You'll need an amended or new CO for the ADU unit. Consult DOB on the specific CO filing procedure (merged or separate CO for ADU).

**Q: City of Yes zoning changes don't apply to our district. Our CO should remain unchanged, correct?**
A: Correct. If your building's zoning district was not rezoned by City of Yes, and your use and zoning remain unchanged, no amended CO is required.
