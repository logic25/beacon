# BB 2025-011 (Rev1): NB/Alt Structural Filing and Permit Requirements

## CRITICAL OVERVIEW FOR EXPEDITERS
**This BB clarifies when structural work requires separate ST (Structural) permits and how those relate to parent NB/ALT filings. Updated October 28, 2025 (Rev1) with clarifications on PW1/ZD1 coordination and permit renewal procedures.**

## PART A: STRUCTURAL WORK IDENTIFICATION

### What Constitutes "Structural Work"

#### Always Requires Structural Permits (ST)
1. **Changes to building's structural system:**
   - New structural members or loadbearing elements
   - Modification of existing columns, beams, bracing, bearing walls
   - Removal of existing structural members
   - Changes to foundation design or type
   - New lateral load systems (shear walls, moment frames, bracing)

2. **Foundation-related work:**
   - New or modified footings
   - Underpinning (adding to existing foundation)
   - Dewatering or groundwater control structural components
   - Soil improvement or ground stabilization (engineered)
   - Modification of pile systems

3. **Demolition with structural implications:**
   - Selective demolition affecting lateral stability
   - Temporary shoring/bracing (if part of structural design)
   - [VERIFY] Removal of walls affecting lateral bracing system

4. **Major alterations affecting structural capacity:**
   - Large floor openings reducing structural continuity
   - Changes to roof systems
   - Addition of upper floor loads not accommodated by existing structure
   - Changes to occupancy classification with higher loading requirements
   - [VERIFY] Window/storefront demolition if full height (may affect lateral system)

#### Does NOT Require Structural Permits (ST)
- Cladding/facade replacement (structural adequacy of facade)
- Mechanical/electrical/plumbing upgrades (no structural change)
- Interior fit-out, partition replacement (non-loadbearing)
- Roofing replacement on existing structure (no structural modification)
- Window replacement in existing openings (cladding system only)
- [VERIFY] Interior accessibility modifications (stairs, ramps) if not changing structural loads

## PART B: PERMIT TYPES FOR STRUCTURAL WORK

### Primary Structural Permits

#### ST - Structural Permit
- **Issued for:** Structural work as primary project scope
- **Requires:** Structural design by PE, calculations, PE certification
- **Relationship to NB/ALT:** May be issued with parent NB/ALT or separately
- **Timing:** Issued concurrently with parent permit or after parent permit
- **Validity:** Remains valid as long as parent NB/ALT permit valid
- **Renewal:** [VERIFY Rev1] Renewed separately if parent permit renewed

#### FO - Foundation Permit
- **Issued for:** Foundation-specific work
- **Requires:** Foundation design, geotechnical analysis, PE signature
- **Relationship to NB/ALT:** Issues separately or with parent permit
- **Timing:** Often issued before general construction begins
- **Validity:** May have different renewal cycle than structural work above
- **Uses:** Foundation underpinning, caisson work, new foundation on site with existing structure

#### EA - Equipment/Apparatus Permit
- **Issued for:** Structural support for equipment
- **Requires:** Structural design for equipment loads
- **Examples:** HVAC unit supports, rooftop installations, mechanical equipment foundations
- **Relationship to NB/ALT:** Often issued with general permit; may be separate
- **Typical scope:** Limited to equipment-specific structural work

## PART C: WHEN STRUCTURAL PERMITS ARE REQUIRED

### Structural Permits Required With NB (New Building)
- **Standard rule:** ST (Structural) permit automatically required for all NB applications
- **Scope:** All structural systems must be designed and sealed by PE
- **Filing:** Structural design included in NB application package
- **Permit issuance:** ST permit issued concurrently with NB permit approval
- **No option:** Cannot file NB without structural design/permit

### Structural Permits Required With Major ALT (Alteration)
- **Triggers for ST permit requirement:**
  - Any change to primary loadbearing system
  - Addition of new floor levels (vertical expansion)
  - Significant floor area increase
  - Change to occupancy classification with structural implications
  - Foundation modifications
  - Lateral system modifications (shear walls, bracing, moment connections)

- **Decision point:** Must be made during scope definition phase
- **Filing:** If ST required, structural design submitted with ALT application
- **Approval:** ST and ALT permits issued together or ST after ALT approval [VERIFY sequence]

### Structural Permits NOT Required With Minor ALT
- **Examples (typically no ST required):**
  - Interior renovation, non-loadbearing partitions, flooring
  - Facade/cladding replacement (mechanical connection only)
  - Mechanical/electrical upgrades
  - Accessibility modifications to non-structural elements
  - Interior paint, finishes, fixtures

- **Verification:** Architect/engineer must confirm no structural modification
- **Risk:** If structural work discovered later, requires filing amendment (PW1 or separate ST)

### ALT with Mixed Scope
- **Scenario:** Renovation with some structural component (e.g., new skylight opening floor)
- **Strategy:**
  - Can file as ALT (general scope) + separate ST permit (structural scope)
  - Or file as comprehensive ALT with ST included
  - [VERIFY] Cost/timeline implications vary by DOB processing
- **Expediency:** Separate permits often faster if structural simple/isolated

## PART D: FILING PROCEDURES & DOCUMENTATION

### Submitting Structural Work

#### With NB (New Building) Application
1. All structural design included in NB scope
2. Structural plans, calculations, PE certification in NB drawings
3. NB and ST permits issued together
4. Single parent permit covers all work

#### With ALT (Alteration) Application - ST Included
1. Structural scope defined in scope statement
2. Structural design submitted as part of ALT drawings
3. PE certification for structural design
4. ALT and ST permits issued together

#### With ALT (Alteration) Application - ST Separate
1. ALT filed for non-structural scope (renovation, MEP, etc.)
2. Separate ST application filed concurrently or after ALT approval
3. ST application references parent ALT permit number
4. ST permit issued after structural review

#### Standalone ST Permit
- [VERIFY] May be filed independently for structural-only work on existing building
- [VERIFY] Requires demonstration that no other permits needed
- Examples: Underpinning, foundation repair, temporary lateral bracing

### Required Documentation
- **Structural plans:** Showing all new/modified structural elements
- **Structural calculations:** Load analysis, member sizing, connection details
- **Details:** Specific connection details, reinforcement, sequencing
- **PE Certification:** Form C-2 (Structural Work) sealed and signed
- [VERIFY] Geotechnical report if foundation work involved
- [VERIFY] Soil/boring analysis for foundation design justification
- Building code compliance matrix addressing structural code sections
- [VERIFY] Sequence of construction (if sequencing affects stability)

## PART E: RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER FILINGS (Rev1 Clarifications)

### Coordination with PW1 (Public Works Permit)

#### When PW1 Also Required
- Structural work that impacts public right-of-way
- Foundation underpinning affecting sidewalk or street
- Temporary bracing/shoring in street or affecting pedestrian area
- Dewatering discharge to public system
- Excavation in street

#### Permit Timing & Renewal (Rev1)
- **Original issue:** Both PW1 and ST issued on same date or PW1 issued first
- **Renewal:** [Rev1 clarifies] PW1 renewal and ST renewal are independent
  - PW1 renewed on its own schedule (typically 6-month periods)
  - ST permit renewal follows parent NB/ALT renewal schedule
  - **Action required:** If PW1 expires before ST, applicant must renew PW1 separately
  - Street work must stop if PW1 expired, even if ST still valid

#### Separate Applications
- PW1 filed with NYC Department of Transportation (DOT), not DOB
- ST filed with DOB
- However, coordination necessary — PW1 scope must match ST scope

### Coordination with ZD1 (Zoning Certification) (Rev1)

#### When ZD1 Also Required
- Zoning compliance certification needed for work scope
- Zoning issues may exist separate from structural questions

#### How They Coordinate
- [Rev1 clarifies] ZD1 and ST are independent
- ZD1 filed to address zoning compliance (use, FAR, setbacks, etc.)
- ST filed to address structural compliance
- Both may be required on same project; different aspects

#### Timing
- May file ZD1 and ST concurrently with ALT/NB
- Or file separately after base permit
- No dependency between them

## PART F: PERMIT ISSUANCE & RENEWAL

### Initial ST Permit Issuance

#### Timeline for Issuance
- **With parent permit:** ST issued same date as NB/ALT approval
- **After parent permit:** [VERIFY Rev1] Typically 2-5 days after parent permit if structural design already approved
- **Separate ST filing:** [VERIFY] Standard structural review timeline

#### Permit Validity Period
- **Duration:** Permit remains valid as long as parent permit valid
- **Extensions:** If parent NB/ALT extended, ST extends automatically
- **Exceptions:** [VERIFY] Special circumstance extensions may be granted

#### Before Work Begins
- Contractor must have issued ST permit in hand
- Structural engineer must be on call during construction
- [VERIFY] Permit must be posted on site

### ST Permit Renewal (Rev1)

#### When Renewal Needed
- If parent ALT/NB permit extended (work continues beyond initial term)
- Initial ST valid for [VERIFY] 24-36 month period
- If initial period expires, renewal required to continue structural work

#### Renewal Process (Rev1 Updated)
- [Rev1 clarifies] ST permit renewal filed separately from parent permit renewal
- Application: Certification that structural work still within original design parameters
- [VERIFY] Updated inspection sign-offs from structural engineer
- [VERIFY] Structural observations report if construction has begun
- Renewal typically processed faster than original permit

#### Relationship to Parent Permit Extension
- Parent NB/ALT renewed: You may apply for ST renewal on same timeline
- Parent expires: ST automatically expires even if structural work ongoing
- **Action:** If parent expires, do not start new structural work until parent renewed

### Inspections During Construction

#### Structural Inspections Trigger Points
- [VERIFY Rev1] Structural inspections required at specific phases
- **Typical trigger points:**
  - Foundation preparation (before excavation/underpinning)
  - Foundation completion (after foundation work finished)
  - Structural framing (before connections closed in)
  - Connections completion (before covering/concealing)
  - Final inspection (before occupancy)

#### Inspection Responsibilities
- Structural engineer responsible for scheduling inspections
- Licensed PE inspector typically conducts inspections
- Contractor must notify engineer before critical phases
- [VERIFY] Inspection reports must be filed with DOB

#### Structural Observations Reports
- Required if construction phase significant
- Documents inspection dates, findings, engineer approval
- [VERIFY] Filed as part of progress filings or final certification

## PART G: SPECIAL SITUATIONS

### Temporary Structural Support (Shoring, Bracing)

#### When Temporary Structures Need Permits
- If temporary support is part of permanent structural design
- If temporary support is engineered system requiring design certification
- Most temporary shoring requires structural design and certification

#### Permit Type
- May be issued as ST (Structural) permit
- Or may be approved under parent permit with design specifications
- [VERIFY] Standalone shoring permit procedures

#### Design & Inspection
- Temporary support must be designed to support loads
- Temporary structures may have higher factor of safety than permanent
- Inspection required before and after use
- Removal certification required

### Underpinning Projects

#### Always Requires Permits
- Underpinning is foundation modification — ST required
- May also require FO (Foundation) permit depending on scope

#### Special Procedures
- Geotechnical analysis typically required
- Sequencing and temporary support critical
- Multiple inspections required during work
- Building monitoring may be required [VERIFY if in BB 2025-011]

### Remedial Structural Work (Repairs)

#### Defects Discovered During Construction
- If existing building has unforeseen structural defects
- Repair work may require amended structural permit
- [VERIFY] Amendment procedures and timeline

#### Changes to Original Structural Design
- If during construction, structural system modified from design
- Requires engineer approval and permit amendment
- [VERIFY] Change order vs. permit amendment procedures

### Facade Work / Curtain Wall Attachment

#### Structural vs. Non-Structural
- If facade support is independent structure: ST permit required
- If facade attached to existing structure only: typically no ST
- [VERIFY] Verify based on whether structural system modified

#### Connection Design
- All connections to existing structure must be reviewed
- If requiring new core work or modification: ST permit
- If only surface attachment: typically alteration only

## PART H: EXPEDITING STRATEGIES

### Getting Permits Faster: Structural

#### ST Included With NB/ALT Approval
- ST typically issued same day as parent permit if design ready
- No additional waiting period for ST
- **Best practice:** Ensure structural design complete and approved with main design

#### Separate ST Filing After ALT Approval
- File ALT first (general alteration, no structural modifications)
- Then file ST permit once structural scope finalized
- **Advantage:** Allows for parallelization of non-structural design
- **Disadvantage:** Longer overall project timeline due to sequential filings

#### Phased Approach
- Phase 1: File ALT for above-grade work (no structural)
- Phase 2: File ST for foundation/below-grade work (separate filing)
- [VERIFY] Allows concurrent design/approval where appropriate

### Cost Considerations

#### Permit Fees
- ST permit fees based on construction cost of structural work
- Lower cost structural work = lower permit fees
- Combined ALT+ST vs. separate filings: [VERIFY fee implications]

#### Expediting Fees
- Structural expediting adds complexity/cost
- Expediters with structural background may manage more efficiently
- [VERIFY] DOB structural review timeline (may be slower than general ALT review)

### Coordination with General Contractor

#### General Contractor Responsibilities
- Contractor must have ST permit before starting structural work
- Contractor responsible for coordinating inspections with structural engineer
- Contractor may not modify structural design without engineer approval
- Contractor must coordinate temporary support with engineer

#### Inspector/Engineer Coordination
- Structural engineer typically performs or arranges inspections
- Inspector must be scheduled with adequate notice
- Contractor may not proceed through critical phases without inspection clearance

## PART I: COMMON MISTAKES & PITFALLS

### Structural Work Identification

❌ Assuming facade replacement never requires structural permit (some do)
❌ Filing ALT without structural design when structural work is involved
❌ Missing foundation implications when modifying interior bearing walls
❌ Not recognizing lateral system changes (e.g., infilling shear walls)
❌ Underestimating scope — starting with "no structural" then discovering structural work

### Filing & Sequencing

❌ Filing ST separately without first securing parent ALT/NB approval
❌ Allowing ST permit to expire by not renewing parent permit
❌ Assuming ST expires when parent permit is extended (it doesn't)
❌ Not coordinating ST renewal with PW1 renewal [Rev1] if both required
❌ Not recognizing ZD1 may also be needed for some projects [Rev1]

### Construction & Inspections

❌ Starting structural work before ST permit issued
❌ Contractor proceeding without inspection clearance
❌ Not maintaining structural engineer on call during work
❌ Missing inspection deadline windows (phases may pass before inspection available)
❌ Not documenting temporary support removal

### Permits & Renewals

❌ Assuming ST doesn't expire if parent permit extended (must renew ST separately) [Rev1]
❌ Not understanding PW1/ST are independent permits with separate renewal cycles [Rev1]
❌ Failing to renew PW1 on its own schedule (street work must stop if PW1 expired)
❌ Not realizing ST automatically expires if parent permit expires

## CHECKLIST: STRUCTURAL SCOPE DECISION

Before filing NB or ALT, verify structural permit requirement:

- [ ] Are any existing structural members being modified or removed?
- [ ] Are any new structural elements being added?
- [ ] Is the foundation being modified or added to?
- [ ] Is the lateral load system (shear walls, bracing) being modified?
- [ ] Is occupancy classification changing to higher structural loads?
- [ ] Are floor openings being created that affect structural continuity?
- [ ] Is the building being vertically expanded (new stories)?
- If ANY "yes" → ST permit required
- If ALL "no" → ST permit typically not required

**If uncertain, consult with structural engineer or DOB.**
