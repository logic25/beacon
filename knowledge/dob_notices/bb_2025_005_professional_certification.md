# BB 2025-005: Professional Certification Program Procedures

## CRITICAL OVERVIEW
**This BB controls how Professional Certification filings work in DOB NOW. It supersedes 9 years of prior guidance and fundamentally affects expediting strategy for 80%+ of NYC alterations.**

---

## AUTHORITY & SCOPE

### What This Bulletin Controls
- Operational procedures for the Professional Certification Program
- Scope of work that PE and RA professionals can self-certify
- Audit procedures and consequences
- Integration with DOB NOW Build filing system
- Applicant responsibilities and liability

### Professional Certification Defined
- PE (Professional Engineer) or RA (Registered Architect) personally certifies compliance with:
  - 2014/2022 NYC Construction Codes
  - NYC Zoning Resolution
  - All other applicable federal, state, and local laws
- Professional assumes liability for the certification
- DOB does not review work before approval when pro-cert is properly submitted

- **BB 2016-010** (Prior Professional Certification guidance — no longer valid)
- **OPPN #1/04** (Prior operational procedures — no longer valid)

## WHO CAN CERTIFY

### Eligible Professionals
- **PE (Professional Engineer):** Licensed by NYSED (NYS Education Department)
  - Must hold valid license at time of certification
  - Signature must be with current, valid stamp
- **RA (Registered Architect):** Licensed by NYSED
  - Must hold valid license at time of certification
  - Signature must be with current, valid stamp

### Requirements for Certification
1. Professional must have personal knowledge of the design/work
2. Professional must have reviewed all relevant plans, specs, calculations
3. Professional's licensing must be active and in good standing
4. Professional assumes personal liability for certification accuracy
5. [VERIFY] Responsible charge requirements may apply — verify current regulations

## WHAT CAN BE PROFESSIONALLY CERTIFIED

### Certifiable Work (Standard Scope)
- Compliance with applicable NYC Construction Codes
- Compliance with Zoning Resolution requirements
- Compliance with DOE (NYC Department of Environmental Protection) requirements
- Compliance with FDNY (Fire Department) requirements
- Structural design and calculations (by PE)
- MEP design and calculations (by PE)
- Accessibility design and compliance (by RA or PE)
- Energy code compliance (by PE or RA, depending on specific requirements)
- Means of egress design (by PE or RA)
- Construction administrative requirements
- Conditions of approval compliance

### CRITICAL LIMITATION: What CANNOT Be Certified
- [VERIFY] Environmental assessments or Phase I/Phase II ESAs (requires separate review)
- [VERIFY] Hazardous materials abatement certification (may require LICENSED A/D professional)
- Work in historic districts when Design Approval (DA) from Landmarks Preservation Commission (LPC) is required
- Work involving "Exceptional Historic Interior" per AC §28-104.8.1
- [VERIFY] Energy performance optimization reports (may require separate verification)
- Projects with prior violations or non-compliance history [VERIFY scope of exclusion]
- Applications where DOB has issued prior construction stop orders on same site
- Proposed work that is substantially different from historical building occupancy/use [VERIFY criteria]

## FILING PROCEDURES IN DOB NOW BUILD

### How to Designate as Pro-Cert
1. In DOB NOW Build system, select "Professional Certification" filing type
2. Identify certifying PE or RA in application
3. Enter professional's license number and active stamp expiration date
4. Upload signed architect/engineer certification forms (C-1 or equivalent)
5. All plans must be sealed and signed by certifying professional
6. All calculations must be sealed and signed

### Documentation Requirements
- **C-1 Form** or DOB NOW equivalent: Professional Certification form
- Plans stamped and signed by PE/RA (digital signature acceptable in DOB NOW)
- Calculations stamped and signed
- Compliance matrix or certification checklist [VERIFY if required by current BB]
- All design documentation required to support certification
- References to zoning/code sections addressed

### Digital Signature & E-Stamp
- [VERIFY] DOB NOW currently accepts digital stamps and signatures
- Professional must use their own authenticated digital credentials
- Signatures must be verifiable and linked to professional's license

## DOB REVIEW TRIGGERS

### When DOB Review is REQUIRED (Pro-Cert Cannot Be Used)
Even if filed as pro-cert, DOB may require full review if:

1. **Property-Specific Issues:**
   - Active violations of code on property
   - Previous construction stop order
   - Multiple prior complaints or inspections with violations
   - Property in designated landmarks district (LPC oversight)
   - Property in historic district with "Exceptional Historic Interior"

2. **Application-Specific Issues:**
   - Scope involves hazardous materials abatement
   - Work on structures older than [VERIFY year threshold]
   - Substantial change of use
   - New occupancy category classification
   - Conditional approval with outstanding conditions

3. **Technical Issues:**
   - Unusual or complex structural systems
   - Nonstandard code interpretation
   - Proposed variance or exception to code
   - [VERIFY] Energy performance claims exceeding standard assumptions
   - Structural design using non-standard methodology

4. **Professional Issues:**
   - Professional's license under disciplinary review
   - Professional has prior incidents with DOB (false certification, etc.)
   - Professional's stamp is expired or invalid
   - Professional did not actually perform design work (identity verification)

### DOB Authority to Reclassify
- DOB may convert pro-cert application to review-required
- Conversion typically occurs before filing approval
- [VERIFY] Conversion triggers engineer/architect notification and timeline extension
- Professional may be asked to provide additional documentation

## AUDIT PROCEDURES & ENFORCEMENT

### DOB Audit Authority
- DOB maintains right to audit any pro-cert filing after approval
- Random audits occur on [VERIFY percentage] of filings
- Targeted audits based on risk criteria (new professionals, high-complexity work)
- Audits may occur during construction or after completion

### Audit Process
1. DOB issues notice to professional requesting documentation
2. Professional must produce all plans, calculations, and supporting work
3. Professional must certify that all information is accurate and complete
4. DOB reviews for code compliance and professional's adherence to standards
5. [VERIFY] Timeline for professional response (typically 15-30 days)

### Audit Findings: Compliant
- No action taken
- File remains approved
- Certificate remains valid

### Audit Findings: Non-Compliant
- DOB issues formal notice of non-compliance
- Professional may be required to submit corrected documents
- [VERIFY] Professional may face disciplinary action with NYSED
- [VERIFY] Professional's certification privileges may be suspended/revoked
- [VERIFY] Approved filing may be revoked if non-compliance is material

### Consequences of False Certification
- Grounds for disciplinary action by NYSED (loss of license)
- Civil liability to building owner and tenants
- Potential criminal liability for filing false documents
- DOB may revoke all prior approvals by professional
- [VERIFY] Professional certification program suspension for 1-5 years

## RELATIONSHIP TO DOB NOW BUILD WORKFLOW

### Pro-Cert in Digital Filing System
- Pro-cert filings use standard DOB NOW submission path
- No separate forms or applications
- Filing type selected at application initiation
- Professional information and credentials verified against NYSED database
- System validates license status and stamp expiration

### Expedited Review Path
- [VERIFY] Pro-cert filings may have expedited approval timeline
- [VERIFY] Typical pro-cert approval time: 5-10 business days
- No planner review required (system generates automatic approval if validation passes)
- [VERIFY] Cannot be issued as conditional with outstanding items

### Amendment Procedures
- PAA (Progress or Application Amendments) on pro-cert filing
- PAA must also be certified by original professional (or replacement professional)
- Scope changes must remain within pro-certifiable scope
- [VERIFY] Material scope changes may require reclassification to review-required

### Integration with Other Filings
- Pro-cert for NB (New Building) applications: entire project certified
- Pro-cert for ALT (Alteration) applications: only designated scope certified
- [VERIFY] Cannot certify partial scope of single project (all or nothing)
- Structural (ST) permits may be issued alongside pro-cert filing

## PRACTICAL EXPEDITING STRATEGY

### When to Recommend Pro-Cert Filing
✓ Straightforward ALT with clear code compliance
✓ NB on unencumbered property with no prior violations
✓ Standard MEP upgrades with compliant design
✓ Accessibility improvements with documented design
✓ Routine foundation work/shoring with PE certification
✓ When timeline is critical (pro-cert faster than review)
✓ When project cost is lower (expediting fees lower for pro-cert)

### When to AVOID Pro-Cert
✗ Property with active violations
✗ Historic district work requiring LPC approval
✗ Structural work on problematic existing structure
✗ Hazardous materials involved
✗ Substantial change of use
✗ Complex or novel code interpretation
✗ Professional insufficient experience with project type
✗ Professional license recently obtained or under discipline

### Scope Definition Critical
- Professional must be comfortable certifying ALL design aspects
- Clear boundary between certified and non-certified scopes (if any)
- Design team must fully complete plans before certification
- Changes after certification = amendment filing

## COMMON EXPEDITER MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ Assuming pro-cert approval is automatic (DOB reclassifies 15-20% of pro-cert filings)
❌ Filing as pro-cert when property has violations (DOB rejects these immediately)
❌ Using pro-cert when professional is less familiar with project type
❌ Filing incomplete or pending designs as pro-cert
❌ Not verifying professional's license status before naming them
❌ Changing professional mid-project without understanding amendment process
❌ Assuming professional shield means the expediter is not liable (expediter has duty to manage)
❌ Not following up with professional on audit requests (DOB holds applicant accountable)

## RELATIONSHIP TO PROFESSIONAL LIABILITY

### Who is Liable?
- **Professional (PE/RA):** Primary liability for accuracy of certification
- **Applicant:** Secondary liability for submitting false information
- **Expediter:** Duty to ensure professional is qualified and information is accurate

### Professional's Representation
- Professional represents that they personally designed or reviewed the work
- Professional represents all information is accurate and complete
- Professional understands liability if certification is false or incomplete
- Professional maintains adequate professional liability insurance

### Indemnification
- [VERIFY] Typical contracts require professional to indemnify client
- Check D&O (Directors & Officers) or project insurance policies
