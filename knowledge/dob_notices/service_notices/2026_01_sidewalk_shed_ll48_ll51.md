# Local Law 48 and Local Law 51 of 2025: Sidewalk Shed Filing and Permit Changes in DOB NOW

**Effective February 2, 2026** — Major changes to sidewalk shed permits: 90-day expiration, no auto-renewal, new PW1/PW2 questions.

## Sidewalk Shed Permit Expiration

- All permits **issued or renewed on or after February 2, 2026** expire every **90 days**
- **No auto-renewal** — must manually renew each time
- Renewal fee: **$130 per renewal**
- Permits issued **before** February 2, 2026 expire under the old rules

## New PW1 Scope of Work Question

For sidewalk sheds on Building Types **3-Family or Other**, new question:

**"Is the Sidewalk Shed installed in connection with New Building, Enlargement, or Demolition work for which a permit has been issued?"**

### If Yes (NB/Enlargement/Demolition connected):
- LL48 and LL51 requirements **do NOT apply**
- No progress-of-work questions on PW1 or PW2
- Standard sidewalk shed rules apply

### If No (facade work, repairs, etc.):
- LL48 and possibly LL51 requirements **DO apply**
- Applicant must answer new questions about the sidewalk shed on PW1
- A **Registered Design Professional (RDP)** is added to PW2 as a Stakeholder
- RDP must login to DOB NOW and answer progress-of-work questions:
  - At initial permit request submission
  - At each 90-day renewal

## Practical Impact

- Sidewalk sheds for facade/repair work now require active management every 90 days
- $130 renewal fee adds up: a 2-year shed costs $520/year in renewals alone
- RDP involvement at every renewal adds coordination overhead
- NB/enlargement/demolition sheds are exempt from the new requirements
- Factor the 90-day renewal cycle into project timelines and client communication
- Set calendar reminders — expired sheds mean work stoppages
