# Reporting Construction Cost in DOB NOW

**Effective February 2, 2026** — Applicants will have the option to report the estimated job cost of the entire project on the Initial filing, including work types that will later be filed as subsequent filings.

## New Option: Report Entire Cost on Initial Filing

A new question on the PW1 tab under Additional Information:

**"Are you reporting the entire cost of the project with this initial filing, including work types that will be filed later as subsequent filings?"**

### If Yes:
- Report the **entire project cost** in the Estimated Job Cost field
- Fees calculated based on the full amount entered
- Provide **Initial Cost Details on the PW3** for each work type on the Initial filing AND all subsequent filings to be filed
- No further cost reporting required on subsequent filings

### On Each Subsequent Filing (if entire cost was reported on I1):

New question: **"Are there additional costs with this subsequent filing that were not included in the Estimated Job Cost and Cost Affidavit/PW3 on the initial filing?"**

- If **Yes**: Estimated job cost and PW3 required, additional fees must be paid
- If **No**: DOB NOW verifies work types were reported on the I1 PW3, minimum fee of **$130** charged

### If Entire Cost NOT Reported on Initial Filing:
- Cost Affidavit (PW3) must be completed on each subsequent filing to account for remaining work types
- Standard fee calculation applies to each subsequent

## Strategic Considerations

- Reporting full cost upfront means subsequent filings only pay the $130 minimum — potential fee savings on multi-discipline projects
- Must accurately forecast ALL work types and costs at initial filing stage
- If additional costs arise later, you still pay the difference
- Best suited for projects where the full scope is well-defined at filing
- For projects with uncertain scope, traditional per-filing cost reporting may be safer
