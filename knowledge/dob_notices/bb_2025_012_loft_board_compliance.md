# BB 2025-012: Loft Board Compliance Application Pro-Cert Scope (MDL 7-C)

## Overview

This bulletin clarifies the scope of work that may be professionally certified (pro-cert) in Loft Board Compliance applications filed under Interim Multiple Dwelling (IMD) provisions of the Multiple Dwelling Law (MDL) Article 7-C. The guidance addresses what work qualifies as pro-certified scope versus what requires DOB examination and approval, and provides expeditors with a framework for determining pro-cert eligibility and filing strategy for loft legalization projects.

## Background: MDL Article 7-C and the Loft Board

### What Is an Interim Multiple Dwelling (IMD)?

An **Interim Multiple Dwelling** is an illegally subdivided residential space (typically a commercial or manufacturing loft) that was occupied as residential prior to July 1, 2008, and meets certain occupancy criteria. The MDL Article 7-C ("Loft Law") provides a path to legalization through the Loft Board.

**Key Characteristics of IMD:**
- Located in non-residential zoning district (e.g., M1, M2, C6)
- Occupied as residential dwelling unit(s) before July 1, 2008
- Not built as residential building
- Original work (subdivision into units) was done without DOB permits
- Occupants have tenant protections under MDL Article 7-C

### Loft Board Role

The **Loft Board** (a division of DOB) oversees legalization applications for IMD units. It has authority to:
- Approve legalization plans and compliance programs
- Set alteration requirements for safety and code compliance
- Review applications and grant IMD status [VERIFY: specific Loft Board authority]

## What is "Professional Certification" (Pro-Cert) in Loft Context?

**Professional Certification** means that a licensed professional engineer (PE) or licensed architect (LA) seals plans and specifications, certifying that proposed work complies with applicable Building Code and safety standards. The PE/LA takes responsibility for the design and compliance, allowing DOB to approve the work without detailed plan examination.

**Pro-Cert is NOT:**
- A waiver of DOB requirements
- Permission to skip code-required work
- Approval of non-compliant designs (sealed plans must still comply)
- Authority to override Loft Board requirements

**Pro-Cert IS:**
- Certification by licensed professional of compliance
- Streamlined approval pathway (faster than full DOB review)
- Professional's seal and signature on all design documents
- Assumption of liability by PE/LA for compliance

## Pro-Cert Eligible Scope: Work That Can Be Sealed by PE/LA

### Category 1: Interior Alteration Work (Generally Pro-Cert Eligible)

**Eligible Work:**
- Partition walls and interior subdivisions (non-load-bearing walls creating dwelling units)
- Interior door, window, and trim installation
- Interior finishes (flooring, wall finishes, ceilings)
- Interior electrical outlets and lighting (branch circuit work)
- Interior plumbing (branch lines to fixtures; within unit)
- HVAC branch ducts and registers (interior distribution)
- Interior acoustic/sound-dampening treatments
- Interior egress signage, exit markings
- Smoke detectors and fire extinguishers (interior-only device placement)

**Pro-Cert Process:**
- PE/LA designs interior layout and assemblies
- PE/LA certifies compliance with occupancy load, egress requirements, fire-rated assemblies (if applicable)
- PE/LA seals floor plans, interior elevation drawings, and finish schedules
- DOB issues approval without detailed examination [VERIFY: expedited timeline]

**Limitations:**
- Interior work must not affect building's structural system or exterior envelope
- Interior work must not modify MEP systems beyond branch connections
- If interior walls are load-bearing, structural engineer must certify [VERIFY: specific requirement]

### Category 3: Fire and Life Safety Upgrades (Partial Pro-Cert)

#### Fire-Rated Assembly Installation

**Pro-Cert Eligible:**
- Interior fire-rated partition walls separating units from corridors or other units
- Fire-rated door and frame assemblies (interior; certified assemblies per UL or FAS)
- Caulking and sealant of penetrations (certified fire-stop details per construction standards)
- Interior smoke barriers (non-rated, smoke-control)
- [VERIFY: specific fire-rated assembly elements pro-cert-eligible in loft context]

**NOT Pro-Cert Eligible (DOB Review or Additional Approvals):**
- Building exterior facade fire rating (exterior walls; may require special engineering review)
- Roof fire rating (membrane, decking; may require special approval)
- Common vertical shafts with fire ratings (complex calculations; often DOB examines)
- Firestopping of penetrations through exterior walls (may require DOB due to facade integration)

**Pro-Cert Limitations:**
- PE must specify UL-listed or NYC FAS (Fire Appearance and Safety) rated assemblies
- Correct installation details required; PE seals drawings showing assembly per rating
- Paint or finish specifications must not violate fire rating (e.g., non-rated paint on rated walls)

#### Egress System Design

**Pro-Cert Eligible:**
- Interior corridor layout and egress pathway design
- Exit door sizing and swing direction (per occupancy load)
- Egress signage and emergency lighting layout and power requirements
- Stairway and egress handrails (if interior renovation includes stairs)
- Egress width calculations and travel distance (per 2022 Building Code Chapter 10)
- Accessible routes within units (ADA accessibility) [VERIFY: pro-cert scope for accessibility]

**NOT Pro-Cert Eligible (DOB Review):**
- Building primary exit stairway (if shared common stair; DOB often requires structural engineer review, and DOB examination)
- Exterior exit stairs or fire escapes (exterior work; requires DOB approval)
- Exit discharge conditions (exterior exit door and landing; connection to street) [VERIFY: pro-cert scope]
- Accessible exit route (if involving common areas or structural elements)
- Occupancy load and egress capacity (complex; often requires DOB coordination) [VERIFY: whether PE can seal occupancy load for multiple units, or DOB sets]

**Pro-Cert Limitations:**
- PE must calculate occupancy load per 2022 Building Code Table 1004.1.2
- Travel distance to exit: interior measurement per code; exterior measurement (street access) must be confirmed
- Exit discharge: exterior conditions evaluated and certified by PE (or DOB may require field review)

### Category 4: Structural/Load-Bearing Work (Limited Pro-Cert or DOB Required)

#### Interior Structural Modifications (Generally Requires Structural Engineer, DOB May Review)

**Pro-Cert Eligible (Structural PE Only):**
- Interior non-load-bearing partition walls (minimal structural consideration)
- Interior floor system modifications (if existing floor can support without reinforcement) [VERIFY: scope]

**LIKELY REQUIRES DOB EXAMINATION (Not Fully Pro-Cert):**
- Removal of load-bearing walls or columns (requires reinforcement design, structural calculations)
- New openings in load-bearing walls (lintel design, structural certification)
- Floor reinforcement due to use change or new partition loading
- Roof modifications or new openings
- Foundation or basement work (rare in lofts, but highly restricted)

**Pro-Cert Limitations:**
- Structural PE must perform calculations and site visits
- Load-bearing element modifications typically require DOB structural engineer review and approval [VERIFY: standard practice]

### Loft Board vs. DOB Authority

| **Issue** | **Loft Board Authority** | **DOB Authority** | **Coordination** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Occupancy eligibility (pre-July 1, 2008 date) | Primary; Loft Board determines | Advisory | Loft Board leads; DOB follows |
| Building structural safety | Advisory | Primary; DOB examines | DOB leads structural review |
| Fire and life safety code compliance | Advises on loft-specific issues | Primary; DOB examines | DOB examines against 2022 BC |

| Interior design and space layout | Advises on habitability | Examines per occupancy code | Both review; DOB final on occupancy |
| Ongoing compliance and enforcement | Loft Board monitors unit conditions | DOB inspects for violations | Shared enforcement; Loft Board tenant-focused |

## Pro-Cert Scope Decision Framework: What Can Be Sealed?

### Worksheet for Determining Pro-Cert Eligibility

**For each work item in the loft compliance scope:**

**Question 1: Is this interior or exterior work?**
- EXTERIOR → Requires DOB examination (not pro-cert, unless specific roof/facade exception [VERIFY])
- INTERIOR → Continue to Question 2

**Question 2: Does this work affect building structure, exterior envelope, or common systems?**
- YES → Requires DOB examination (not fully pro-cert; may have PE involvement but DOB reviews)
- NO → Continue to Question 3

**Question 3: Is this work isolated to individual dwelling units with no impact on other units?**
- YES → Likely pro-cert eligible; continue to Question 4
- NO (affects common areas or multiple units) → Likely DOB examination required

**Question 4: Can a licensed PE/LA seal this work and certify code compliance?**
- YES → Pro-cert eligible (interior alteration, MEP branch systems, interior fire-rated assemblies, egress design)
- NO → DOB examination required

**Question 5: Is this work a standard application of code-compliant details (e.g., fire-rated doors, egress signage)?**
- YES → Pro-cert eligible (PE seals using certified/rated assemblies)
- NO (complex or novel design; calculations; unique conditions) → DOB examination may be recommended or required [VERIFY]

## Common Pro-Cert Scope Examples for Loft Compliance

### Example 1: Residential Loft — Interior Subdivision Into Two Units

**Work Scope:**
- Existing open loft space (approx. 2,000 sf) with existing kitchen and bathroom
- Subdivision into two residential units using interior partition walls
- Each unit: approx. 1,000 sf, shared vertical core (stairs, MEP)

**Interior Partition Wall Design (Pro-Cert Eligible):**
- Design of non-load-bearing gypsum drywall partition to separate units
- 1-hour fire-rated partition assembly (if required for occupancy separation) [VERIFY: current rule]
- Door opening between units (if connecting; fire-rated door if assembly is rated)
- Penetration of utilities (electrical, plumbing, HVAC) through partition (PE designs, specifies fire-stopping)

**PE/LA Seals:**
- Floor plan showing partition wall location and assembly type
- Partition wall section detail and fire-rated assembly specification (UL or FAS rated assembly reference)
- Fire-stop details at penetrations (per construction standard fire-stop drawings)
- Finishes (paint, caulk, sealant) per fire-rating specification (e.g., intumescent caulk at gaps)

**Pro-Cert Result:** PE certifies interior partition complies with fire-rating requirement and building code. DOB approves without detailed examination (if documents are complete).

## Expediting Tips for Loft Compliance Applications

### Tip 1: Clarify Pro-Cert Eligibility Early

**Before hiring PE/LA and preparing plans, consult with DOB or expediting counsel about:**
- Which work is definitely pro-cert eligible (interior partitions, MEP branch systems)
- Which work will require DOB examination (exterior, structural, common systems)
- Whether the specific loft layout and work scope support a fully pro-cert pathway

**Benefit:** Avoid designing work that cannot be pro-cert-ed, then having to re-submit for DOB examination (delays, cost).
