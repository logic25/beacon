# Vacate Order Removal Guide

## Overview

A Vacate Order is an official directive issued by the NYC Department of Buildings (DOB) or FDNY requiring that a building or portion thereof be immediately vacated and remain unoccupied due to unsafe conditions. Vacate Orders represent serious regulatory actions that halt occupancy and require substantial remedial action before the building can be reoccupied. This guide outlines the process for removing/rescinding a Vacate Order to restore occupancy rights.

## What is a Vacate Order?

### Definition
A Vacate Order is a mandatory directive issued by DOB or FDNY stating that a building, floor, or portion thereof is unsafe for occupancy and must be immediately vacated. The order prohibits any occupancy or use until rescinded by the issuing authority following correction of unsafe conditions and reinspection.

### Legal Basis
- Issued under **NYC Administrative Code § 28-207.1** (Hazardous Buildings) and § 28-202.1 (Applicability)
- Enforced by DOB (building/structural issues) or FDNY (fire safety issues)
- Creates a record on the building that affects property value, insurability, and financing
- Violation of a Vacate Order (continuing occupancy) results in serious fines [VERIFY: $500-$5,000 per day]

### Impact on Property
- Prevents issuance or renewal of Certificate of Occupancy (CO)
- Blocks operational permits and business licenses
- Impacts property financing and insurance
- Creates significant liability for owner and occupants
- May trigger emergency rent procedures for residential properties (HPD involvement)

## Types of Vacate Orders

### Full Vacate Order
- Entire building declared unsafe
- All occupants must vacate immediately
- No portions of the building may be occupied
- Issued when conditions threaten life safety across the entire structure

### Partial Vacate Order
- Specific floors, areas, or spaces declared unsafe
- Remainder of building may remain occupied
- Common when damage is limited to specific areas (e.g., fire damage to upper floors)
- Issued when isolation of affected area allows safe occupancy of remainder

## Common Triggers for Vacate Orders

### Structural Damage
- **Foundation or structural failure**: cracking, settling, buckling, compression
- **Severe water damage**: structural degradation, rot, collapse risk
- **Earthquake, storm, or impact damage**: structural compromise from external events
- **Overloaded or improper construction**: exceeding design capacity or code non-compliance
- **Deteriorated structural members**: advanced corrosion, rot, or degradation

### Fire Damage
- **Structural fire damage**: weakened columns, beams, or floor systems
- **Fire with structural involvement**: burning through structural elements
- **Building not meeting fire rating requirements** after damage
- **Issued by FDNY**: if fire damage creates immediate life safety hazard

### Serious Building Code Violations (SWO - Serious and Willful Omissions)
- **Substantial lack of egress**: missing or obstructed emergency exits
- **Lack of fire-rated separation**: walls not meeting code fire ratings
- **Hazardous electrical conditions**: exposed wiring, serious fire risk
- **Lack of essential utilities**: no water, heat, or functioning plumbing
- **Structural conditions dangerous to human life**: general dangerous condition

### Illegal Occupancy
- **Illegal conversion**: unauthorized change of occupancy or use
- **Occupancy exceeding permitted density**: more occupants than legally allowed
- **Illegal residential conversion**: non-residential space converted to housing without permits
- **Unpermitted subdivisions**: illegal creation of additional units or spaces

### Environmental Hazards
- **Contamination**: lead, mold, asbestos, or chemical contamination
- **Lack of habitability features**: absence of windows, ventilation, or sanitary facilities
- **Utility hazards**: gas leaks, electrical hazards, or plumbing failures

## Steps to Remove/Rescind a Vacate Order

### Phase 1: Investigation and Planning

#### 1.1 Obtain the Vacate Order Details
- Request a copy of the Vacate Order from DOB or FDNY (depending on issuing authority)
- Identify the specific unsafe conditions that triggered the order
- Determine if it's a full or partial vacate
- Identify any associated violations that must be addressed

**Action**: Contact DOB Housing Violations Bureau (HVB) or FDNY depending on issuer. [VERIFY: specific contact procedures and timeframes]

#### 1.2 Engage Professional Assessments
- **Structural Engineer**: Assess structural damage and provide remediation recommendations
- **Fire Safety Consultant**: Assess fire safety issues and code compliance
- **Environmental Inspector** (if applicable): Assess contamination or hazard conditions
- Document all findings in detailed engineering reports

#### 1.3 Develop Remediation Plan
- Based on professional assessments, develop a comprehensive remediation plan
- Identify all work required to cure unsafe conditions
- Estimate costs, timeline, and sequencing
- Determine permits and approvals needed for remediation work

### Phase 2: Permits and Approvals for Remediation Work

#### 2.1 Obtain Work Permits
If remediation requires construction, obtain necessary permits:
- **ALT (Alterations)** permit for structural or system repairs
- **Demolition** permit if removal of damaged materials required
- **Electrical**, **Plumbing**, **HVAC** permits for system repairs
- **Sprinkler**, **Fire Alarm** permits if systems affected
- [VERIFY] Expedited permitting may be available for emergency remediation

#### 2.2 DOB Plan Review and Approval
- Submit remediation plans and engineering reports to DOB for review
- Plans must demonstrate that proposed work will cure all unsafe conditions
- DOB approves plan or provides deficiency letter requiring revisions
- Obtain **DOB approval** before commencing remediation work

#### 2.3 FDNY Coordination (if applicable)
If Vacate Order issued by FDNY or involves fire safety:
- Submit fire safety remediation plans to FDNY Bureau of Fire Prevention
- Demonstrate compliance with Fire Code and Life Safety Code
- Obtain FDNY approval for fire safety remediation
- Coordinate with DOB on fire-related system approvals

### Phase 3: Execution of Remediation Work

#### 3.1 Perform Approved Remediation
- Execute all work according to DOB-approved plans
- Engage licensed contractors for structural, electrical, plumbing, and fire safety work
- Maintain compliance with all permit requirements and construction codes
- Document all work completion with photos and reports

#### 3.2 Inspections During Work
- DOB may conduct inspections during remediation to verify work is proceeding per plans
- Address any issues identified during inspections immediately
- Maintain communication with DOB project manager regarding progress
- Schedule inspections at key phases (e.g., structural repairs, system reinstallation)

### Phase 4: Required Inspections and Certifications

#### 4.1 Structural Inspection and Certification
**Required for structural damage vacates:**
- Licensed Professional Engineer (PE) inspects completed structural repairs
- PE verifies repairs meet code requirements and design plans
- PE issues certification statement: "Building is structurally sound and safe for occupancy"
- This certification is essential for DOB approval of vacate rescission

**Documentation required:**
- Engineer's inspection report
- Structural calculations verifying adequacy of repairs
- Photos of completed work
- Copy of PE license

#### 4.2 Building Systems Inspection
**Required for utility/system deficiencies:**
- Electrical inspection (Licensed Electrician or DOB inspector)
- Plumbing inspection (Licensed Plumber or DOB inspector)
- HVAC inspection (Licensed HVAC contractor) for heating/ventilation adequacy
- Fire safety system inspections (fire alarm, sprinklers, standpipes)

**Documentation required:**
- Inspection certificates from licensed tradespeople
- Testing results demonstrating system functionality
- Code compliance certifications

#### 4.3 Fire Safety Inspection (if FDNY-issued Vacate)
- FDNY inspector conducts on-site fire safety inspection
- Verifies corrected conditions meet Fire Code requirements
- Confirms egress, fire separation, fire protection systems, and hazard removal
- FDNY issues inspection approval before DOB rescission

**Documentation required:**
- FDNY inspection report
- Fire Safety Director certification (if applicable)
- Fire protection system approvals

#### 4.4 Environmental Certification (if hazard-related)
**For contamination or environmental hazards:**
- Licensed Environmental Professional inspects conditions
- Confirms remediation of contamination or hazards
- Issues certification of compliance with environmental standards
- For lead: EPA or state-certified lead inspector may be required

### Phase 5: Engineering Reports and Structural Assessments

#### 5.1 Comprehensive Engineering Report
A detailed engineering report is critical for demonstrating the building is safe:

**Required components:**
- **Executive Summary**: Overview of unsafe conditions and remediation performed
- **Condition Assessment**: Professional description of damage/unsafe conditions identified
- **Remediation Plan**: Detailed description of all work performed
- **Code Compliance Analysis**: Demonstration that corrected conditions meet code requirements
- **Structural Calculations**: Engineering analysis (for structural damage only)
- **Materials and Methods**: Description of materials used and construction techniques
- **Testing and Inspection Results**: Documentation of all inspections and system testing
- **Professional Certification**: PE certification that building is safe for occupancy

#### 5.2 Structural Assessment (if structural damage)
For structural damage vacates, a comprehensive structural assessment is essential:
- **Load Analysis**: Verification that structure can support design loads
- **Member Verification**: Each structural member (columns, beams, floor systems) analyzed
- **Connection Verification**: All critical connections verified for adequacy
- **Deflection Analysis**: Verification that deflections are within allowable limits
- **Comparison to Original Design**: Verification that repaired structure meets original specifications
- PE stamp and signature

#### 5.3 Building Envelope Assessment (if water damage)
For water damage or building envelope issues:
- Assessment of roof conditions and waterproofing
- Inspection of exterior walls and window/door seals
- Documentation of moisture barriers and vapor protection
- Analysis of any mold or biological contamination
- Certification that moisture intrusion has been eliminated

### Phase 6: DOB Re-inspection and Rescission

#### 6.1 Prepare Rescission Application
When remediation is complete and all certifications obtained:
- Compile all documentation (engineering reports, inspection certificates, test results)
- Prepare formal written request to rescind Vacate Order
- Include summary of work performed and copies of approvals
- Include PE certification of structural adequacy and safety
- Submit to DOB with all supporting documentation

**Submission location**: [VERIFY: DOB Housing Violations Bureau or project manager]

#### 6.2 DOB Final Inspection
DOB conducts final inspection to verify:
- All unsafe conditions have been corrected
- Remediation work was performed per approved plans
- Building is safe for occupancy
- All code requirements are met
- All required inspections and certifications are in place

**Typical inspection items:**
- Visual verification of structural repairs
- Functionality testing of fire safety systems
- Electrical system safety verification
- Plumbing system functionality
- Egress and life safety compliance
- General code compliance review

#### 6.3 Issuance of Rescission
Upon successful final inspection:
- DOB issues **Vacate Order Rescission Letter** officially removing the vacate order
- Rescission letter states building is safe for occupancy
- Rescission is recorded in DOB system
- Building occupancy rights restored
- Owner may proceed with Certificate of Occupancy (CO) application if needed

**Timeline for DOB action**: [VERIFY: 1-4 weeks after submission of complete rescission request]

## Coordination with HPD (if Residential)

### HPD Jurisdiction
If the building or portion thereof is **residential housing**, the NYC Department of Housing Preservation and Development (HPD) has concurrent jurisdiction:

### Rental Assistance and Emergency Housing
- Vacate Order in residential building triggers HPD emergency housing procedures
- Tenants may be eligible for temporary relocation assistance (HPD Emergency Assistance)
- Tenants may have rights to "Temporary Alternate Dwelling" (TAD) assistance
- HPD coordinates with DOB on residential vacate conditions

### HPD Coordination Steps
1. HPD must be notified of Vacate Order rescission process
2. HPD will conduct its own inspection of residential conditions
3. Residential units must meet habitability standards (heat, hot water, safe egress, etc.)
4. HPD approval required before units can be re-occupied
5. Building owner may be obligated to provide relocation assistance during remediation

### Timeline Coordination
- HPD re-occupancy approval timeline: [VERIFY: typically 1-2 weeks after remediation]
- Coordinate HPD inspection with DOB inspection to minimize disruption
- Allow time for tenant notification and return to building after approvals

## Timeline Expectations

### Typical Vacate Order Removal Timeline

**Phase 1 (Investigation & Planning)**: 2-4 weeks
- Obtain order details and professional assessments
- Develop remediation plan
- Total: 2-4 weeks

**Phase 2 (Permits & Approvals)**: 4-8 weeks
- Obtain construction permits for remediation work
- DOB plan review and approval
- FDNY coordination (if applicable)
- Total: 4-8 weeks

**Phase 3 (Remediation Work)**: 4-12 weeks
- Execution timeline depends on extent of damage and complexity
- Structural repairs: 4-12 weeks
- System repairs: 1-4 weeks
- Minor deficiencies: 1-2 weeks
- Total: highly variable (4-12 weeks typical)

**Phase 4 (Inspections & Certifications)**: 2-4 weeks
- Professional inspections and certifications
- Engineering reports preparation
- Compilation of documentation
- Total: 2-4 weeks

**Phase 5 (DOB Rescission)**: 1-4 weeks
- DOB final inspection and rescission letter
- Total: 1-4 weeks

**Overall Timeline**: 13-32 weeks (3-8 months)
- Simple structural repairs (minor damage): 2-4 months
- Complex structural repairs (extensive damage): 4-8 months
- Multiple deficiencies requiring coordination: 6-8+ months

**Factors affecting timeline:**
- Extent and complexity of unsafe conditions
- Availability of skilled contractors
- Permit queue times at DOB
- Weather (for exterior/structural work)
- Contractor schedule and capacity
- DOB inspection queue and availability
- Tenant coordination requirements (for residential)

## Relationship to Unsafe Building Proceedings

### Unsafe Building Violation
A Vacate Order is often issued in conjunction with an Unsafe Building violation (ECB violation) under NYC Administrative Code § 28-202.1.

### Relationship:
- **Unsafe Building violation**: Formal violation issued for structural/code deficiency
- **Vacate Order**: Accompanying enforcement action requiring occupancy cessation
- **Violation and Vacate are linked**: Removal of Vacate Order typically requires closure of associated Unsafe Building violation

### Closure of Associated Violations
When rescinding a Vacate Order:
1. All associated unsafe building violations must be corrected
2. DOB verifies violations are closed when reviewing rescission request
3. Engineering reports must demonstrate code compliance addressing violation
4. Violation closure certification required as part of rescission package

## Emergency Vacate vs. Standard Vacate

### Emergency Vacate Order
- **Issued when**: Immediate life-threatening danger identified
- **Issued by**: FDNY (fire) or DOB (structural)
- **Effective**: Immediate
- **Authority**: Emergency authority under Administrative Code § 28-207.1
- **Consequences**: Severe penalties for non-compliance

**Examples:** Fire with structural involvement, collapsed floor, active structural failure

### Standard Vacate Order
- **Issued after**: DOB inspection identifies unsafe condition
- **Issued by**: DOB Inspector or FDNY Inspector
- **Notice period**: [VERIFY: typically 24-48 hours before effective date]
- **Process**: Usually includes Unsafe Building violation as precursor
- **Consequences**: Serious penalties but may allow brief compliance period

**Examples:** SWO violations, serious code non-compliance, long-standing deficiencies

### Difference in Rescission Process
- Emergency vacates may require faster remediation and rescission
- Standard vacates allow more time for planning and contractor engagement
- Process for removal is similar, but emergency situations may warrant prioritized DOB review [VERIFY]

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### Challenge 1: Financing Remediation Work
- Cost of structural repairs can be substantial
- Owner may have difficulty funding work
- **Solution**: Explore SBA disaster loans, insurance claims, or financing from banks
- Some organizations provide emergency remediation grants [VERIFY: specific programs]

### Challenge 2: Contractor Availability
- Shortage of skilled structural contractors
- Long lead times for emergency work
- **Solution**: Engage multiple contractors, explore out-of-state contractors, prioritize work sequencing

### Challenge 3: DOB Permit Queue Delays
- DOB plan review can take 6+ weeks
- Expedited review available in some cases [VERIFY]
- **Solution**: Engage expeditor/consultant to track progress, request expedited review if justified

### Challenge 4: Tenant Coordination (Residential)
- Displaced tenants need temporary housing
- Re-occupancy requires coordination of tenant return
- **Solution**: Work with HPD for relocation assistance, maintain tenant communication

### Challenge 5: Multiple Systems Issues
- Vacate may involve multiple deficiencies (structural, fire safety, electrical)
- Coordination of multiple inspectors and contractors
- **Solution**: Develop integrated remediation plan, schedule inspections together

## Important Notes for Expeditors

1. **Act quickly**: Vacate Orders create urgent timelines and significant financial impact
2. **Engage professionals early**: Structural engineers and consultants should be engaged immediately
3. **Develop comprehensive plan**: Plan must address all unsafe conditions identified in the vacate order
4. **Document everything**: Professional certifications and inspection reports are critical to rescission
5. **Coordinate with DOB**: Early communication with DOB can identify specific requirements and expedite review
6. **Manage timeline carefully**: Permit queues and inspection scheduling can extend the process significantly
7. **Address related violations**: Any associated ECB violations must be simultaneously resolved
8. **For residential properties**: Engage HPD early to coordinate tenant rights and relocation assistance
9. **Maintain tenant communication**: Regular updates to displaced tenants on remediation progress and re-occupancy timeline
10. **Plan for follow-up**: After rescission, ensure long-term maintenance plans are in place to prevent recurrence
