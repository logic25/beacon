# Equipment Use Permit (EUP) Guide

## Overview

An Equipment Use Permit (EUP) is a formal authorization from the NYC Department of Buildings that allows building owners and operators to install, maintain, and operate specific types of mechanical and technical equipment. This guide covers the EUP filing process, required documentation, and integration with other DOB regulatory requirements.

## What EUP Covers

Equipment Use Permits are required for the following equipment categories:

- **Boilers and Pressure Vessels**: Steam boilers, hot water boilers, low-pressure vessels, high-pressure vessels
- **Elevators and Conveyances**: Passenger elevators, freight elevators, dumbwaiters, escalators, moving walkways
- **Refrigeration Equipment**: Commercial refrigeration systems, cooling systems, chiller units
- **Fuel Oil Storage**: Underground and aboveground fuel oil storage tanks for heating systems
- **Mechanical Equipment**: HVAC systems, ventilation equipment, energy systems
- **Other Regulated Equipment**: Equipment specifically regulated under the NYC Building Code and Administrative Code

The permit confirms that equipment meets current Code standards and that the operator is qualified to maintain it.

## When EUP is Required vs. Included in Other Permits

### EUP is Required Independently When:
- Adding new equipment to an existing building
- Replacing or upgrading existing equipment
- Transferring equipment responsibility to a new operator or owner
- Equipment use has changed materially
- Renewing annual permits

### EUP May Be Included With Other Permits:
- **Alteration Permits**: When equipment installation is part of a larger building alteration, the EUP may be issued concurrently with the Alteration permit
- **New Building Permits**: For new construction, equipment permits are typically part of the CO (Certificate of Occupancy) issuance process
- **Site Safety Plans**: Certain temporary or project-specific equipment may be covered under site safety documentation

**Note**: Always clarify with DOB NOW or your expediter whether separate EUP filing is necessary or if approval is embedded in related permits.

## Filing Process

### Step 1: Prepare Documentation
Gather all required documents (see Required Documents section below) and ensure they are current and accurate.

### Step 2: File via DOB NOW
All Equipment Use Permits must be filed electronically through **DOB NOW**:
- Access the filing portal at [VERIFY: dob.mycheckup.nyc or applicable portal]
- Select "Equipment Use Permit" application type
- Complete all required fields
- Upload supporting documents
- Pay applicable fees

### Step 3: Initial Review
DOB conducts a preliminary review of completeness (typically 5-10 business days). If documents are missing or incomplete, you will receive a Request for Additional Info (RAI). Respond promptly to avoid delays.

### Step 4: Detailed Technical Review
Once initial review passes, DOB performs a detailed technical evaluation (typically 15-30 business days depending on equipment complexity and DOB workload).

### Step 5: Approval and Issuance
Upon approval, the EUP is issued and becomes effective. You will receive the permit number and authorization documentation via DOB NOW.

## Required Documents

### Universal Requirements (All Equipment Types):
- **Equipment Use Permit Application**: Completed DOB application form
- **Building Address and BIN**: Accurate Building Identification Number
- **Equipment Description**: Type, manufacturer, model number, specifications
- **Equipment Location**: Floor level, room designation, building location
- **Operator/Responsible Party Information**: Licensed operator credentials (if required)
- **Building Code Compliance Certification**: Statement confirming equipment meets current NYC Building Code standards
- **Insurance Information**: General liability insurance and equipment-specific coverage [VERIFY minimum coverage amounts]

### Equipment-Specific Requirements:

**Boilers:**
- Boiler manufacturer specifications and performance data
- Safety valve certification
- Piping and controls diagram
- Latest inspection report (if existing boiler)
- H-Class or U-Class stamp certification
- ASME or equivalent certification documentation

**Elevators:**
- Elevator pit and hoistway plans
- Roping diagrams and specifications
- Safety equipment certifications
- Inspection and testing records
- Licensed Elevator Mechanic (LEM) certification
- Machine room specifications and safety features

**Refrigeration Equipment:**
- Refrigerant type and charge specifications
- Piping and component diagrams
- Pressure relief system documentation
- Vibration isolation specifications
- NYS Department of Environmental Conservation compliance (if required)

**Fuel Oil Storage:**
- Tank specifications (size, material, construction)
- Tank location diagram and site plan
- Secondary containment documentation
- Fill and vent line diagrams
- Environmental compliance documentation
- Spill response plan

**HVAC/Mechanical Systems:**
- Equipment schedules and specifications
- Ductwork and distribution diagrams
- Controls and monitoring system details
- Capacity and performance data

## Relationship to DOB Equipment Inspections

EUP approval is distinct from DOB equipment inspections:

- **EUP Approval**: Administrative authorization that equipment is permitted and compliant at the time of filing
- **Periodic Inspections**: DOB conducts periodic inspections during building operations to verify ongoing compliance
- **Annual Boiler Inspections**: Required for boilers under NYC Administrative Code § 4-303
- **Elevator Inspections**: Required annually or per elevator-specific regulations
- **Violation Response**: If an inspection identifies a violation, the EUP remains valid but a violation must be corrected

Equipment can be taken out of service (deactivated) without canceling the EUP, but reactivation requires updated compliance certification.

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Issue 1: Incomplete or Missing Documentation
**Problem**: DOB issues RAI for missing technical drawings, certifications, or operator credentials.
**Solution**:
- Verify all required documents before submission
- Use a checklist based on equipment type
- Confirm professional certifications are current and properly sealed
- Respond to RAIs within DOB's specified timeframe (typically 10 business days)

### Issue 2: Equipment Does Not Meet Current Code Standards
**Problem**: Existing equipment may not fully comply with updated NYC Building Code provisions.
**Solution**:
- Pursue a determination (see Determination Filing Guide) to establish code compliance pathway
- Document equivalent compliance or hardship conditions
- Consider equipment replacement or upgrades
- Obtain PE-sealed certification of code compliance or waiver application

### Issue 3: Operator/Responsible Party Credentials Missing or Expired
**Problem**: Licensed operator credentials (such as LEM for elevators) are missing or expired.
**Solution**:
- Verify current licensing status of all required professionals
- Designate a qualified, licensed operator or contractor
- Obtain required certifications before EUP renewal
- Maintain ongoing professional development records

### Issue 4: Insurance Documentation Insufficient or Expired
**Problem**: General liability or equipment-specific insurance is expired or does not meet DOB minimums.
**Solution**:
- Verify current insurance coverage amounts with your insurance provider
- Ensure policy includes NYC Department of Buildings as additional insured
- Update insurance information in DOB NOW before expiration
- Maintain certificates of insurance on file

### Issue 5: Equipment Specifications Changed or Modified
**Problem**: Equipment has been altered, upgraded, or had components replaced without updating the permit.
**Solution**:
- File an EUP Amendment application (see Step 2 of Filing Process)
- Provide updated equipment specifications and supporting documentation
- Obtain revised professional certifications if required
- Avoid using modified equipment until amendment is approved

### Issue 6: Multiple Equipment Items in Single Permit
**Problem**: Uncertainty about whether multiple pieces of equipment can be covered in one EUP application.
**Solution**:
- Equipment can often be grouped by type and location on a single permit (e.g., multiple boilers in same plant room)
- Verify with DOB NOW or an expediter whether consolidation is permitted
- If required, file separate applications with clear cross-references
- Document the relationship between related equipment

### Issue 7: Permit Expires During Processing
**Problem**: EUP expires before renewal is approved, creating an operational gap.
**Solution**:
- File renewals 45 days in advance to ensure approval before expiration
- Request expedited processing if normal timeline will not meet renewal date
- If permit has expired, the equipment cannot legally operate; file emergency renewal immediately
- Consult with your expediter regarding any penalty procedures
