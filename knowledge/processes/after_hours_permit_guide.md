# After Hours Work Permit (AHV) Guide

## Overview

An After Hours Variance (AHV) permits construction work outside standard NYC work hours. This guide covers the regulatory framework, filing process, requirements, and compliance obligations for obtaining and maintaining AHV permits through the Department of Buildings (DOB).

## Standard NYC Construction Work Hours

Construction work in NYC is restricted to the following hours without a variance:

- **Weekdays (Monday-Friday):** 7:00 AM - 6:00 PM
- **Saturdays:** Prohibited except in limited circumstances
- **Sundays:** Prohibited
- **Holidays:** Prohibited (including all City, State, and Federal holidays)

These hours apply regardless of the type of work or building classification.

## When an AHV is Required vs. Exemptions

### When AHV is Required

An AHV is required for any construction work that falls outside standard hours, including:

- Evening work (after 6:00 PM on weekdays)
- Weekend work (Saturday or Sunday)
- Holiday work
- Early morning work (before 7:00 AM on weekdays)

### Exemptions (No AHV Needed)

Certain activities do not require an AHV permit:

- **Emergency work:** Life safety repairs, structural hazards, water intrusion, boiler failures, and other immediate safety threats may proceed without AHV approval. DOB must be notified immediately (typically within 24 hours) of emergency conditions.
- **Utility work exemptions:** Certain utility companies (Con Edison, NYC Department of Environmental Protection, telecommunications providers) may have standing exemptions for critical infrastructure repairs. Verification required.
- **Pre-approved maintenance:** Some routine building maintenance may be permitted outside hours depending on building occupancy and the work scope.

Note: Emergency work still requires full DOB documentation and may require DEP coordination for noise-sensitive work.

## AHV Permit Types

### Standard AHV (Weekday Evening Variance)

Permits work beyond 6:00 PM on weekdays, typically ending at 10:00 PM or midnight depending on work type and location.

- **Typical use:** MEP installations, interior finishes, testing after hours
- **Noise restrictions:** May apply depending on DEP coordination
- **Duration:** Usually 30-90 days

### Weekend/Holiday AHV

Permits work on Saturdays, Sundays, or holidays.

- **More restrictive approval:** Requires strong justification (critical path, safety, operational necessity)

## Initial AHV Filing vs. Renewal Process

### Initial AHV Filing

**Timing:** File 5-10 business days before planned work start date (minimum). Weekend/holiday permits require 15+ business days advance notice.

**In DOB NOW:**
1. Navigate to "Filings" → "New Filing" → "Work Permit" or "Variances"
2. Select "After Hours Variance" permit type
3. Complete work scope description (detailed description of work to be performed)
4. Specify exact hours and dates
5. Upload supporting documents (see Required Documents section)
6. Pay applicable fee
7. Submit for DOB review

**Approval window:** 3-7 business days for standard requests; 7-15 business days for weekend/holiday

### Renewal Process

**When needed:** If work continues beyond the original AHV expiration date

**In DOB NOW:**
1. Locate the original AHV permit in your Active Filings
2. Select "Renewal" or "Extension" option
3. Update work scope if scope has changed
4. Specify new end date
5. Resubmit any updated documentation if conditions have changed
6. Pay renewal fee [VERIFY if lower than initial fee]
7. Submit for approval

**Renewal approval window:** 2-5 business days (typically faster than initial approval)

**Note:** Each renewal extends the permit but does not change the underlying conditions or requirements.

## Filing Through DOB NOW

All AHV permits must be filed electronically through DOB NOW (the Department of Buildings' digital filing system).

### System Access

- **Account creation:** Register with NYC Department of Buildings credential system
- **User roles:** General contractor, construction manager, expediter, or applicant can file
- **Authorization:** Applicant must have authority over the project or written authorization from permit holder

### Filing Steps in DOB NOW

1. **Log in** to DOB NOW at www1.nyc.gov/html/dob/html/dob_now/dobhome.shtml
2. **Select project** from your active filings or create new project record
3. **Choose filing type:** Variance → After Hours Work
4. **Enter project details:**
   - Job site address
   - Building classification
   - Work permit or job number
   - General contractor information
5. **Specify work details:**
   - Detailed description of work activities
   - Expected duration (start/end dates)
   - Specific hours (e.g., 6:00 PM - 10:00 PM weekdays, or Sat 7 AM - 6 PM)
   - Reason for after-hours work (critical path, equipment installation, etc.)
6. **Indicate noise mitigation:**
   - Will DEP coordination be required? (Yes/No)
   - Noise mitigation plan attached? (Yes/No)
7. **Upload documents** (see Required Documents section)
8. **Pay fee** through DOB NOW secure payment portal
9. **Submit** for DOB review
10. **Monitor status** in DOB NOW (notifications sent via email)

### Document Upload Requirements

All documents must be:
- PDF format (except images can be JPG/PNG)
- Clearly labeled with document type
- Legible (min. 200 dpi for scanned documents)
- Complete (no missing pages)

## Required Documents

### For All AHV Filings

- **Detailed work scope description:** Narrative describing all work activities, why after-hours work is necessary, expected duration of need, and impact assessment
- **Project site plan:** Showing building location, adjacent buildings/residences, site access, and equipment staging areas
- **Contractor information:** License numbers (GC, electrical, plumbing if applicable), insurance certificates, workers' compensation documentation
- **Timeline/schedule:** Indicating precisely when work will occur, how many weeks/months the AHV will be needed, and daily work hours
- **Contact information:** Site superintendent, project manager, and contractor 24-hour emergency contact

### For Weekend/Holiday AHV Filings

All of the above, plus:

- **Justification letter:** Explaining why weekend/holiday work is necessary; critical path delay analysis if applicable
- **Project schedule:** Showing how weekend work is essential to project timeline
- **Building occupancy plan:** If building is occupied (residential or commercial), document occupancy schedule and any tenant notification plan

### For AHVs Requiring Noise Mitigation/DEP Coordination

All of the above, plus:

- **Noise mitigation plan:** Prepared by qualified noise engineer (see Noise Mitigation Requirements section)
- **DEP Noise Mitigation Letter:** Pre-approval or coordination letter from NYC Department of Environmental Protection if required for noise-sensitive areas
- **Building notification plan:** Documentation of how neighbors/building tenants will be notified of after-hours work
- **Equipment list:** All machinery, tools, and equipment to be used, with noise levels [VERIFY dB requirements]
- **Protective measures description:** Temporary noise barriers, equipment location, operational procedures designed to minimize noise

### For Emergency AHV Filings

- **Emergency certification letter:** Description of emergency condition, life safety threat, or critical infrastructure impact
- **Structural engineer report:** If structural hazard is involved
- **Utility company coordination:** If utility infrastructure is at risk
- **Post-emergency documentation:** Submit follow-up documentation within 24 hours of work commencement

## Noise Mitigation Requirements

### When Noise Mitigation Plans are Required

Noise mitigation is typically required for:

- **Weekend/holiday work** in any building type
- **Evening work after 10:00 PM** on weekdays
- **Work in noise-sensitive areas:** Residential buildings, schools, hospitals, senior facilities
- **High-decibel activities:** Jackhammer work, pile driving, large equipment operation
- **Work within 500 feet** of occupied residential or institutional buildings [VERIFY distance threshold]

### DEP Coordination

For projects requiring noise mitigation:

1. **Contact DEP Noise Code Enforcement** (212-388-1631 or through NYC311) minimum 10 business days before work
2. **Provide project details:** Location, work type, hours, expected noise levels, mitigation measures
3. **Obtain DEP approval letter:** DEP may approve plan, request modifications, or deny AHV recommendation
4. **DEP letter must accompany AHV filing** in DOB NOW

### Noise Mitigation Plan Components

A formal noise mitigation plan should include:

- **Noise level assessment:** Projected noise levels (in decibels) for each work activity
- **Noise barriers:** Temporary sound walls, noise curtains, or enclosures with acoustic ratings
- **Equipment selection:** Specification of quieter equipment models or electric vs. diesel alternatives
- **Operational controls:** Limiting work duration, staggering loud activities, using noise suppressors on machinery
- **Monitoring protocol:** How noise compliance will be verified (noise monitoring logs, third-party monitoring if required)
- **Community notification:** How affected neighbors/occupants will be notified in advance
- **Complaint resolution:** Procedure for addressing noise complaints from adjacent properties or occupants

### Noise Levels [VERIFY Current DOB Standards]

Typical noise limits (subject to verification with current DOB/DEP standards):
- **Residential areas, weekdays 7 AM - 6 PM:** 85 dB(A)
- **Residential areas, weekdays 6 PM - 10 PM:** 80 dB(A)
- **Residential areas, weekends/holidays:** 75 dB(A)
- **Commercial areas, after hours:** 85 dB(A)

## Common Conditions and Restrictions

AHV permits are frequently issued with conditions that must be met. Common conditions include:

### Operational Conditions

- **Specific work hours only:** Work limited to stated hours; early shutdown required if conditions change
- **Specific days only:** Weekend work limited to stated dates (e.g., "Saturdays only, January 15-29")
- **Noise monitoring:** Independent noise monitoring required; reports submitted to DOB [VERIFY if required]
- **Site superintendent presence:** Qualified superintendent must be on-site during all AHV work hours
- **24-hour hotline:** Maintain complaint hotline for neighbor issues with documented responses
- **Dust and particle control:** Additional dust suppression measures required (additional beyond standard requirements)

### Notification Conditions

- **Neighbor notification:** Written notice to adjacent buildings/properties 48 hours before work
- **Building posting:** Notices posted in common areas of residential buildings
- **NYC311 advance notification:** Project details filed with NYC311 prior to commencement
- **DOB field inspector notification:** Site superintendent must call DOB before starting work each day

### Environmental/Safety Conditions

- **Air quality monitoring:** For work generating dust/particulates
- **Traffic control plan:** Enhanced traffic management if work affects street/sidewalk
- **CEQR conditions:** Environmental review findings (if applicable to project)
- **Building occupant coordination:** Procedures for notifying occupants of disruptions

### Penalty Conditions

- **Immediate suspension:** Work must stop if specified conditions are violated
- **Additional fines:** Violation may trigger separate violation notices beyond AHV revocation
- **Permit revocation:** Serious violations (safety threats, major noise disturbances) may result in immediate AHV cancellation

## Penalties for Working Without AHV

Working without a required AHV or working outside AHV permit conditions carries significant penalties:

### Administrative Penalties

- **Permit suspension:** General construction permit may be suspended pending investigation
- **License action:** Violations reported to NYC Department of Consumer Affairs; may affect contractor licensing

- **Cumulative effect:** Multiple violations can trigger contractor de-licensing

## Relationship to DOB After-Hours Enforcement Schedule

### DOB Enforcement Operations

The DOB Compliance and Enforcement division actively monitors for after-hours work violations, particularly:

- **Routine patrols:** Field inspectors conduct after-hours patrols in construction-heavy areas (average 2-3 patrols per week per district [VERIFY frequency])
- **Complaint response:** NYC311 complaints for after-hours work generate same-day or next-day field response
- **Weekend/holiday presence:** Enhanced enforcement on weekends and holidays in residential areas
- **High-risk areas:** Residential neighborhoods, historic districts, and noise-sensitive zones receive increased monitoring

### Inspector Authority

DOB field inspectors can:

- **Demand permit display:** Require visible AHV permit at job site
- **Review permit conditions:** Verify compliance with stated hours and conditions
- **Document violations:** Photograph/record work outside permit scope
- **Issue violations:** Issue violation notice for unpermitted work (NOV)
- **Issue stop work orders:** Order immediate cessation of work for safety violations or serious permit breaches
- **Interview superintendent/contractor:** Gather information on work activities and permit knowledge

### Coordination with NYPD

For serious violations or safety threats, DOB may coordinate with NYPD to:

- **Block site access:** Secure site to prevent continued unpermitted work
- **Issue criminal penalties:** In cases of egregious or repeat violations [VERIFY threshold for criminal referral]

### Notification Requirements During Enforcement

If inspector observes work, contractor must:

- **Provide permit documentation:** Display valid AHV permit
- **Identify responsible party:** Provide superintendent and contractor contact information
- **Explain work activities:** Describe how activities comply with permit scope
- **Cease restricted work:** Stop any work outside permit conditions immediately
