# TPA (Temporary Place of Assembly) Filing Guide

## 1. What is a TPA?

A **Temporary Place of Assembly (TPA)** is a temporary approval issued by NYC's Department of Buildings (DOB) and Fire Department (FDNY) that authorizes an event or assembly to take place at a location that does not have a permanent Place of Assembly Certificate of Operation (PACO).

A TPA allows a building or space that is not normally designated for assembly purposes to be temporarily used for gatherings, events, or assemblies. It is a time-limited authorization specific to a single event or a short series of events.

**Key Point:** A TPA is fundamentally different from a permanent PACO. A TPA is always temporary; a PACO is permanent.

---

## 2. When is a TPA Needed?

A TPA is required whenever you need to use a space that:

- **Does not have a permanent PACO** (Certificate of Operation for Place of Assembly)
- Is being temporarily repurposed for assembly or gathering use
- Will host occupants for a limited, defined period

### TPA is Needed For:

- **Temporary events** - one-time gatherings with a defined date and duration
- **Pop-up venues** - temporary business operations using a non-assembly space
- **One-time gatherings** - weddings, concerts, conferences, art openings
- **Seasonal uses** - venues operating only during certain months
- **Unconventional spaces** - lofts, warehouses, outdoor structures, converted industrial spaces
- **Emergency/disaster recovery** - temporary shelters or community gathering spaces
- **Festival and market spaces** - temporary outdoor assembly areas
- **Food vendor events** - food festivals, pop-up markets, temporary food halls

### TPA is NOT Needed If:

- The location already has a permanent PACO (Certificate of Operation for Place of Assembly)
- The assembly is incidental to a permitted office/retail/manufacturing use
- The gathering is purely private with no commercial intent and under a certain occupancy threshold [VERIFY threshold]

## 3. Common TPA Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Wedding in a Loft
- **Space:** Residential loft in Soho, 2,500 sq ft, typically office/residential
- **Event:** Wedding reception for 150 guests, 4-hour duration
- **TPA Required:** Yes. Loft has no PACO; temporary assembly use requires TPA.
- **Key Issues:** Egress paths, occupant load calculations, ADA access, emergency lighting
- **Timing:** File 4-6 weeks in advance; event scheduled for specific date

### Scenario 2: Corporate Conference in Warehouse
- **Space:** Industrial warehouse in Long Island City, 10,000 sq ft
- **Event:** 2-day tech conference, 500 attendees, breakfast/lunch service
- **TPA Required:** Yes. Warehouse is industrial use; temporary office/assembly designation needed.
- **Key Issues:** Adequate exits, stair widths for full occupancy, kitchen/food preparation coordination with Health Dept, parking/traffic with NYPD
- **Timing:** File 8-10 weeks in advance; coordinate with Health Dept, FDNY, and potentially NYPD

### Scenario 3: Food Festival (Outdoor)
- **Space:** Parking lot or plaza in Williamsburg, 15,000 sq ft
- **Event:** 2-day food festival, 30+ food vendors, 2,000+ attendees at peak
- **TPA Required:** Yes. Outdoor temporary assembly structure/vendor area.
- **Key Issues:** Weather protection structures, egress routes, vendor spacing, temporary toilet facilities, trash management
- **Timing:** File 10-12 weeks in advance; coordinate with Parks Dept (if applicable), FDNY, Health Dept, and DEP (noise permit)

### Scenario 4: Fashion Show/Art Opening
- **Space:** Empty retail space, galleries, or studio in Chelsea, 3,500 sq ft
- **Event:** 3-hour fashion show or evening art opening, 200-300 attendees
- **TPA Required:** Yes. Temporary assembly use in non-PA space.
- **Key Issues:** Temporary staging/runway setup, ADA accessibility, emergency egress, occupant load verification
- **Timing:** File 6-8 weeks in advance

## 4. TPA vs. PA (PACO) - Key Differences

| **Aspect** | **TPA (Temporary Place of Assembly)** | **PA/PACO (Permanent Place of Assembly)** |
|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | Time-limited (hours, days, weeks) | Permanent; indefinite operation |
| **Validity** | Single event or short series of defined events | Ongoing authorization |
| **Approval Level** | Filed with DOB; FDNY inspection typically required | Permanent license issued by DOB |
| **Building Alteration** | Temporary modifications acceptable | Permanent structural/safety modifications required |
| **Cost** | Lower filing fees | Higher initial cost; annual compliance |
| **Timeline** | Faster approval (2-6 weeks) | Slower approval (2-4 months) |
| **Renewal** | Not applicable; expires on event date | Annual renewal required |
| **Occupant Load** | Based on temporary egress plan | Based on permanent egress design |
| **Insurance** | Short-term event insurance | General liability policy required |
| **Ongoing Compliance** | No post-event compliance | Ongoing inspections, maintenance, certifications |

**Decision Tree:**
- Is the assembly use permanent or indefinite? → PACO (PA) required
- Is the assembly use time-limited to specific event(s)? → TPA required
- Is the space already licensed as a Place of Assembly? → Check existing PACO; may not need TPA

## 5. TPA Filing Process - Step by Step

### Phase 1: Pre-Filing Preparation (6-12 weeks before event)

**Step 1.1: Identify the Space**
- Confirm the exact address and building owner contact information
- Verify building use classification (residential, commercial, industrial, etc.)
- Confirm NO permanent PACO exists for the space [verify with DOB]
- Obtain site access for measurements and documentation

**Step 1.2: Determine Occupant Load**
- Define maximum expected occupancy for the event
- Calculate based on space capacity using NYC Fire Code Table 1003.2.2.6 [VERIFY exact table reference]
- Account for:
  - Floor area in sq ft
  - Type of use (e.g., standing/assembly = 7 sq ft per person; seated = 15-20 sq ft per person)
  - Space for food service, staging, registration areas
- Document occupancy assumptions

**Step 1.3: Analyze Egress Requirements**
- Identify all exits (doors, stairways, emergency escapes)
- Measure exit door widths and verify minimum 36" clearance (44" for assembly use) [VERIFY widths]
- Calculate required egress capacity based on occupant load
- Verify exit path is unobstructed and adequately signed
- Confirm adequate stairway width for expected traffic
- Identify any ADA accessibility issues requiring temporary ramps, elevators, or accessible seating

**Step 1.4: Arrange Insurance**
- Obtain short-term event liability insurance for the specific event dates
- Minimum coverage: $1 million general liability [VERIFY amount]
- Certificate of Insurance must be in DOB and FDNY names
- Contact insurance broker early; process takes 2-3 weeks
- Some policies have event-day issuance; others require advance notice

**Step 1.5: FDNY Pre-Submission Consultation** [OPTIONAL BUT RECOMMENDED]
- Contact FDNY in advance to discuss TPA requirements for the specific use
- FDNY may have preferences on occupant load limits or egress requirements
- Early coordination can speed up approval process
- Contact: FDNY Fire Prevention Bureau (Bureau of Fire Prevention, Manhattan Division)

### Phase 2: Document Preparation (4-8 weeks before event)

**Step 2.1: Prepare Floor Plan**
- Provide CAD or professional drawing of the space
- Show:
  - Overall dimensions and total square footage
  - Existing walls, columns, fixed equipment
  - Proposed event layout (tables, seating, staging, service areas)
  - All exit locations (marked clearly in green)
  - Exit paths and travel distances to egress
  - Occupancy distribution (where people will stand/sit)
  - Accessible parking, accessible route, ADA seating area (if applicable)
  - Kitchen/food service area (if applicable)
  - Emergency lighting locations [VERIFY if required for TPA]
  - AED (Automated External Defibrillator) location [VERIFY if required]
- Scale: 1/8" = 1' minimum
- Clearly label all areas by function

**Step 2.2: Prepare Egress Plan**
- Separate document from floor plan showing:
  - Exit routes from all areas of the space
  - Exit signage locations and sizing
  - Corridor widths to exits
  - Door swing directions and hardware
  - Handrails and stair specifications (if applicable)
  - Travel distance to exits (max 250 ft for assembly use) [VERIFY distance]
  - Emergency lighting requirements
  - Accessible egress routes (ADA compliance)
- Dimension all key distances
- Show compliance with NYC Fire Code Chapter 10 (Means of Egress)

**Step 2.3: Prepare Occupant Load Calculation Sheet**
- Document:
  - Total square footage of event space
  - Occupant load factor used (e.g., 7 sq ft/person for standing assembly)
  - Maximum occupancy for event
  - Breakdown by area (e.g., main event space, food area, lounge, etc.)
  - Basis for calculations (reference NYC Fire Code Table 1003.2.2.6)
- Include floor plan annotations showing occupancy density
- Conservative approach: round up occupancy numbers

**Step 2.4: Prepare Additional Documents**

**Insurance Certificate:**
- Certificate of Insurance showing:
  - Event date(s)
  - Minimum liability limits ($1M) [VERIFY]
  - DOB and FDNY as additional insured
  - Non-cancellation clause (cannot be cancelled without 30 days notice to city)

**Building Owner/Operator Consent:**
- Letter from building owner authorizing temporary assembly use
- Owner must acknowledge awareness of TPA requirements
- Owner contact information and signature

**Proof of Building Occupancy**
- If building is commercial/residential with ongoing use:
  - Confirmation that TPA is additional/temporary use
  - Plan for occupancy evacuation (if applicable)
- If building is vacant/industrial:
  - Confirmation of use category

**Event Details Sheet:**
- Event name and description
- Event date and time
- Expected maximum occupancy
- Type of event (assembly, reception, conference, etc.)
- Any special activities (food service, dancing, live entertainment, etc.)
- Operator/organizer contact information

**Step 2.5: Prepare Health Department Coordination (if applicable)**
- If food service is included:
  - File food service permit with NYC Health Department separately
  - TPA filing may reference Health Dept permit
  - Coordinate timeline: Health Dept permits may take 2-4 weeks [VERIFY]
- If beverages (including alcohol) will be served:
  - May require separate Liquor Authority approval
  - Coordinate if applicable

## 7. FDNY Coordination Requirements for TPA Events

### Mandatory FDNY Involvement:

**FDNY Fire Prevention Bureau must:**
1. Review and approve TPA floor plan and egress layout
2. Inspect the space before the event (typically 1-2 weeks before or 3-5 days before) [VERIFY timing]
3. Issue approval/permit letter if all requirements met
4. May require on-site Fire Marshal presence during event (for large events) [VERIFY thresholds]

### FDNY Contact Process:

1. **Initial Contact** (8-10 weeks before event):
   - Reach out to FDNY Fire Prevention Bureau, Manhattan Division (or applicable borough)
   - Provide basic event details: location, occupancy, date
   - Request guidance on specific TPA requirements
   - Confirm whether Fire Marshal presence required

2. **Formal Submission** (4-6 weeks before event):
   - Submit formal TPA application package to DOB
   - DOB will route to applicable FDNY division for review
   - Expect initial comments/questions within 1-2 weeks

3. **Revisions** (2-4 weeks before event):
   - Address FDNY/DOB comments
   - Resubmit revised floor plans and egress plans
   - Work with FDNY on occupant load adjustments if needed

4. **Final Inspection** (3-7 days before event):
   - FDNY inspects the space in its final condition
   - Confirms egress paths are clear and properly marked
   - Verifies occupant load controls in place
   - Confirms temporary lighting/signage installed
   - Issues final approval

### FDNY Requirements for TPA:

- **Emergency Egress**: All exits must be clear, unlocked, and properly marked
- **Occupant Load Control**: Doorstaffing or gating may be required to prevent overcrowding
- **Exit Signage**: Illuminated exit signs (or compliant temporary signage)
- **Emergency Lighting**: Path of egress must be lit; battery-backup lighting for assembly over 50 people [VERIFY occupancy threshold]
- **Fire Extinguishers**: Portable fire extinguishers based on occupancy and use (especially if food service)
- **Portable Toilets**: If building toilets inadequate for expected occupancy [VERIFY threshold]
- **No Blocked Exits**: Fire Marshal will cite any locked/blocked exits
- **Fire Watch**: For certain high-risk events (fireworks, special effects), Fire Marshal may require on-site presence

### FDNY Fire Marshal On-Site Presence:

Typically required for:
- Events over 500+ attendees [VERIFY threshold]
- Events with special effects, fireworks, or flame effects
- Events in non-traditional spaces (outdoor, temporary structures)
- Events with high-risk assembly configurations
- Events in buildings with known violations or history

**Cost:** Fire Marshal presence is typically a fee-based service ($250-$500+ per hour) [VERIFY fees]

## 8. Advance Filing Requirements - How Far Ahead?

### Minimum Filing Timeline:

| **Event Size** | **Minimum Filing Time** | **Recommended Filing Time** |
|---|---|---|
| **Small (under 100 people)** | 3-4 weeks | 6-8 weeks |
| **Medium (100-300 people)** | 4-6 weeks | 8-10 weeks |
| **Large (300-500 people)** | 6-8 weeks | 10-12 weeks |
| **Very Large (500+ people)** | 8-10 weeks | 12-14 weeks |
| **Complex events (multiple permits, outdoor, etc.)** | 10-12 weeks | 14-16 weeks |

### Critical Deadlines:

1. **Insurance Procurement**: Begin 8-10 weeks in advance (processing takes 2-3 weeks)
2. **Health Dept Coordination**: File 2-4 weeks before event if food service included
3. **FDNY Pre-Consultation**: Contact 8-10 weeks in advance
4. **DOB Application Submission**: 6-8 weeks before event (minimum)
5. **FDNY Inspection Scheduling**: 3-7 days before event
6. **All Permits Issued**: Must have by event date

### Why Advance Notice Matters:

- DOB and FDNY have varying review timelines depending on complexity
- Insurance brokers need time to issue certificates
- Building modifications (temporary ramps, signage, lighting) take 1-2 weeks to install
- Unforeseen issues or required revisions need buffer time
- Emergency/last-minute TPAs may face delays or denial

**Late Filing Warning:** Submitting TPA within 2 weeks of event date greatly increases risk of denial or approval delays.

### Penalties for Late/Non-Compliance:

**If TPA is filed too close to event date:**
- **0-2 weeks before event**: DOB may deny application or issue conditional approval requiring additional requirements
- **Days before event**: High likelihood of denial; insufficient time for FDNY inspection and approval

- Start insurance procurement immediately once event date confirmed
- Build in 2-week buffer for revisions/resubmissions
- Contact FDNY early for guidance on requirements

## 10. Duration Limits - How Long is a TPA Valid?

### TPA Validity Periods:

| **Duration of Event** | **TPA Validity** | **Examples** |
|---|---|---|
| **Single day/event** | TPA valid for calendar day of event | Wedding, conference day, opening |
| **Multi-day event (2-7 days)** | TPA valid for all event dates | 3-day festival, 2-day conference |
| **Weekly recurring (same day each week)** | TPA typically valid for defined period (e.g., 8 weeks) | Weekly pop-up market, seasonal Friday night event |
| **Long-term temporary (seasonal/indefinite)** | TPA valid for 6-12 months [VERIFY duration limit] | Seasonal pop-up retail, temporary outdoor venue |

### TPA Expiration:

- **TPA expires automatically** at the end of the approved event dates
- **No renewal needed** if event is one-time
- **If event extends beyond approved dates**, organizer must file amended TPA or request extension
- **Operating beyond TPA expiration** is a violation and carries penalties

### TPA Extension/Continuation:

If the event needs to extend or repeat:
- **Option 1**: File new TPA application (same process, shorter timeline if submitted 4+ weeks before new event)
- **Option 2**: Request extension at least 2 weeks before TPA expiration (faster process) [VERIFY]
- **Option 3**: Pursue permanent PACO if long-term assembly use intended

### Important: One-Time vs. Recurring Events

- **One-time TPA**: Simple 1-event approval
- **Recurring TPA** (e.g., every Friday for 8 weeks): Single TPA covering all dates; same floor plan applies
- **Open-ended recurring** (ongoing seasonal use): May trigger conversation about PACO instead of TPA [VERIFY]

## 11. Occupant Load and Egress Requirements Specific to Temporary Setups

### Occupant Load Calculation for TPA:

**Formula:**
```
Occupant Load = Total Floor Area ÷ Occupancy Load Factor (per Fire Code Table 1003.2.2.6)
```

### Common Occupancy Load Factors (per NYC Fire Code):

| **Type of Use** | **Occupancy Load Factor** | **Notes** |
|---|---|---|
| **Standing Assembly** | 7 sq ft per person | Concerts, receptions, exhibitions |
| **Seated Assembly** | 15-20 sq ft per person | Conferences, dining, theater seating |
| **Dining/Food Service** | 15 sq ft per person | Seated meal events |
| **Office/Retail** | 250 sq ft per person | If dual-use space |
| **Dance Floors** | 5 sq ft per person | High-density dancing |
| **Exhibition/Museum** | 20 sq ft per person | Walking exhibits, galleries |

**Example Calculation:**
- Event space: 3,000 sq ft
- Type: Standing reception
- Load factor: 7 sq ft/person
- **Max Occupancy = 3,000 ÷ 7 = ~428 people**

### Controlling Occupancy in TPA:

**Method 1: Doorstaffing**
- Staff at entrance counts attendees real-time
- Prevents entry once max occupancy reached
- Suitable for smaller events (under 300 people)

**Method 2: Pre-Ticketed Events**
- Fixed number of tickets issued
- Confirms exact occupancy
- Preferred by FDNY for larger events

**Method 3: Physical Barriers/Gating**
- Temporary barriers limit entry to defined areas
- Physical queue management
- Used for multi-space venues

**Method 4: Time-Slot Entry**
- Staggered entry times to manage peak occupancy
- E.g., "slots close at 300 attendees; next slot available at 10pm"

### Egress Requirements for Temporary Setups:

#### Exit Requirements:

**Minimum exits:** 2 exits required for occupancy over 49 people; 3+ exits for larger events [VERIFY thresholds]

**Exit door widths:**
- Assembly use: 44" minimum clear width [VERIFY]
- Must swing in direction of egress (outward from occupied space)
- Cannot swing into exit path

**Exit path minimum width:** 36-44" depending on occupancy [VERIFY]

#### Travel Distance:

**Maximum travel distance to exit:** 250 feet for assembly use [VERIFY distance]
- Measured from farthest occupied point to exit
- Applies in TPA scenarios

#### Stairway Requirements:

**If multi-floor event:**
- Handrails on both sides for stairs over 44" wide [VERIFY width]
- Maximum 7" riser height, minimum 11" tread depth [VERIFY dimensions]
- Landing widths match stairway width
- Adequate lighting (emergency lighting if occupancy over 50) [VERIFY threshold]

#### Emergency Lighting and Signage:

**For occupancy over 50 people [VERIFY threshold]:**
- Emergency lighting required in egress paths
- Can be temporary battery-powered fixtures
- Illumination level: 1 foot-candle minimum [VERIFY]
- Exit signs must be illuminated or glow-in-the-dark

**For smaller occupancies:**
- May use temporary reflective signage (with FDNY approval)

#### ADA Egress Requirements:

**Accessible routes:**
- At least 1 accessible exit route required
- 48" minimum width (36" minimum at doorways) [VERIFY]
- No steps; ramps (1:12 slope maximum) if level change [VERIFY]

**Accessible seating:**
- If event has fixed seating, 1-4% of seats must be accessible [VERIFY percentage]
- Wheelchair spaces, companion seating, accessible routes to seating

**Accessible restrooms:**
- If permanent building restrooms not accessible, portable ADA-compliant toilets may be required [VERIFY]

## 13. TPA Inspection Process

### FDNY Inspection Timeline and Schedule:

**Inspection occurs:** Typically 3-7 days before event date [VERIFY window]

**How to schedule:**
- FDNY will contact applicant after TPA approval-in-concept
- Inspection date/time coordinated with building owner and event organizer
- Usually 1-2 hour on-site visit
- Must have space accessible and in event-ready condition

### What FDNY Inspects:

**1. Egress Verification**
- All exits confirmed clear and unobstructed
- Exit doors functional and swing properly
- All exit routes marked with signage
- Emergency lighting installed (if required)
- Adequate stairway width and handrails confirmed

**2. Occupancy Control**
- Method for occupancy control confirmed (doorstaffing, ticketing, barriers, etc.)
- Staff briefed on occupant load limits
- Occupancy limit posted at entrance

**3. Fire Safety Equipment**
- Portable fire extinguishers present and accessible
- Fire Alarm/pull station access verified (if existing)
- Electrical outlets, extension cords, etc. reviewed for safety

**4. Accessibility Compliance**
- Accessible routes confirmed clear
- Accessible parking confirmed (if applicable)
- Accessible restroom facilities reviewed

**5. Special Use Features**
- Kitchen setup reviewed (if food service)
- Temporary structures inspected for stability
- Propane/special equipment reviewed if applicable

**6. Event Operations Review**
- Event organizer/operator briefed on occupancy limits and egress procedures
- Evacuation procedures discussed
- Any last-minute questions addressed

### Post-Inspection Actions:

**If Inspection PASSES:**
- FDNY issues approval letter
- TPA is finalized; event approved to proceed
- Organizer receives event permit

**If Inspection FAILS/Requires Changes:**
- FDNY issues deficiency list
- Organizer must correct issues within specified timeframe (typically 2-3 days) [VERIFY]
- Re-inspection may be required
- If issues cannot be corrected, event may be cancelled [VERIFY]

### Fire Marshal On-Site During Event:

**For large events (500+ people) [VERIFY threshold] or high-risk events:**
- FDNY may require Fire Marshal presence during event
- Fee-based service: $250-$500+ per hour [VERIFY]
- Fire Marshal ensures occupancy compliance, egress monitoring
- Fire Marshal authority to halt event if serious safety violation occurs

## 16. Common Reasons for TPA Denial

### Top Reasons for TPA Rejection or Conditional Approval:

**1. Inadequate Egress/Exit Capacity**
- Insufficient number of exits for occupancy
- Exits too narrow or obstructed
- Travel distance to exits exceeds code limits
- **Resolution:** Reduce occupancy or add temporary exit improvements

**2. Inaccurate/Missing Occupant Load Calculation**
- Calculation doesn't match Fire Code methodology
- Occupancy too high for space
- **Resolution:** Recalculate conservatively; reduce occupancy if needed

**3. Missing or Incomplete Floor Plans**
- Plans missing required details (exits, dimensions, occupancy layout)
- Plans not to scale or not professional grade
- Missing ADA accessibility information
- **Resolution:** Resubmit compliant, detailed floor plans

**4. Insurance Issues**
- Certificate missing non-cancellation clause
- DOB/FDNY not listed as additional insureds
- Coverage limits too low
- Certificate doesn't cover event dates
- **Resolution:** Obtain corrected certificate from insurance broker

**5. Building Owner Consent Not Obtained**
- No signed authorization from building owner
- Owner unwilling to authorize temporary assembly use
- **Resolution:** Obtain owner's written consent; may need to change venue if owner refuses

**6. Health Department Coordination Not Submitted** (for food events)
- No proof of food service permit
- TPA applicant failed to coordinate with Health Dept
- **Resolution:** File Health Dept permit; resubmit TPA with Health Dept approval

**7. Structural/Permanent Alterations Proposed**
- TPA cannot be used to justify permanent building changes
- Proposed changes would require full building permits
- **Resolution:** Remove permanent alteration requirements; use temporary solutions only

**8. ADA Non-Compliance**
- Event would exclude people with disabilities
- Insufficient accessible routes, parking, or facilities
- **Resolution:** Add temporary accessibility measures (ramps, portable toilets, etc.)

**9. Conflicting NYC Code Violations**
- Building has existing violations that prevent assembly use
- Code issues cannot be waived for TPA
- **Resolution:** Building owner must cure violations before TPA approval (or reduce occupancy below violation threshold) [VERIFY]

**10. Inadequate Timeline**
- TPA filed less than 3 weeks before event
- Insufficient time for review and inspection
- **Resolution:** Extend event date or accept risk of denial; plan earlier next time

**11. Exaggerated or Unclear Event Description**
- Applicant overstates expected occupancy
- Event type unclear or misrepresented
- **Resolution:** Provide clear, accurate, conservative event details

**12. Multiple Permits Not Coordinated**
- Outdoor event without Parks Dept approval
- Food event without Health Dept coordination
- Noise event without DEP permit
- **Resolution:** File all required permits simultaneously

## 17. Renewal/Extension of TPA

### When TPA Renewal is Needed:

**Scenario 1: Multi-date Event (Already Covered)**
- TPA issued for defined dates (e.g., Sept 1-3)
- No renewal needed if event ends on approved date
- Extension available if event extends beyond dates

**Scenario 2: Recurring Weekly Event**
- TPA issued for 8 weeks of repeating dates
- No renewal needed within that 8-week window
- If event wants to continue beyond 8 weeks, must file extension or new TPA

**Scenario 3: Event Wants to Extend**
- Originally approved for 1 day; now wants 2 additional dates
- Must file TPA extension or new TPA application

### TPA Extension Process:

**Timeline:** File extension request at least 2-3 weeks before original TPA expiration [VERIFY]

**Required Documents for Extension:**
- Original TPA approval letter
- Updated event details (new date(s), continued occupancy)
- Updated Certificate of Insurance (if dates extended beyond original insurance policy)
- Any updated floor plans (if event setup changes)

**Processing Time:** Typically 5-10 business days (faster than initial TPA) [VERIFY]

**Cost:** Lower fee than initial TPA [VERIFY amount]

**FDNY Inspection:**
- May not require new on-site inspection if setup unchanged
- May only require desk review [VERIFY]
- Or brief walk-through to confirm conditions [VERIFY]

### Alternative: New TPA Application

**If event significantly changes (occupancy increases, different venue, etc.):**
- File new TPA application instead of extension
- Process same as initial TPA (6-8 weeks)
- Same fees apply

### Upgrade to Permanent PACO (if venue becomes permanent):

**If temporary venue becomes semi-permanent:**
- 6+ month consecutive use → consider PACO instead
- PACO application process more complex (different requirements, higher cost)
- PACO application submitted separately to DOB, full Place of Assembly licensing process
- Once PACO issued, TPA no longer needed
