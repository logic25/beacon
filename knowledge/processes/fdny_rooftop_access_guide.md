# FDNY Rooftop Access Requirements Guide

## Overview

FDNY rooftop access requirements establish mandatory safety protocols and physical infrastructure for firefighters to safely access and operate on building rooftops during emergency situations. These requirements are codified in the New York City Fire Code and apply to both new construction and existing buildings undergoing modifications.

## When FDNY Rooftop Access Filing is Required

FDNY rooftop access filing and review is required in the following scenarios:

- **New Buildings**: All new construction projects
- **Major Alterations**: Alterations that affect roof structure, access points, or occupancy classification
- **Solar Panel Installations**: Rooftop solar installations subject to Fire Code Section 605
- **Building System Modifications**: Changes to mechanical, electrical, or structural systems on roofs
- **Occupancy Changes**: Changes in building use that affect fire safety classifications
- **Roof Deck Conversions**: Addition of rooftop gardens, seating areas, or other uses
- **Facade Alterations**: Changes that affect roof edge conditions or access routes

## FDNY Rooftop Access Requirements

### Bulkhead Door and Stairwell Access

- All roof access stairwells must terminate in a bulkhead structure
- Bulkhead doors must open outward and be at least 3 feet wide and 6 feet 8 inches tall
- Doors must be self-closing and self-locking
- Door hardware must be operable from both inside and outside the building
- Bulkhead structures must be fire-rated in accordance with Fire Code requirements

### Clear Access Paths

- A minimum 6-foot-wide clear path must be maintained from the bulkhead door to the roof perimeter
- Paths must be unobstructed by equipment, HVAC units, vents, or other building systems
- Equipment can be placed adjacent to access paths with proper clearance
- Access paths should not contain steps, ramps, or significant elevation changes
- Safety railings must be provided at roof edges (minimum 42 inches high)

### Roof Markings and Signage

- The access path from bulkhead to roof perimeter must be marked with high-visibility tape or paint
- Directional arrows must indicate the route to roof edges
- "FDNY ACCESS" signage must be posted at the bulkhead door (minimum 10" x 14")
- All markings must meet visibility standards and be maintained in good condition
- Markings should contrast with roof surface color

### Address Signage

- Building address numbers must be clearly visible on the roof
- Numbers must be at least 24 inches in height
- Numbers must be in a color that contrasts with the roof surface
- Placement should be near the bulkhead door or primary access point
- Address must be legible from helicopter view during emergency operations

### Additional Infrastructure Requirements

- Roof surface must be capable of supporting emergency personnel and equipment
- Roof hatches used for access must be at least 3 feet x 4 feet in dimension
- Rooftop equipment must be properly secured and labeled
- Electrical equipment on roofs must meet FDNY clearance requirements
- Combustible materials must not be stored on roofs

## Relationship to Fire Code

### Fire Code Section 504 - General Requirements

Fire Code Section 504 establishes general roof and roof access requirements, including:
- Roof load capacity standards
- Access opening dimensions and spacing
- Roof covering and fire rating requirements
- Maintenance and inspection protocols

### Fire Code Section 605 - Solar Energy Systems

Section 605 applies specific requirements to rooftop solar installations:
- Solar panels must not obstruct FDNY roof access requirements
- Setbacks from roof edges must be maintained for access
- Minimum 5-foot setback from parapet edges (typical requirement)
- Walkways around solar array perimeters for maintenance and emergency access
- Electrical disconnects must be accessible and clearly marked
- FDNY coordination is required during design phase

### Fire Code Compliance

All rooftop modifications must comply with:
- NYC Fire Code Chapter 5 (Fire-Resistance-Rated Construction)
- NYC Fire Code Chapter 6 (Building Separation and Openings)
- NYC Building Code coordination for structural and safety requirements

## FDNY Filing Process

### Step 1: Initial Consultation

- Contact FDNY Bureau of Fire Prevention (BFP) for technical guidance
- Clarify specific requirements for your building type and roof configuration
- Obtain preliminary design guidance

### Step 2: Plans Preparation

- Prepare detailed roof plans showing:
  - Bulkhead location and dimensions
  - Access path layout with 6-foot minimum width
  - Clear zones for equipment and systems
  - Marking and signage locations
  - Address number placement
  - Roof structural elements and load ratings

### Step 3: Submission to FDNY

- Submit plans through DOB's Department of Buildings (for projects requiring permits)
- Or submit directly to FDNY Bureau of Fire Prevention for standalone rooftop approvals
- Include architect certification of Fire Code compliance
- Provide professional engineer stamp for structural elements if applicable

### Step 4: FDNY Review

- FDNY reviews plans for compliance with Fire Code Section 504
- Comments or deficiency notices issued within [VERIFY] 5-10 business days
- Resubmit revised plans if deficiencies are noted

### Step 5: Approval and Issuance

- FDNY issues approval letter upon compliance
- Letter confirms Fire Code Section 504 compliance
- Approval letter required before DOB permit issuance (for new construction)
- Certificate of Occupancy requires final roof access verification

## Required Plans and Documents

### Architectural Plans

- Roof plan at 1/8" = 1' scale minimum showing:
  - Bulkhead door opening dimensions and swing direction
  - Access path marked with hatching or color
  - Equipment locations and clearances
  - Perimeter conditions and railings
  - Address number placement and size

### Signage and Marking Details

- Detail drawings of:
  - "FDNY ACCESS" signage (size, material, mounting)
  - Directional arrows and stripe markings
  - Color specifications and contrast ratios
  - Address number sizing and placement

### Structural Documentation

- Roof loading calculations (live load capacity)
- Structural drawings for bulkhead and access frame
- Floor plans showing stairwell locations and dimensions
- Section details of bulkhead structure

### Fire Safety Certification

- Architect letter certifying Fire Code Section 504 compliance
- List of specific code requirements met
- Warranty or maintenance plan documentation (if required)

### For Solar Projects (Section 605)

- Solar panel layout and setback calculations
- Electrical system schematic with FDNY disconnects
- Structural attachments and wind load analysis
- Maintenance access walkway details

## Common Deficiencies

### Access Path Issues

- Access path width less than 6 feet
- HVAC units or other equipment blocking path
- Loose cables or piping crossing access path
- Marked path not visible or faded
- Path blocked by roof hatches or skylights

### Bulkhead Door Problems

- Door width less than 3 feet or height less than 6'8"
- Non-self-closing or non-self-locking doors
- Door handles not operable from exterior
- Rusted or corroded hardware
- Interior stairs too narrow or steep for emergency personnel

### Signage and Marking Deficiencies

- Missing or illegible "FDNY ACCESS" signage
- Address numbers too small (less than 24 inches)
- Poor contrast between markings and roof surface
- Damaged or faded directional arrows
- Address numbers placed in obscured location

### Roof Edge and Safety Issues

- Railings below 42 inches or non-code compliant
- Unsecured or improperly labeled rooftop equipment
- Combustible materials or debris on roof
- Deteriorated roof surface affecting foot traffic
- Unclear perimeter conditions

### Solar Installation Deficiencies

- Solar panels encroaching on required setbacks
- Missing or inaccessible electrical disconnects
- Inadequate maintenance walkways around arrays
- Structural attachments not properly engineered
- Missing FDNY coordination documentation

## Best Practices for Expediting FDNY Approval

- Engage FDNY early in design phase for preliminary guidance
- Provide complete, high-quality plans in initial submission
- Use FDNY-approved consultants familiar with Section 504 requirements
- Include detailed narrative explaining compliance approach
- Request preliminary meeting with FDNY reviewer if configuration is complex
- Maintain clear communication with FDNY throughout review
- Have revised plans ready immediately upon receiving deficiency comments
- Consider professional expediter for complex projects with multiple agencies
