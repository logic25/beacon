# FDNY Filing and Coordination Guide

## Overview

FDNY (Fire Department of New York) operates a separate filing and inspection process parallel to DOB (Department of Buildings) requirements. Many projects require **both DOB and FDNY approvals** before final Certificate of Occupancy can be issued. Understanding FDNY's jurisdiction and timeline is critical for expediting construction projects involving fire protection systems.

The FDNY Bureau of Fire Prevention handles all fire protection system filings. Unlike DOB's online platforms, FDNY filings involve manual submission and coordination with fire marshals and inspectors.

---

## When FDNY Filing Is Required

FDNY filings are mandatory for the following work:

- **Fire alarm system** installation or modification
- **Sprinkler system** installation or modification
- **Standpipe system** installation or modification
- **Kitchen hood suppression systems** (Ansul systems and similar)
- **Gas piping** in certain scenarios (fuel lines for emergency generators, bulk storage)
- **Fire suppression systems** (pre-action, dry-pipe, specialty systems)
- **Storage of hazardous materials** (requires Plan Review for storage facilities)
- **Firehouse access and parking modifications** (if applicable to project location)

**Critical:** Even if work appears minor, when it touches fire protection systems, FDNY filing is required. Omitting an FDNY filing can result in work stoppage and fines.

## FDNY Filing Process

### Step-by-Step Filing Workflow

1. **Plans Preparation**
   - Licensed professional (PE, architect, fire protection engineer) prepares FDNY-compliant drawings
   - Plans must include system schematics, riser diagrams, calculations, equipment specifications
   - All stamped and signed per FDNY requirements

2. **Submission to FDNY Bureau of Fire Prevention**
   - File submitted to FDNY at 9 MetroTech Center, Brooklyn, or via authorized third-party expediter
   - Paper submission still primary method; some items may be submitted electronically
   - Application includes: Application form, plans, calculations, equipment specifications

3. **FDNY Plan Review**

   - FDNY fire protection engineers review for code compliance
   - Review examines: System adequacy, calculations, equipment listing, coordination with building systems

4. **Approval or Objections**
   - FDNY issues approval (usually marked "No Objection" or "Approved")
   - OR issues objections (detailed comments requiring modifications)
   - If objections: Revisions prepared, resubmitted; review cycle repeats

5. **Installation by Licensed Installer**
   - Only licensed, FDNY-certified installers may install fire protection systems
   - Installation must follow FDNY-approved plans exactly
   - Changes during installation require FDNY approval

6. **FDNY Inspection**
   - FDNY fire marshal or inspector performs on-site inspection after installation
   - Verifies compliance with approved plans, code requirements, equipment listing
   - Tests system operation (if applicable)

7. **FDNY Sign-Off and Acceptance**
   - Upon passing inspection, FDNY issues "Acceptance" or "Letter of Acceptance"
   - FP Number stamped on acceptance document
   - This document required by DOB for Certificate of Occupancy

## FP Number

**FP Number** = FDNY filing number assigned upon application submission.

- Different from DOB Job Number
- Format: Variable; may be alphanumeric
- Must be referenced in all correspondence with FDNY
- Required in DOB CO/TCO application as evidence of FDNY coordination
- Appears on final FDNY acceptance letter

## FDNY Violations

### How FDNY Violations Are Issued

- Issued by FDNY fire marshals during inspections or compliance sweeps
- May be issued for non-compliance with approved plans, code violations, system failure
- Violations are **NOT** processed through OATH/ECB (unlike DOB violations)
- Must be resolved directly with FDNY

## Fire Alarm System Requirements

### When Is Fire Alarm Required?

The NYC Building Code (2022) requires fire alarm systems in:

- **Assembly occupancies** (theaters, conference rooms, nightclubs, etc.)
- **Educational occupancies** (schools, training facilities)
- **High-rise buildings** (75+ feet in height)
- **Residential buildings** with certain configurations (Group R-2 high-rise buildings, hotels)
- **Buildings with certain hazardous materials**

### Existing Buildings and Fire Alarm

Fire alarm systems are often required even in sprinklered buildings if the occupancy classification requires one. Sprinklers do not eliminate fire alarm requirements.

### Filing Fire Alarm with FDNY

- Fire alarm design and approval handled by FDNY
- Licensed alarm company handles installation and monitoring
- System must be monitored by approved monitoring station (UL-listed)
- FDNY issues acceptance before DOB will issue CO/TCO

## Ansul/Kitchen Suppression Systems

### Requirement

- **Mandatory** for all commercial cooking operations (restaurants, commissaries, institutional kitchens)
- Covers cooking surfaces, fryers, ranges, grills

### FDNY Filing for Ansul

- Ansul/suppression system design filed with FDNY **separately** from DOB application
- Licensed fire protection contractor handles design and installation
- Installation must follow FDNY-approved design
- FDNY inspection and acceptance required before DOB CO

## Coordination Between FDNY and DOB

### DOB Requirement for FDNY Sign-Off

- DOB will **not** issue Certificate of Occupancy (CO) or Temporary Certificate (TCO) without evidence of FDNY approval
- For fire protection work: FP Number and FDNY Acceptance letter required in DOB application
- Fire alarm acceptance specifically needed before CO issuance

### Timeline Coordination

- FDNY approval should be obtained **before or concurrent with** DOB final approval
- Delay in FDNY approval directly delays DOB CO issuance
- Plan coordination with both FDNY and DOB to avoid sequential delays
