# NB (New Building) Filing Guide

## Overview

NB stands for **New Building** — the filing designation for entirely new construction on a vacant lot, or construction following full demolition of an existing structure. NB filings are the most comprehensive DOB applications, requiring full coordination of architecture, structural engineering, MEP design, and code compliance across all building systems.

### What Qualifies as NB

NB filing is required when:

- **Constructing a new building from ground up** on a vacant lot
- **Rebuilding after full demolition** of an existing structure
- **New construction that includes all trades and major systems** — architecture, structural, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, fire protection

NB does NOT apply to:
- Additions to existing buildings (use ALT-1)
- Interior renovations or alterations (use ALT-1 or ALT-2)
- Demolition alone (separate DM demolition permit required first)

---

## NB vs. ALT-1: Key Distinction

| Aspect | NB (New Building) | ALT-1 (Alteration) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Scope | Entirely new construction | Work on existing structure |
| Existing building | None (vacant lot or post-demolition) | Building exists; modifications made |
| Plans required | Full architectural, structural, MEP, site plans | Modified plans showing proposed changes |
| Zoning review | Full zoning analysis; FAR, height, setback, open space | Zoning implications of occupancy change |
| Foundation work | Full foundation design required | Rare unless major structural work |
| Timeline | Longer (comprehensive review) | Moderate to long depending on scope |

## Before NB Filing: Demolition Sequence

If an existing building occupies the site, demolition must occur first.

### DM (Demolition) Permit Process

1. **File DM Application** — Separate from NB; permits full or partial building demolition
2. **DM Plan Review** — DOB examines demolition sequencing, utilities disconnection, waste management
3. **DM Permit Issuance** — Once approved
4. **Demolition Execution** — Building demolished per approved plan; completion inspected
5. **Demolition Completion Certificate** — Issued once site cleared
6. **File NB** — After site is vacant and cleared, proceed with NB filing for new construction

**Timeline:** Demolition itself can take months depending on building size. Plan NB filing for after DM completion.

## Zoning Compliance and Analysis

NB requires full zoning verification. Your zoning analysis must address:

### Basic Zoning Parameters

| Parameter | What It Means | Common Issues |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Zoning District** | Zoning designation (e.g., R7-2, C6-4M) | Wrong lot classification; map outdated |
| **Use Group** | Building occupancy classification (residential, office, retail, etc.) | Proposed use not allowed in district |
| **FAR (Floor Area Ratio)** | Total floor area ÷ lot area; max allowed by zone | Building too large; exceeds FAR cap |
| **Height Limit** | Max building height (ft or stories); zoning restrictions | Building taller than permitted |
| **Setbacks** | Distances from lot lines required (e.g., 25 ft from front) | Building too close to street or neighbor |
| **Lot Coverage** | % of lot that building can cover | Building footprint exceeds allowance |
| **Open Space** | Required open area/yard; minimum area, dimensions | Insufficient yard or plaza |
| **Parking** | # of parking spaces required based on use/occupancy | Deficit spaces; waiver needed if not provided |

### Common Zoning Objections

- Use group not permitted in district → Seek variance or change use
- FAR exceeded → Reduce floor area
- Height exceeds limit → Reduce stories or height
- Setback violation → Shift building footprint
- Parking deficit → Add spaces or seek waiver
- Open space insufficient → Expand yard/plaza area

**Recommendation:** Perform zoning analysis early in design, before finalizing plans. Catching zoning issues after DOB submission costs time and design revisions.

## Construction Sequencing and Phasing

NB projects typically proceed in phases, each requiring specific inspections and permits:

### Phase 1: Foundation and Excavation
- Geotechnical investigations
- Foundation permits and inspections
- Caisson/pile testing (if applicable)
- Concrete curing and strength verification

### Phase 2: Superstructure (Structural Frame)
- Steel or concrete structural system
- Structural steel inspections and certifications
- Fireproofing application and inspection
- Floor decking installation

### Phase 3: Envelope (Weather Protection)
- Exterior walls (masonry, metal, curtain wall)
- Windows and doors
- Roof system
- Waterproofing

### Phase 4: Interior Build-Out (Fit-Out)
- Walls and partitions
- Mechanical rough-in (HVAC ducts, piping)
- Electrical rough-in (circuits, conduit)
- Plumbing rough-in (water, waste lines)

### Phase 5: Systems & Finishes
- Final mechanical connections
- Final electrical connections
- HVAC balancing and startup
- Plumbing testing and flushing
- Fire alarm and sprinkler commissioning

### Phase 6: Final Inspections and CO
- Final building inspection
- All system testing and startup
- Certificate of Occupancy issuance

Each phase has inspections and milestones; timely scheduling keeps project on track.

## Key Inspections During NB Construction

### Structural Inspections
- **Soils/Foundation:** Bearing soil verification, pile/caisson load testing
- **Concrete:** Pour inspections, strength testing (cylinders), structural certification
- **Structural Steel:** Connection inspections, certified mill test reports, fireproofing coverage

### Building Envelope & Waterproofing
- **Foundation Waterproofing:** Before backfill
- **Exterior Walls:** Masonry/metal studs installation, flashing
- **Roofing:** Membrane installation, drainage verification
- **Window Installation:** Sealant and flashing verification

### MEP Inspections
- **Mechanical:** Ductwork, equipment installation, control systems, balancing
- **Electrical:** Panel installation, circuit verification, emergency power testing
- **Plumbing:** Rough-in inspection, pressure testing, final connections
- **Fire Protection:** Sprinkler installation and flow testing, fire alarm wiring and programming
- **Special Systems:** Elevators, HVAC startup, standpipe pressure testing

### Safety Systems
- **Fire Alarm:** System wiring, device placement, control panel programming
- **Sprinkler System:** Head spacing, pressure testing, riser inspection
- **Emergency Lighting:** Coverage and battery backup verification
- **Exit Signage:** Installation and illumination

### Final Inspection
Comprehensive final inspection confirming all work complete, all inspections passed, no outstanding violations.

## Special Situations

### Historic District Designation

If lot is in a **historic district** or building is **landmark-designated**:
- Architectural Commission (AC) or Landmarks Preservation Commission (LPC) review required
- Design must be approved by LPC before DOB filing
- Adds 4–12 weeks to timeline; requires architect coordination

### Community Board (CB) Involvement

Some projects trigger **Community Board review** or ULURP (Uniform Land Use Review Procedure):
- Zoning variance or use variance → CB hearing required
- Change to public land or major public impact → ULURP review
- Timeline: 4–8 weeks for CB review

### City Planning Review

Large or complex projects may require **City Planning review** (BSA, CPC, etc.):
- Variance applications → City Planning Board review
- Major developments → Coordination with planning agencies

## Project Management: Keeping NB Moving

- **Assign a Point Person:** Single project coordinator for all DOB communication
- **Preemptive DOB Meetings:** Schedule pre-filing meetings with DOB examiners to identify likely objections
- **Plan Examiner Assignment:** Once assigned, establish regular communication; response to objections within 1–2 weeks (faster = better)
- **Parallel Permitting:** File mechanical, electrical, plumbing permits in parallel if possible to avoid sequential delays
- **Inspection Scheduling:** Book inspections in advance; have all work ready for inspector arrival
- **Documentation:** Keep detailed logs of all submittals, objections, responses, inspections; submit evidence of compliance
