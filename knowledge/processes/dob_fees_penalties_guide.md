# DOB Fees, Penalties, and Payment Guide

**Last Updated:** February 2026
**Audience:** GLE Expediters, Project Managers, Finance Team
**Purpose:** Reference guide for all DOB filing fees, permit fees, penalties, and payment procedures.

---

## Overview

DOB fees and penalties are governed by **NYC Administrative Code Title 1, Chapter 3** and **DOB Rules Chapter 101**. Fees are revised annually (typically in March). This guide reflects 2026 rates; verify all amounts before filing.

**Key distinction:**
- **Filing fees:** One-time charge to submit application
- **Permit fees:** Based on cost of work; enables actual construction
- **Inspection fees:** Charged for DOB inspections (some districts)
- **Penalties:** Civil violations; paid to DOB or adjudicated at ECB/OATH

## 5. Civil Penalties: Payment and Procedures

### What Creates a Civil Penalty?

**Common violations leading to penalties:**
- Working without a permit
- Violating DOB notice of violation
- Failure to maintain construction site safety
- Illegal occupancy
- Facade/structural danger
- Environmental violations

### How Penalties Are Assessed

1. **DOB Inspector:** Issues violation notice (orange placard or letter)
2. **Amount:** Ranges from $250 to $25,000+ depending on severity
3. **Appeal deadline:** Typically 30 days

### Payment Methods

**Option 1: Direct payment to DOB**
- **Manhattan office:** [VERIFY: Address and procedure]
- **Payment method:** In-person check/money order, wire transfer, or credit card [VERIFY: Which methods accepted in 2026?]
- **Reduced penalty consideration:** Must request in writing with explanation; DOB may reduce by 10–25%

**Option 2: Pay through ECB (Environmental Control Board)**
- **What is ECB?** Administrative tribunal that hears DOB violation disputes
- **When to use:** If violation is contested or penalty seems incorrect
- **Online portal:** [VERIFY: URL and access procedure]
- **Payment online:** Yes, if violation confirmed/accepted

**Option 3: OATH (Office of Administrative Trials & Hearings)**
- **Used for:** Complex disputes, significant penalties
- **Hearing process:** Expediter testifies, DOB presents evidence
- **Timeline:** 30–90 days

## 10. Fee Payment Procedures & Best Practices

### Payment Methods

**Accepted by DOB:**
- Check/money order (mail or in-person)
- Wire transfer (business accounts)
- Credit card (online through DOB eBiz system)
- ACH transfer (for large projects)

[VERIFY: Which methods are active in 2026? Any changes?]

### eBiz Payment Portal

**What is eBiz?** DOB's online portal for filing, payments, and status tracking.

**How to use:**
1. Create account (or use existing)
2. Select application or violation
3. Generate payment link
4. Pay by credit/debit card
5. Receipt emailed immediately

**Advantages:**
- No processing delays
- Real-time confirmation
- Can pay 24/7
- Card receipts for accounting

**Disadvantage:** Credit card fees apply (typically 2–3%) [VERIFY]

### Mail-In Payment
