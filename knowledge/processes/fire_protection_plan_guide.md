# Fire Protection Plan Guide

## Overview

A Fire Protection Plan (FPP) is a comprehensive document that outlines how a building and its occupants will prevent, respond to, and manage fire emergencies. FPPs are required for certain building types and occupancies and must be submitted to and approved by the FDNY's Bureau of Fire Prevention before a Certificate of Occupancy (CO) can be issued.

## When a Fire Protection Plan is Required

Fire Protection Plans are mandated for:

- **High-rise buildings** (buildings over 12 stories)
- **Buildings with occupancy over 500 persons** in any floor
- **Places of assembly** (theaters, nightclubs, concert halls, Houses of Worship with assembly capacity)
- **Hotels and hostels**
- **Hospitals and health care facilities**
- **Educational institutions** (schools, colleges, universities)
- **High-hazard occupancies** (manufacturing, storage, laboratories)
- **Buildings with special hazards** (hazardous materials storage, flammable/combustible use)
- **Buildings with protected premises** (automatic sprinkler systems exceeding certain thresholds)

Buildings not meeting these criteria may still require a Basic Fire Safety Plan or an abbreviated FPP depending on occupancy and DOB requirements.

## Relationship to FDNY Bureau of Fire Prevention

The FDNY's Bureau of Fire Prevention (BFP) is the primary regulatory authority for FPPs in New York City. Key relationships include:

- **Jurisdiction**: The BFP reviews and approves all FPPs prior to issuance of a CO or TCO
- **Consultation**: The expeditor should consult with FDNY BFP early in the design process to understand specific requirements for the building type and occupancy
- **Plan Review**: Formal FPP submission triggers a plan review by FDNY BFP, which may require revisions before approval
- **Enforcement**: Once approved, the FDNY has ongoing authority to enforce FPP compliance through inspections and violations

## Required Components of a Fire Protection Plan

A comprehensive FPP must include:

### 1. Fire Safety Director Information
- Name, title, and qualifications of the designated Fire Safety Director (FSD)
- Certification that the FSD holds a valid **NYC Fire Safety Director Certificate** issued by the FDNY
- FSD responsibilities (on-site presence, training, drills, daily duties)
- [VERIFY] Requirement for 24/7 on-site FSD presence for certain building types

### 2. Evacuation Procedures
- Detailed written procedures for orderly evacuation of the building
- Identification of evacuation routes and assembly points
- Special procedures for occupants with mobility challenges
- Procedures for persons unable to self-evacuate
- Communication protocols during evacuation
- Procedures for accountability of occupants post-evacuation

### 3. Fire Drills and Training
- Frequency of fire drills (typically quarterly or as required by FDNY)
- Procedures for conducting drills
- Attendance tracking and documentation
- Employee training on fire safety procedures and use of fire extinguishers
- Tenant and occupant notification of drill schedules

### 4. Fire Command Station (FCS)
- Location and design of the Fire Command Station
- Equipment required in the FCS (building plans, emergency contact info, FDNY communication systems)
- Procedures for staffing and operating the FCS during emergencies
- Integration with building security and management systems

### 5. Building Safety Systems
- Description of all fire safety systems (sprinklers, standpipes, fire alarms, emergency lighting, etc.)
- Maintenance schedules and responsibility assignments
- Testing and inspection protocols
- Procedures for out-of-service system notifications

### 6. Emergency Procedures
- Procedures for reporting fires and emergencies
- Procedures for notifying tenants and occupants of emergencies
- Procedures for cooperating with FDNY and emergency responders
- Procedures for post-fire investigations and incident reporting

### 7. Tenant/Occupant Information
- List of tenants and principal occupants
- Emergency contact information
- Hazardous materials identification and storage locations
- Special occupancy information (e.g., manufacturing processes, chemical storage)

## Building Types Requiring Fire Protection Plans

### High-Rise Buildings
Buildings exceeding 12 stories require comprehensive FPPs with stringent requirements including on-site Fire Safety Director, advanced fire command station capabilities, and frequent drills.

### Places of Assembly
Theaters, concert halls, nightclubs, and Houses of Worship with occupancy exceeding [VERIFY] require detailed evacuation procedures and trained ushers/monitors.

### Hotels and Hostels
Guest occupancy regulations require specific housekeeping fire safety procedures, staff training, and guest notification procedures.

### Healthcare Facilities
Hospitals and nursing homes require specialized FPPs addressing patient evacuation, including mobility-limited occupants, and coordination with medical operations.

### Educational Institutions
Schools and universities require age-appropriate training procedures, regular drills, and coordination with on-site staff.

## Required Plans and Documents

Coordinate with DOB and FDNY regarding supporting documentation:

- **Building Floor Plans** with exits, staircases, fire command station, and emergency equipment clearly marked
- **Fire Safety System Plans** (sprinkler, standpipe, fire alarm layout)
- **Tenant Directory** with emergency contacts
- **Fire Safety Director Certificate** (original or certified copy)
- **Certification of Occupancy Readiness** (confirming all fire systems operational)
- **Emergency Contact Procedure** document (911 procedures, FDNY notification, building management notification)

## Coordination with DOB (Fire Alarm, Sprinkler, Standpipe)

The FPP must coordinate with several DOB-regulated systems:

### Fire Alarm Systems
- FPP must reference the Fire Alarm system and its approval status
- DOB will not issue a CO until fire alarm is accepted and approved by FDNY
- FPP should outline procedures for fire alarm monitoring and response
- [VERIFY] FP (Fire Protection) number from FDNY must be obtained before DOB final inspection

### Sprinkler Systems
- FPP must document sprinkler system installation and maintenance
- Sprinkler certificate of compliance must be provided to DOB
- FPP should identify sprinkler coverage areas and any protected versus unprotected zones

### Standpipe Systems
- For buildings over 12 stories, standpipe systems are required per Building Code
- FPP must document standpipe location, valve access, and testing procedures
- DOB inspection and FDNY sign-off required before final acceptance

## Annual Updates and Compliance

### Annual Review and Update
- FPP should be reviewed annually by the building owner/manager
- Changes to occupancy, systems, or staffing require FPP revisions
- Major modifications may require FDNY re-approval

### Ongoing Compliance Obligations
- Fire Safety Director must maintain current certification (requires annual [VERIFY] continuing education)
- Fire drills must be conducted quarterly and documented
- All fire safety systems must be maintained and tested per code requirements
- Building management must keep records of inspections, testing, and drills
- FDNY may conduct inspections to verify FPP compliance

### Violation Response
- Failure to maintain an approved FPP is a violation of NYC Fire Code
- FDNY issues violations (ECB violations) for FPP non-compliance
- Violations must be corrected and certified within specified timeframe [VERIFY]

## Fire Safety Director Certification

### Qualifications and Certification
- Any person designated as Fire Safety Director must hold an NYC Fire Safety Director Certificate
- Certificate is issued by FDNY upon passing the Fire Safety Director Examination
- Exam covers NYC Fire Code, building systems, evacuation procedures, and fire safety management
- [VERIFY] Certification valid for 3 years; requires renewal and continuing education

### Training and Ongoing Requirements
- Fire Safety Director must complete annual continuing education (typically [VERIFY] hours)
- Must maintain knowledge of building-specific fire safety systems and procedures
- Should attend FDNY training updates on code changes and best practices
- Building owner is responsible for ensuring FSD maintains current certification

### On-Site Responsibilities
- High-rise buildings typically require 24/7 on-site Fire Safety Director presence [VERIFY]
- FSD responsible for conducting and documenting fire drills
- FSD coordinates with building management on system maintenance and compliance
- FSD serves as primary liaison with FDNY during emergency situations

## Important Notes for Expeditors

1. Start FPP development early—it can be a critical path item for CO issuance
2. Engage FDNY BFP consultation before finalizing FPP to avoid major revisions
3. Ensure Fire Safety Director certification timeline is coordinated with project schedule
4. Coordinate closely with building systems contractors regarding documentation requirements
5. Plan for potential revision rounds with FDNY—initial approval is rarely granted without deficiencies
6. Maintain copies of all approvals and certifications for DOB coordination
7. Communicate with building management early regarding post-occupancy FPP maintenance obligations
