# DOB Inspections and Sign-Off Guide

## Overview

DOB inspections are the verification mechanism by which the Department of Buildings confirms that work on a construction or alteration project meets code requirements and matches approved plans. Inspections are required at various stages of construction and for specific compliance areas. Once an inspection is completed and signed off, it enters the QA (Quality Assurance) review process before the sign-off is recorded in the system.

This guide covers inspection types, scheduling, sign-off procedures, and common issues encountered during the inspection and QA process.

---

## DOB Inspection Types

### Progress Inspections
Conducted during active construction to verify that work in progress complies with approved plans and building code. Progress inspections may be scheduled for:
- Foundation and concrete work
- Structural framing
- Mechanical, electrical, plumbing rough-in
- Fireproofing
- Any other phase-specific work

**Requirement:** Varies by project scope. Some jobs require no progress inspections; others require multiple.

### Special Inspections
Third-party certified inspectors conduct specialized inspections for specific work types, as mandated by NYC Building Code:
- **Structural Steel:** Certified special inspector verifies structural steel fabrication, connections, and installation
- **Reinforced Concrete:** Special inspector verifies concrete testing, rebar placement, form work, and curing
- **Fireproofing:** Special inspector verifies fireproofing material application to structural elements
- **Sprinkler Systems:** Certified sprinkler inspector verifies system design and installation per NFPA 13
- **Fire Alarm Systems:** Licensed fire alarm contractor/inspector verifies system installation and testing
- **Boiler Installation:** Licensed boiler inspector verifies boiler testing and certification
- **Elevator Installation:** Licensed elevator inspector verifies elevator equipment and testing

**Requirement:** Mandatory for projects involving these work types. Special inspectors must be DOB-approved.

### Final Inspection
The comprehensive final inspection conducted by DOB just before CO issuance. Inspector verifies:
- All construction work complete per plans
- All prior inspections signed off
- All required certificates and approvals on file
- No outstanding code violations
- Building ready for occupancy

**Requirement:** Mandatory on all projects seeking CO or final LOC.

## QA (Quality Assurance) Process

After an inspector completes an inspection and submits the results in the DOB system, the inspection report enters the **QA review phase**:

### QA Process Steps

1. **Inspection Submitted:** Inspector uploads inspection results, photos, and any deficiency notes to Build or BIS
2. **QA Review:** DOB QA examiner reviews the inspection report for completeness, accuracy, and compliance with requirements
3. **QA Pass:** Inspection report is accepted; sign-off is recorded in the system and becomes active
4. **QA Fail:** Inspection report is rejected; inspector must resubmit with corrections or address deficiencies

## Preparing for Inspection

### Before Inspector Arrival

**Site Readiness:**
- All work in inspection scope must be complete or clearly delineated
- Areas must be safe and accessible; no active hazards
- Temporary barriers, if needed, must clearly mark inspection zones
- Lighting must be adequate (especially for concealed areas)
- Pathways must be clear for inspector access

**Documentation Ready:**
- Approved plans on site (digital or printed)
- Material test reports and certificates (concrete, steel, etc.)
- Equipment certifications (fire alarm, sprinkler, elevator)
- Affidavits and owner authorizations
- Any amendments or RFI approvals affecting the scope

**Personnel on Site:**
- Project manager or superintendent
- Trade-specific foreman (if applicable to inspection type)
- Licensed professional (architect or engineer) if signature verification needed

## During Inspection

### Inspector Communication
- Be honest about existing conditions and any deviations from plans
- Point out completed work relevant to the inspection scope
- Provide all documentation upon request
- Do not conceal or hide areas under inspection
- Note any questions or concerns the inspector raises

### Inspection Not Completed
If the inspector indicates work is not ready:
- Ask specifically what items must be corrected
- Request written deficiency list if possible
- Ask about timeline for reinspection
- Do not assume inspection will be re-signed without correction

### Inspection Failed
If the inspector fails the inspection (indicates code violations or non-compliance):
- Get detailed deficiency description
- Photograph inspector notes or request written report
- Correct deficiencies and schedule reinspection
- Do not proceed with downstream work until reinspection passes

## Sign-Off Recording Process

### After Inspection Completion

1. **Inspector uploads results** to Build/BIS system with photos, notes, and pass/fail determination
2. **System notifies applicant** of inspection status
3. **QA review begins** (see QA Process section above)
4. **If QA passes:** Sign-off is automatically recorded in system and becomes visible in project dashboard
5. **If QA fails:** Inspection is rejected; inspector must resubmit with corrections

### Monitoring Sign-Off Status
- Log into Build/BIS regularly to track inspection status
- If sign-off shows "Pending" longer than expected, contact DOB
- If status shows "Failed" or "Rejected," determine reason immediately and resubmit

## Special Inspection Agencies

Special inspections (structural steel, concrete, fireproofing, sprinkler, fire alarm, boiler, elevator) must be conducted by **DOB-approved agencies or licensed professionals**. The contractor typically hires these agencies, but the applicant of record is responsible for ensuring they are qualified and DOB-approved.

### Requirements for Special Inspectors
- Licensed or certified in their field (per NYC Building Code)
- Current DOB approval
- Current insurance and bonding
- Ability to perform inspections per code standards

## Energy Compliance (EN / RCP Process)

### EN Sheets (Energy Compliance Documentation)

**EN1, EN2, and other energy documents** are technical compliance documents that verify the building meets NYS Energy Code. These must be filed and signed by a **Registered Code Professional (RCP)** or licensed MEP engineer qualified in energy code.

### EN2: Final Energy Compliance Sheet
The critical final energy document. Must be signed by RCP before TR8 (final energy inspection) can be scheduled and passed. EN2 confirms:
- Building envelope thermal performance
- HVAC system efficiency
- Lighting power density compliance
- All efficiency requirements met per NYS Energy Code

### Coordinating FDNY with DOB Inspections

**Fire Alarm and Sprinkler Systems:** FDNY conducts its own acceptance tests for fire alarm and sprinkler systems, separate from DOB inspections. Both FDNY and DOB approval are typically required for CO.

**Coordination Timeline:**
- Schedule FDNY tests early; FDNY moves slower than DOB
- FDNY acceptance letters must be obtained and uploaded to Build/BIS
- Do not schedule final DOB inspection until FDNY sign-offs are secure

### Best Practices
- **Start inspection coordination early:** Do not wait until final phase; schedule TR and special inspections as work progresses
- **Keep detailed records:** Document all inspection requests, results, and deficiencies in a centralized log
- **Assign one point person:** Have a single project team member manage all inspection scheduling and follow-up
- **Proactive deficiency correction:** Address deficiencies immediately rather than waiting for reinspection scheduling
- **Stay in contact with inspectors:** If an inspector is frequently on-site, maintain good communication to understand expectations
