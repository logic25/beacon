## 1. Asbestos Coordination

### Overview
Asbestos management is required for pre-1985 buildings in NYC. Coordination involves DOB and DEP oversight, with different forms and timelines based on project scope and abatement needs.

### When Asbestos Survey is Required
- **All buildings constructed before 1985** - mandatory survey or presumption of asbestos
- Interior gut renovations (>25% of interior floor area)
- Exterior work affecting potentially ACM materials
- Mechanical system work (pipes, boiler insulation)
- Roofing projects on older buildings
- Demolition projects (DEP requirement)

### ACP-5 Filing (Asbestos Control Project - Phase 1)
**Purpose**: Notification to DOB that asbestos work will occur

**When Required**:
- Any project with known or presumed asbestos disturbance
- Required before any ACM removal begins
- Can be filed simultaneously with main permit

**Filing Process**:
1. Complete ACP-5 form with asbestos consultant
2. Include: building age, material inventory, work scope, abatement contractor license
3. File with DOB NOW (electronically) or paper at relevant borough office
4. DOB issues notice of filing within 5-7 days
5. Project cannot commence without ACP-5 approval
6. [VERIFY] Current fee structure for ACP-5 filing

**Required Documentation**:
- Asbestos survey report (certified by licensed asbestos consultant)
- Abatement contractor license verification (OSHA certification)
- Work plan showing removal methodology
- Waste disposal plan and licensed disposal facility contact info

### ACP-7 (Asbestos Control Project - Phase 2)
**Purpose**: Project completion notification and clearance verification

**When Filed**:
- After all ACM removal is complete
- Before occupancy or final CO

**Filing Process**:
1. Obtain final clearance air sample from industrial hygienist
2. Complete ACP-7 form with clearance documentation
3. File with DOB NOW
4. Failure to file can delay final inspection/CO issuance

**Key Documentation**:
- Air clearance certification (visual + air sampling results)
- Waste removal records from licensed disposal facility
- Project completion affidavit from contractor

### DEP vs DOB Coordination
**DOB Jurisdiction**:
- Interior building work on asbestos
- Renovation and alteration projects
- ACP-5 and ACP-7 filings

**DEP (Department of Environmental Protection) Jurisdiction**:
- Demolition projects (mandatory asbestos survey)
- Building deconstruction
- Environmental remediation
- Can issue separate DEP asbestos compliance requirements

**Coordination Strategy**:
- If demolition is planned, route through DEP first
- For renovation, DOB ACP process typically sufficient
- [VERIFY] whether project triggers both DOB and DEP requirements
- Some projects may need parallel filings with both agencies

### Abatement Monitoring
**DOB Role**:
- Issues violation if work proceeds without ACP-5
- Can conduct site inspections during removal
- Enforces proper containment and disposal

**Contractor Responsibilities**:
- Licensed abatement contractor required
- Weekly worker medical monitoring
- Daily site documentation and air monitoring
- Proper waste containment and handling

**Expediter Coordination**:
- Ensure abatement contractor licenses verified before work starts
- Coordinate with main project schedule (abatement often critical path)
- Monitor ACP-7 filing deadline to avoid final inspection delays
- Maintain communication with DOB inspector if on-site monitoring required

### Timeline
- ACP-5 filing to approval: 5-10 business days
- ACP-7 filing to issuance: 3-5 business days (if documentation complete)
- Total asbestos process: 2-4 weeks depending on project complexity

## 2. Subdivision Approval

### Overview
Subdivision creates separate tax lots from one existing tax lot. Required for development scenarios where original lot will be split for separate ownership/financing.

### When Subdivision is Needed
- Separating one tax lot into multiple tax lots
- Creating individual lots for sale or development
- Parcel subdivision (fewer than 5 lots) - administrative process
- Replat subdivision (5+ lots) - requires full survey and filing

**Does NOT require subdivision**:
- Zoning lot creation (multiple tax lots under single development)
- Air rights transfers
- Easement or covenant transfers

### DOB Filing Requirements
**For Subdivision (Parcel)**: [VERIFY] Specific DOB forms required
- Tax lot changes are primarily DOF/ACRIS matter
- DOB involvement limited to ensuring no permit/job impact
- Coordinate DOB alterations if building is affected

**For Replat** (5+ new lots):
- Full survey required (see Survey Requirements below)
- Must follow CPLR Article 54 subdivision procedures
- Requires court filing (unless filed as administrative)

### DOF (Department of Finance) Coordination
**Primary DOF Role**:
- Issues new tax lot numbers
- Updates assessment records
- Updates ACRIS records

**Process**:
1. Prepare subdivision plat (licensed surveyor)
2. File application with DOF (Borough President office routes)
3. DOF issues new tax lots (typically within 30-60 days)
4. Original tax lot is marked "subdivided" in system
5. New tax lots become active

**Required Documentation for DOF**:
- Subdivision plat/survey (filed with Borough President)
- Property deed or ownership proof
- Legal description of original and new lots
- Notarized affidavit of applicant

### Survey Requirements
**Licensed Surveyor Mandatory**:
- NYC Licensed Professional Land Surveyor (PLS)
- Must perform boundary survey if lot corners in doubt

**Subdivision Plat Contains**:
- Current lot boundaries with measurements
- Proposed new lot lines and dimensions
- Existing buildings, easements, restrictions
- Street frontage and access identification
- Department of State title block and surveyor seal

**Timeline for Survey**: 2-4 weeks depending on lot accessibility and complexity

### ACRIS Recording
**What is ACRIS**: Automated City Register Information System
- NYC's official real property records database
- Accessible online via city website

**When Recording Occurs**:
- After DOF issues new tax lots
- New lots automatically appear in ACRIS
- Existing deed remains recorded against original lot
- New lots have clean record (no encumbrances carry forward unless noted)

**Expediter Role**:
- Verify original lot/new lot information in ACRIS post-subdivision
- Confirm tax lot numbers match DOF issuance
- Flag any discrepancies for DOF correction

### Timeline
- Survey preparation: 2-4 weeks
- DOF application to new tax lot issuance: 30-60 days
- ACRIS updates: 5-10 business days after DOF issuance

## 4. Verify Tax Lot

### Overview
Tax lot verification confirms accurate property identification and is essential before starting permits, subdivision, or financing.

### ACRIS Search Process
**Online Access**:
- NYC Department of Finance ACRIS system
- Free online at nyc.gov/finance
- Search by address, tax lot number, or property ID

**Search Results Include**:
- Current ownership information
- Deed history
- Tax lot number and block number
- Property address
- Assessment value (annual update)
- Easements and restrictions
- Previous transfer history

**Search Steps**:
1. Navigate to ACRIS website
2. Enter property address or Block/Lot number
3. Review deed and transaction history
4. Confirm current owner matches project applicant
5. Note any easements or restrictive covenants
6. Print/save for project file

### DOF Property Records
**Additional Information Source**:
- DOF maintains assessment rolls
- Available via ACRIS or paper request
- Shows tax class (residential, commercial, etc.)
- Lists assessment value and tax amount
- Indicates if property is exempt (HPD, public benefit, etc.)

**Information Included**:
- Full legal address and location
- Current assessment value
- Tax class and percentage
- Building class
- Lot size and building square footage
- Neighborhood code

**How to Obtain**:
- Online via ACRIS (preferred)
- [VERIFY] Paper request process and DOF office contacts
- Request includes deed copy if needed

### Tax Lot vs Zoning Lot Distinction
**Tax Lot**:
- Individual parcel for property tax purposes
- May be all or part of a building
- Identified by Block and Lot number in ACRIS
- Separate assessment and tax bill

**Zoning Lot**:
- Land area used for zoning compliance calculations (FAR, setbacks, height)
- May include multiple tax lots
- May be assembled lots under unified ownership
- Not a separate record but a planning/zoning concept
- Important for development rights and bonus floor area calculation

**Practical Implications**:
- Building permit applicant must own full zoning lot
- May require multiple tax lot purchases for development
- Subdivision may create new zoning lot opportunities
- Zoning lot merger possible if contiguous and under same ownership

### Verification Checklist
- [ ] Current owner matches permit applicant name
- [ ] Tax lot number confirmed (Block/Lot)
- [ ] Full address matches project scope
- [ ] No deed restrictions affecting proposed work
- [ ] No easements granted to third parties
- [ ] Property tax status confirmed (not in arrears)
- [ ] Building class appropriate for proposed use

### Timeline
- ACRIS search: immediate (online)
- DOF records request: 3-5 business days if requested by mail

## 5. Address Verification

### Overview
Proper address verification ensures DOB has correct building location and prevents permit rejections due to address mismatch.

### How to Verify/Obtain Building Address
**DOB BIS Address Search**
- Use DOB Building Information System (BIS)
- Access via DOB website or through BIS account
- Search by street address or tax lot number
- Returns: current BIN (Building Identification Number), address format, zip code

**Address Format Requirements**:
- Street number, street name, borough
- Apartment/suite number if applicable
- DOB requires specific formatting for DOB NOW filing
- Address must match USPS records for best results

**Borough President Address Assignment**
- If building lacks official city address, Borough President office assigns one
- Typically needed for: new buildings, subdivisions with new structures
- [VERIFY] Borough President office procedures for address assignment
- Timeline: 5-10 business days from request

**When Address Verification is Needed**:
- New construction (building doesn't yet exist in BIS)
- Subdivided property (new tax lot may need address)
- Change of address (building renumbered)
- Converting tax lots (address may change)
- Permit application rejection citing address issues

### DOB BIS Process
**Accessing BIS**:
1. Create account with DOB (via BIS registration)
2. Search property by address
3. Confirm BIN and official address
4. Download/print verification for project file

**Information Retrieved**:
- Building Identification Number (BIN)
- Official address as recorded by DOB
- Zip code and borough code
- Current building class
- Number of floors and units (if applicable)

### Address Assignment from Borough President
**Required Documentation**:
- Legal description of property
- Site plan showing address location
- Proof of ownership
- For new buildings: foundation permit or certificate

**Process**:
1. Contact Borough President's office (address assignment division)
2. Submit required documentation
3. Address assigned by field verification
4. Address officially recorded with DOB
5. BIS updated within 5-10 business days

**Timeline**:
- Application to address assignment: 3-7 business days
- BIS update: 5-10 business days after assignment

### Verification Checklist
- [ ] Address matches property deed/tax lot
- [ ] BIN confirmed in DOB BIS
- [ ] Address format matches DOB BIS official format
- [ ] Zip code correct
- [ ] Apartment/suite number correct (if applicable)
- [ ] Address matches survey/subdivision plat if new lot

## 6. 10-Day Notices

### Overview
10-day notices alert adjacent property owners of upcoming work that may affect their properties. Required in specific circumstances by NYC law.

### What are 10-Day Notices
- Legal notification served on owners of adjacent/abutting properties
- Provides 10-day advance notice of proposed work
- Required for certain construction activities
- Failure to serve can result in DOB violations and work stops

### When Required

**Excavation Work**:
- Any excavation within 10 feet of adjacent property
- Basement or foundation excavation
- Pile driving or deep foundation work
- Caisson or underpinning work

**Foundation Work**:
- Removing or modifying existing foundation
- Underpinning adjacent buildings
- Soil stabilization near property line

**Other Triggering Work**:
- Demolition (adjacent properties)
- Shoring or bracing installation
- Deep excavation affecting groundwater
- [VERIFY] Additional activities triggering 10-day notice requirement

**Does NOT Require 10-Day Notice**:
- Interior renovations (not affecting structure)
- Roofing or facade work
- MEP system upgrades
- Normal maintenance

### Service Requirements
**Who Must Be Served**:
- Owners of all properties within 10 feet of proposed work
- Identified from tax assessor records or ACRIS search
- May include multiple owners per property (corporate entities, co-owners)

**How Service is Performed**:
- Personal delivery (hand delivery to property owner or agent)
- Certified mail with return receipt (preferred, creates documentation)
- Published notice in newspaper if owner cannot be located
- Service must be completed 10 days before work commences

**Content of Notice**:
- Description of proposed work and location
- Name, address, and phone of project manager/contractor
- Proposed start and end dates
- Site address and address of adjacent property receiving notice
- Signature of project manager and date

**Proof of Service**:
- Certified mail return receipts (required)
- Signed affidavit of service (if hand-delivered)
- Newspaper publication proof (if published notice used)
- All documentation must be retained for DOB inspection

### 10-Day Notice Process
**Step 1: Identify Adjacent Properties** (2-3 days before filing for permit)
- Map property location with surveyor
- Identify all properties within 10-foot radius
- Obtain owner names from ACRIS or property assessor

**Step 2: Prepare Notices** (2-3 days)
- Draft notice letter on company letterhead
- Include all required information
- Prepare certified mail labels for each owner

**Step 3: Serve Notices** (10 days before work starts)
- Mail certified letters to identified owners
- Retain return receipts
- Document names and addresses of all parties served
- Prepare affidavit of service

**Step 4: File with DOB**
- Include 10-day notice documentation with permit application
- OR file separately with DOB after service is complete
- [VERIFY] DOB submission procedures for 10-day notice proof

**Step 5: DOB Verification**
- DOB inspects service documentation
- May contact adjacent property owners to verify notice received
- If service inadequate, DOB may issue violation and halt work

### Documentation Required
- Copy of notice sent to each property owner
- Certified mail receipts or signed affidavits
- Complete list of properties notified
- Map showing property location and 10-foot radius
- All documentation must be on site during construction

### Timeline
- Identify adjacent properties: 2-3 days
- Prepare and serve notices: 5-7 days
- Receive certified mail returns: 5-10 days
- Wait period: 10 days minimum after last service
- Total process: 20-30 days before work can start

### Common Issues
- Incomplete adjacent property identification
- Failure to serve all required owners
- Serving notice fewer than 10 days before work start
- Lost or incomplete certified mail documentation
- Adjacent property owner disputes (may file objection with DOB)

## 7. Tenant Protection Plan (TPP) Submission

### Overview
Tenant Protection Plans protect residents in occupied buildings during construction. Required for work in buildings with rent-regulated tenants or SRO residents.

### What is TPP
- Legal requirement to minimize tenant disruption during construction
- Filed with HPD (Housing Preservation Department)
- Outlines protections: noise limits, dust control, work hours, relocation if needed
- Enforced by HPD; violation can halt construction

### When TPP is Required

**Triggering Tenant Types**:
- Rent-regulated tenants (rent-stabilized, rent-controlled)
- SRO (Single Room Occupancy) residents
- Subsidized housing residents (Mitchell-Lama, etc.)
- Public housing residents in mixed-use buildings

**Triggering Work Types**:
- Any renovation or alteration in occupied building
- Facade work (windows, doors, exterior walls)
- Roofing (if affects interior)
- Major structural work
- MEP system replacement
- [VERIFY] Complete list of work types requiring TPP

**Does NOT Require TPP**:
- Buildings with market-rate tenants only
- Vacant buildings or buildings without residential use
- Non-structural interior work (painting, flooring, bathroom fixtures)
- Emergency work (subject to different rules)

### Filing Requirements

**Where to File**:
- HPD (Housing Preservation Department) office
- NYC Department of Housing Preservation and Development
- [VERIFY] Current HPD office locations and submission procedures
- Electronic filing: [VERIFY] if available via HPD NOW system

**When to File**:
- With or before DOB permit filing
- Some jurisdictions allow filing simultaneously
- Must be approved before construction in tenant-occupied spaces commences

**Required Documentation**:
- Completed TPP form (HPD-provided template)
- Project name, location, description
- List of tenant contact information (obtained during tenant notification)
- Proposed work schedule and duration
- Plan for noise, dust, and disruption control

### Required TPP Components

**1. Tenant Notification & Consultation**
- Tenant meetings held before HPD filing
- Advance notice of proposed work (30 days typical)
- [VERIFY] HPD tenant notification requirements
- Documentation of tenant input and concerns

**2. Relocation Plan** (if applicable)
- If tenants must temporarily or permanently relocate
- Temporary relocation: hotel or alternate space during major work phases
- Permanent relocation: only if building becoming non-residential
- Moving cost coverage by project owner
- [VERIFY] HPD relocation standards and cost limits

**3. Work Hour Restrictions**
- Limiting work hours to protect tenant privacy
- Typical: 8 AM - 6 PM weekdays, limited weekend work
- Restrictions on loud equipment operation during night hours
- Emergency work exceptions

**4. Dust & Noise Control Plan**
- Dust barriers and HEPA filtration for adjacent spaces
- Noise monitoring and complaints process
- Vibration limits for heavy equipment
- Air quality management plan

**5. Tenant Services Plan**
- 24-hour complaint hotline for tenants
- On-site superintendent or project representative contact info
- Regular tenant communication (updates, schedule changes)
- Resolution process for complaints

**6. General Conditions**
- No disconnection of utilities (with exceptions)
- Protecting common area access
- Elevator reservation for tenant use
- Trash and debris management

### HPD Coordination Process
**Initial Submission**:
1. Complete TPP form with all required components
2. Include evidence of tenant notification
3. Submit to HPD (may require multiple copies)
4. HPD issues receipt

**HPD Review**:
- HPD reviews for completeness and compliance (5-10 business days)
- May request modifications or additional information
- HPD inspector may visit site to assess conditions

**Approval & Issuance**:
- HPD issues TPP approval letter
- Approval required before DOB issues permit
- Approval valid for duration of project (may need renewal if extends)

**During Construction**:
- Comply with all TPP components
- Track and address tenant complaints
- May be subject to HPD inspection

### Timeline
- Tenant notification and meetings: 30-45 days
- TPP form preparation: 5-10 days
- HPD review: 5-15 business days
- Total process: 45-70 days before work can start in occupied spaces

### Common TPP Issues
- Inadequate tenant notification (fewer than 30 days)
- Missing tenant contact information
- Unrealistic work schedules (contradicting TPP requirements)
- Insufficient dust/noise control plan
- Missing relocation details (if applicable)
- Inadequate tenant complaint process

## 8. Permit Renewal

### Overview
All DOB permits have expiration dates. Renewal extends the permit's validity and allows work to continue.

### When Permits Expire
**Standard Permit Duration**:
- Alterations (ALT): 180 days from issuance
- New Building (NB): 365 days from issuance
- Demolition (DM): 180 days from issuance
- [VERIFY] Current duration for other permit types

**Expiration Calculation**:
- Counted from permit issuance date (shown on permit)
- Does NOT include time permits were suspended/violation status
- Some permits auto-extend during active construction (require evidence)

**When Renewal Needed**:
- Work incomplete by expiration date
- Permit status active at time of filing
- Work may continue through renewal process while filing pending

### Renewal Process

**Via DOB NOW (Preferred)**:
1. Log into DOB NOW account
2. Locate original permit
3. Select "Renew Permit" option
4. Review permit details and confirm still accurate
5. Submit renewal through DOB NOW
6. Pay renewal fee (typically 50% of original fee)
7. Wait for DOB approval (typically 2-5 business days)

**Paper Renewal**:
- [VERIFY] Paper renewal form and procedures if DOB NOW unavailable
- [VERIFY] Submission location (Borough Office)
- Processing time typically longer (7-10 business days)

**Required for Renewal**:
- Original permit number
- Project applicant still valid (same license/qualifications)
- Work still consistent with permitted scope
- No violations on permit (must resolve first)
- Current progress documentation (if DOB requests)

### Fees
**Renewal Fee Structure**:
- Typically 50% of original permit fee
- Calculated based on renewed work scope
- [VERIFY] Current fee schedule and amounts
- Payment made at time of DOB NOW renewal filing

**Fee Calculation Example**:
- Original ALT permit fee: $500
- Renewal fee: $250 (50%)

### What Happens if Permit Lapses
**Consequences of Expired Permit**:
- Work cannot legally continue
- Continued work without active permit = violation
- DOB may issue violations and impose fines
- Construction must stop immediately
- Incomplete work may be subject to demolition orders

**If Permit Expires**:
1. Stop all construction immediately
2. If only few days overdue: file renewal ASAP (may be accepted retroactively)
3. If significantly overdue: may need to file NEW permit instead of renewal
4. [VERIFY] DOB retroactive renewal policy and conditions

**Recovering from Lapsed Permit**:
- File new permit (treating as new application)
- Pay full permit fees (not renewal amount)
- May require updated documents (new C of O, etc.)
- Timeline extends 2-3 weeks for new processing
- Significant cost implications

### Renewal Tracking
**Expediter Responsibilities**:
- Monitor permit expiration dates (set calendar reminders)
- File renewal 30 days before expiration
- Track approval status in DOB NOW
- Confirm new permit date appears in system
- Communicate approval to project team

### Timeline
- Renewal filing to approval: 2-5 business days (DOB NOW)
- Paper renewal: 7-10 business days

## 9. TPA (Temporary Place of Assembly)

### Overview
TPA permits allow temporary events or gatherings in spaces not licensed as assembly venues. Common for events, festivals, temporary markets.

### What TPA Is
- Temporary Place of Assembly permit issued by FDNY
- Allows non-licensed space to host temporary event/gathering
- Required when occupancy exceeds certain limits in non-assembly spaces
- Time-limited (typically single event, not exceeding 30 days)
- Subject to FDNY fire code compliance

### When TPA is Needed

**Triggering Occupancy Levels** (approximate, [VERIFY]):
- More than 50 people in non-assembly space without proper exits
- Any event in public spaces (parks, streets, vacant lots) with expected attendance
- Temporary restaurants or food service areas

**Examples Requiring TPA**:
- Pop-up retail or restaurant in vacant storefront
- Street fair or festival
- Temporary event space (wedding, concert in non-theater venue)
- Temporary public assembly (community center, school gym for specific event)

**Does NOT Require TPA**:
- Permanent assembly spaces (theaters, concert halls) with valid CO
- Office buildings used for normal business
- Residential spaces for private residential use (non-public)
- [VERIFY] Other exemptions

### FDNY Filing Process
**Where to File**:
- FDNY Fire and Life Safety Division
- Local fire department borough office
- [VERIFY] Current FDNY TPA application procedures and addresses

**Application Timeline**:
- File 5-10 business days before event
- Earlier filing preferred (allows inspection time)
- Emergency/expedited review may be available ([VERIFY] availability)

**Required Documentation**:
- Site plan showing exits and egress routes
- Capacity calculation (maximum occupancy)
- Life safety plan (evacuation, emergency procedures)
- List of safety features (fire extinguishers, alarms, exits)
- Proof of lease or property owner permission
- Event schedule and duration

**FDNY Review**:
- Inspector conducts site inspection (typically required)
- Verifies exits and egress compliance
- Checks fire suppression systems
- Confirms capacity calculations
- May require modifications to plan

**Approval & Issuance**:
- FDNY issues TPA permit letter or certificate
- Valid for specific dates/duration only
- May have conditions or requirements noted
- Must be posted on-site during event

### Filing Requirements Breakdown
**1. Site/Floor Plan**:
- Shows layout of space
- Identifies all exits (with widths)
- Shows egress paths and stairways
- Identifies fire safety equipment locations

**2. Capacity Certification**:
- Calculate based on floor space and exit widths
- [VERIFY] Capacity calculation methodology (square footage per person)
- Support with calculations or engineer certification

**3. Life Safety Plan**:
- Evacuation procedures
- Staff training plan
- Communication system (PA system, etc.)
- Emergency procedures

**4. Fire Protection**:
- Location of fire extinguishers and type
- Sprinkler system status (if any)
- Fire alarm system (if any)
- Distance to hydrants/fire access

### FDNY Coordination
**Inspector Visit**:
- Typically required 2-5 days before event
- Inspector confirms plan matches physical space
- May require additional modifications

**Conditions on Permit**:
- Specific occupancy limit (often lower than requested)
- Equipment or safety modifications required
- Staff or monitor requirements
- Time restrictions (if applicable)

**Post-Approval**:
- Permit must be posted and visible at entrance
- Keep copy on-site during event
- Comply with all conditions noted on permit
- FDNY may conduct spot checks during event

### Timeline
- Application preparation: 3-5 days
- Submit to FDNY: [VERIFY] submission method
- FDNY review and inspection: 3-7 business days
- Approval to event: 2-10 days minimum
- Total: 10-20 days before event date

### Limitations
- Single event only (or series of related events on specified dates)
- Temporary duration (typically 30 days maximum)
- Subject to neighborhood conditions (noise, parking, etc.)
- May require additional permits (DOB, Parks, etc.)
- Not a substitute for permanent CO for ongoing use

## 10. Reassignment

### Overview
Reassignment changes the permit applicant of record without voiding the existing permit. Used when ownership/applicant entity changes mid-project.

### What Reassignment Is
- Change of primary applicant/applicant on record
- Permits remain valid; DOB filing updated
- Different from permit cancellation and reapplication
- Maintains permit number and original issuance date
- Continuity for project schedule and cost

**Applicant of Record**: Entity licensed to perform work type
- General Contractor for full project management
- Architectural firm for design/supervision
- Licensed trades (plumbing, electrical) for specific work
- Must maintain valid NYC license

### When Reassignment is Used
**Contractor Changes**:
- General contractor replaced mid-project
- Original GC contract terminated
- New GC takes over remaining work

**Ownership/Entity Changes**:
- Building sold to new owner
- New ownership entity assigned work
- Corporate restructuring or merger
- [VERIFY] whether ownership change alone triggers reassignment need

**Applicant Entity Changes**:
- Licensed professional leaves firm
- Professional relocated/changed firms
- Firm no longer holds required license
- Need for different license type

**When NOT Used**:
- Minor changes to subcontractors
- Change of project manager (not applicant entity)

## 17. Letter of Completion

### Overview
Letter of Completion (LOC) is an alternative to Certificate of Occupancy for certain projects. Allows building occupancy without full CO issuance in specific circumstances.

### What Letter of Completion Is
- DOB-issued document certifying work completion
- Authorizes building occupancy without full CO
- Used for partial projects, renovations, or specific-scope work
- Does not confirm full code compliance (unlike CO)

**LOC vs CO Comparison**:
| Aspect | LOC | CO |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Scope** | Specific work scope only | Entire building occupancy |
| **Compliance** | Work scope compliant | Full building compliant |
| **Occupancy** | Partial/temporary | Full/permanent |
| **Reissuance** | Not needed after CO issued | Permanent until revoked |
| **Typical Use** | Phased renovations | New buildings or major work |

### When LOC is Used Instead of CO

**Phased Construction**:
- Building under construction; some sections ready for occupancy
- LOC allows tenant occupancy while other areas still under construction
- Final CO issued upon project completion

**Partial Scope Work**:
- Renovation of specific floors/units only
- Rest of building continues normal use
- LOC certifies only renovated area

**Temporary Occupancy**:
- Temporary use permit scenarios
- Building occupancy allowed for limited duration
- LOC expires with permit expiration

**Examples**:
- Multi-floor office renovation: LOC for completed floors during phased work
- Ground floor retail conversion: LOC for new retail while upper residential untouched
- Emergency repairs: LOC allows safe occupancy pending full rehabilitation

**Does NOT Use LOC**:
- New buildings (always require CO)
- Building code conversions (require CO)
- Major structural work (require CO)
- Change of occupancy groups (require CO)

### Filing Process for LOC

**Step 1: Verify Eligibility**
- Confirm work scope is limited (not entire building)
- Verify remaining building areas unaffected by work
- Determine rest of building meets existing code
- Consult with DOB if unclear

**Step 2: Prepare LOC Application**
- Complete DOB LOC form [VERIFY] current form designation
- Describe work scope completed
- Identify areas authorized for occupancy
- Include final inspection certification
- Explain why LOC appropriate vs. CO

**Step 3: Schedule Final Inspection**
- Request final inspection for work scope only
- Inspector verifies completed work meets code
- Inspector notes all open/unfinished areas
- Upon approval, inspector certifies readiness for LOC

**Step 4: File with DOB**
- Submit LOC application with inspection certification
- Include supporting documents (as-built plans, etc.)

- DOB reviews for completeness

### Required Documentation for LOC

**LOC Application Package**:
- Completed LOC form (DOB template)
- Final inspection certification
- As-built/completion plans
- Description of work completed
- Description of areas remaining unfinished
- [VERIFY] Additional required documents

**Final Inspection Certification**:
- Signed by DOB inspector
- Confirms specified work scope complete and compliant
- Notes any open items or areas under construction
- Necessary before LOC issuance

### Limitations of LOC vs CO

**LOC Limitations**:
- Does not certify entire building code compliance
- Only certifies specified work scope
- May require future CO if building scope changes
- Some financing/leasing may require CO instead
- May have expiration date (check LOC document)

**CO Advantages**:
- Comprehensive compliance certification
- No expiration date
- Required for most financing scenarios
- Recognized for all occupancy/use purposes

### Common LOC Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Phased Office Renovation**
- Floors 1-3 renovation complete; Floors 4-10 under construction
- LOC issued for Floors 1-3 occupancy
- Final CO issued when entire building renovation complete

**Scenario 2: Retail Ground Floor Conversion**
- Ground floor converted to retail; Upper floors residential unchanged
- LOC issued for retail occupancy
- Existing residential occupancy continues under existing CO
- LOC becomes part of building record

**Scenario 3: Emergency Repair Work**
- Building sustains damage; emergency repairs underway
- LOC allows occupancy during repair phase
- Final CO issued upon completion

### Timeline
- LOC application preparation: 2-3 days
- Final inspection scheduling: 5-10 days
- Inspection to issuance: 3-7 business days
- Total: 10-20 business days

### Post-LOC Considerations
- Track expiration date if LOC has one
- Plan for CO issuance (if necessary) before LOC expires
- Maintain LOC copy in building records
- Update tenant/owner on CO timeline
- Plan inspections for remaining work scope
