# FDNY Withdrawal Processing Timelines

**Based on Green Light Expediting's experience processing 200+ FDNY withdrawals (2023-2026)**

## Executive Summary

FDNY withdrawal timelines vary significantly by filing type and whether there are open issues. The official answer of "1-2 weeks" is often incorrect. Here's what actually happens in practice.

---

## Realistic Processing Times by Filing Type

### Fire Alarm Withdrawals
**Typical timeline:** 7-14 business days  
**Best case (clean submission):** 5-7 business days  
**Worst case (complications):** 3-4 weeks

**Why fire alarms take longer:**
- Often require manual review by FDNY plan exam
- May be linked to active DOB jobs that need coordination
- Inspection scheduling conflicts can delay processing

---

### Sprinkler/Standpipe Withdrawals
**Typical timeline:** 7-14 business days  
**Best case (clean):** 5-10 business days  
**Worst case (complications):** 2-4 weeks

**Common delay factors:**
- Manual review required if system is already installed
- Outstanding inspection requests delay withdrawal
- Linked to DOB job status (if building permit is still active)

---

### Commercial Hood/ANSUL Withdrawals
**Typical timeline:** 5-10 business days  
**Best case (clean):** 3-5 business days  
**Worst case (complications):** 2-3 weeks

**Why these are faster:**
- Less complex review process
- Usually not tied to larger DOB jobs
- FDNY commercial hood unit processes these quickly

---

## What Delays the Process (2-4+ Weeks)

### Major Red Flags:
1. **Open FDNY objections or notices** - Must be resolved before withdrawal
2. **Outstanding invoices or late fees** - FDNY won't process until paid
3. **Linked to active DOB job** - FDNY may want to coordinate with DOB or wait for DOB permit to be closed
4. **Not filed by original applicant** - Additional verification required if consultant is withdrawing on behalf of someone else
5. **Missing or incomplete paperwork** - Withdrawal letter must reference correct job number/address
6. **FDNY backlog** - Processing times increase during busy seasons (varies)

### Minor Delays (Add 3-5 Days):
- Withdrawal letter missing signature or proper authorization
- Incorrect job number referenced
- Address discrepancy between withdrawal letter and FDNY records
- Need to update contact information before processing

---

## What Speeds Up the Process (Under 1 Week)

### Factors That Help:
1. **Property owner or attorney submits directly** - Faster than consultant submission
2. **Clean submission with all info** - Job number, address, reason for withdrawal clearly stated
3. **No open items** - No outstanding fees, inspections, or objections
4. **Specific urgency noted** - Withdrawal letter mentions specific reason (e.g., "need to refile with corrected plans")
5. **Follow-up call to FDNY** - Calling plan exam unit after 7 days can sometimes expedite

---

## The Step-by-Step Process

### Standard FDNY Withdrawal Workflow:

1. **Submit withdrawal letter to FDNY**
   - Must reference job number and property address
   - State reason for withdrawal
   - Include signature from authorized party (owner, owner's rep, or original applicant)

2. **FDNY internal processing: 5-10 business days**
   - Plan exam unit reviews request
   - Checks for open items (fees, inspections, objections)
   - Confirms no active inspection scheduled

3. **Status check**
   - Call FDNY plan exam unit after 7 business days if no update
   - Check online FDNY portal for status updates
   - Email confirmation sometimes sent, but not always

4. **Removal from system**
   - Once processed, job shows as "withdrawn" in FDNY portal
   - May take additional 1-2 days to reflect in DOB BIS if jobs are linked

## Common Gotchas (GLE Lessons Learned)

### Issue #1: "Under Active Review"
**Problem:** If FDNY is actively reviewing plans when you submit withdrawal, they may want to **complete the review** rather than withdraw.

**Solution:** Ask FDNY if they'll accept withdrawal or if you should wait for review to finish. Sometimes it's faster to let them finish and issue objections, then withdraw.

---

### Issue #2: Linked DOB Jobs
**Problem:** If FDNY filing is tied to a DOB permit (common for new buildings), FDNY may coordinate with DOB before processing withdrawal.

**Solution:** Withdraw or close the DOB job first, THEN submit FDNY withdrawal. Or note in withdrawal letter that DOB job is being closed simultaneously.

---

### Issue #3: Inspection Already Scheduled
**Problem:** If an inspection is already on FDNY's schedule, they may complete it before processing withdrawal.

**Solution:** Call FDNY to cancel inspection BEFORE submitting withdrawal letter. Get confirmation inspection is cancelled.

---

### Issue #4: Outstanding FDNY Fees
**Problem:** Even small outstanding fees ($50-$100) can block withdrawal processing.

**Solution:** Check FDNY account for any unpaid fees BEFORE submitting withdrawal. Pay immediately if found.

## Pro Tips for GLE Team

### When Client Asks: "How long will FDNY withdrawal take?"

**Good answer:**
> "Typically 7-14 business days if there are no open items. I'll check your FDNY account for any outstanding fees or inspections first. If everything is clean, we can usually get it done in under 2 weeks. If there are complications, it could take 3-4 weeks."

**Bad answer:**
> "1-2 weeks" (too vague, often wrong)

---

### When to Set Expectations Higher:

Tell client **3-4 weeks** if:
- You see open objections in FDNY system
- Outstanding fees or invoices
- Job is linked to an active DOB permit
- Client is not the original applicant
- It's a fire alarm with complex system details

Tell client **7-10 days** if:
- Clean account, no open items
- Commercial hood or simple sprinkler
- Owner or attorney is submitting (not consultant)

---

### When Client Wants It "Expedited"

**Reality check:** FDNY does **not** offer official expedited processing for withdrawals.

**What sometimes works:**
- Property owner calling FDNY directly and explaining urgency
- Attorney submission with specific legal reason noted
- Following up with FDNY plan exam unit after 5-7 days

**What doesn't work:**
- Offering to pay more (not an option for withdrawals)
- Submitting multiple times (just clogs the system)

## How to Track Status

### Option 1: FDNY Online Portal
- Log into FDNY NOW or legacy FDNY system
- Search by job number
- Status will update when withdrawal is processed
- May lag by 1-2 days after actual processing

### Option 2: Call FDNY Plan Exam Unit
- Call the borough-specific plan exam unit
- Have job number ready
- Ask for status update
- Best to call after 7 business days, not before

### Option 3: Email Confirmation
- FDNY sometimes sends email confirmation when withdrawal is complete
- **Not reliable** - don't count on getting this
- Always verify in portal or by phone

## When to Bill for FDNY Withdrawal

### Included in Original Scope:
- Simple withdrawal as part of project close-out
- Client decided not to proceed before filing (abort early)

### Bill Separately:
- Withdrawal required due to major plan changes mid-project (client's decision)
- Multiple attempts needed due to FDNY complications
- Coordination with DOB job closure required
- Involves >2 hours of follow-up time

**Typical fee:** $500-$1,000 depending on complexity
