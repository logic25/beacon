# DOB Violation Types and Resolution Overview

## DOB Violation Landscape

The Department of Buildings issues multiple types of violations and orders:
- **DOB Violations**: Issued by inspectors; adjudicated at OATH/ECB for civil penalties
- **OATH/ECB Violations**: Administrative violations with monetary penalties
- **Criminal Court Summonses**: Serious violations prosecuted in criminal court
- **Commissioner's Orders**: Non-compliance orders (vacate, unsafe building, etc.)

Each type follows different resolution procedures and carries distinct consequences.

## Violation Types and Categories

### Work Without Permit
**What it is**: Construction/alteration work performed without required DOB permit
**Severity**: Class 1 (immediately hazardous) or Class 2 (major)
**Common scenarios**:
- Renovation started without ALT-1 or PAA
- Structural work without appropriate permit
- MEP systems installed without permit
- Addition built without building permit

**Resolution**:
1. Stop all work immediately
2. File appropriate permit application (ALT-1, ALT-2, or PAA)
3. Bring work into compliance with approved plans
4. Obtain permit issuance
5. Request inspection and violation correction certification
6. Pay civil penalties through OATH/ECB

**Timeline**: 4-12 weeks (depending on application complexity)

---

### Failure to Maintain
**What it is**: Building owner's failure to maintain structure, systems, or conditions to safe standard
**Severity**: Class 2 or Class 3 (depending on hazard)
**Common violations**:
- Deteriorated facade (missing bricks, spalling)
- Roof leaks/damage
- Stairwell deterioration
- Defective windows/doors
- Non-functional exits or safety systems
- Plumbing/HVAC failures affecting habitability

**Resolution**:
1. Identify specific maintenance deficiency (inspect building)
2. Hire contractor to perform repairs
3. Obtain required permits if work is extensive (facade, structural)
4. Complete repairs with licensed contractor
5. Obtain sign-off from licensed architect or engineer if required
6. File Certificate of Correction with DOB
7. Request DOB inspection to verify correction
8. Pay civil penalties

**Timeline**: 2-8 weeks (repair dependent)

---

### Failure to Comply with Approved Plans
**What it is**: Construction work that deviates from plans approved in the permit application
**Severity**: Class 2 or Class 3
**Common scenarios**:
- MEP riser locations changed without approval
- Window locations/sizes altered
- Structural member sizing reduced
- Door swing directions changed
- Ceiling heights modified

**Resolution** (varies by deviation):
1. **Minor deviations**: File design modification (PAA or ALT-C) for approval
2. **Major deviations**: File amended ALT-1 or ALT-2
3. Redesign work to match approved plans OR file amended plans for approval
4. Inspector verifies compliance during rough-in/final inspection
5. Pay penalties for non-compliance period

**Timeline**: 2-6 weeks (depending on amendment scope)

---

### Unsafe Building/Structural Issues
**What it is**: Building condition poses imminent danger to occupants or public
**Severity**: Class 1 (immediately hazardous)
**Examples**:
- Structural cracks/failures
- Compromised floors/stairs
- Hazardous facade condition
- Failed fire separation
- Collapsed mechanical systems
- Load-bearing wall damage

**Resolution**:
1. **Immediate**: Vacate occupants (if required); secure area
2. **Assessment**: Hire licensed PE to evaluate condition
3. **Engineer report**: PE certifies what repairs are needed
4. **Remediation**: Contractor performs repairs per engineer spec
5. **Certification**: PE certifies repairs complete and structure sound
6. **DOB inspection**: Inspector verifies structural integrity
7. **Clearance**: If necessary vacate issued, rescind vacate order

**Timeline**: 2-8 weeks (repair dependent; vacate orders are urgent)

---

### Illegal Conversion
**What it is**: Building altered to different use without legal authorization
**Severity**: Class 2 or Class 1 (depending on hazard)
**Common scenarios**:
- Residential unit illegally subdivided
- Commercial space converted to residential without Certificate of Occupancy
- Dormitory use without approval
- Commercial kitchen in residential unit
- Basement illegally occupied

**Resolution** (two options):
1. **Legalize use**: File ALT-1 or ALT-2 to obtain new Certificate of Occupancy for actual use
   - Update HVAC, fire suppression, egress, etc.
   - Submit revised floor plan
   - Obtain CO for new use
   - Timeline: 8-16 weeks

2. **Restore legal use**: Remove evidence of illegal use, restore to legal use
   - Seal off illegal area or restore to original use
   - Submit restoration plan
   - Request DOB inspection
   - Violation closed
   - Timeline: 2-6 weeks

**Note**: Legalization can be complex and expensive; restoration often faster.

---

### Failure to Certify (Elevators, Facades)
**What it is**: Required professional certifications not filed with DOB
**Severity**: Class 2 or Class 3
**Types**:
- **Elevator certification**: Annual/5-year testing not certified
- **Facade (FISP/LL11)**: Facade inspection report not filed on schedule
- **Other systems**: HVAC, fire suppression certs, plumbing certs

**Resolution**:

**For elevators**:
1. Contractor completes required inspections/testing
2. Licensed elevator contractor files certification with DOB
3. Elevator is cleared to operate
4. Pay penalties for violation period

**For facade (FISP/LL11)**:
1. Hire Qualified Exterior Wall Inspector (QEWI)
2. QEWI performs facade inspection per LL11 requirements
3. QEWI files inspection report with DOB
4. Address any violations/issues noted in inspection
5. Request follow-up inspection if needed
6. Pay penalties

### Immediately Hazardous Violations
**What it is**: Violations posing immediate danger to occupants/public
**Severity**: Class 1
**Examples**:
- Occupied building structural failure
- Fire safety system non-functional
- Electrical hazard
- Hazardous materials exposure
- Inadequate means of egress

**Resolution**: As with unsafe buildings — immediate engineer assessment, remediation, certification, DOB inspection.

**Timeline**: Urgent; typically 3-5 days to remediation unless extensive work required.

## Vacate Orders and Related Enforcement

### DOB Vacate Order
**Issued by**: DOB (structural/fire safety)
**Reason**: Building unsafe for occupancy
**Effect**: Must vacate building; cannot re-occupy until rescinded

**Resolution**:
1. Hire licensed PE
2. PE certifies building is safe for occupancy
3. Submit engineer certification to DOB
4. DOB inspector verifies (may not be necessary if cert is clear)
5. Vacate order rescinded; re-occupancy permitted

**Timeline**: 3-14 days (urgent; affects occupants)

## Impact on Property Transactions and Financing

**Open violations block**:
- **New permits**: Applications may be denied or delayed
- **Certificates of Occupancy**: Cannot be issued with unresolved violations
- **Sales/Closings**: Lenders may require resolution; title companies may hold funds pending resolution
- **Refinancing**: Lenders will not fund if open violations on property
- **Property valuations**: Violations reduce property value

**Priority**: Always resolve violations before closing or before filing new applications.

**Title search impact**: Violations appear on title search; purchasers/lenders see violations.

**General principles**:
1. **Safety-critical first**: Immediately hazardous, unsafe structure, vacate orders
2. **Permit-blocking second**: Work without permit, illegal conversion (block new permits)
3. **Transaction-blocking third**: Failure to maintain, failure to certify (affect closings)
4. **Minor last**: Signage, posting issues (minimal impact)

**Strategy**: Resolve permit-blockers before filing new applications; resolve transaction-blockers before closing date.

## Pro Tips for Efficient Resolution

[VERIFY: Any specific tricks or common mistakes to avoid?]

- **Document everything**: Photos, timelines, contractor invoices — all support Certificate of Correction
- **Use licensed professionals**: Architects, engineers, contractors for certifications carry weight with DOB
- **File promptly**: Don't let violations age; recent corrections are more credible
- **Request inspection scheduling**: Don't assume DOB will schedule; follow up via 311 or DOB NOW
- **Pay attention to class**: Class 1 violations are urgent; more expensive to ignore
- **Check for default status**: If you miss hearing date, violation goes to default; track OATH schedules
- **Obtain permits for remedial work**: If substantial repair work required, may need permit (PAA)
- **Follow up on filings**: Confirm DOB NOW filings are received; some get lost

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Ignoring violation notices (they don't disappear)
- Missing OATH hearing dates (default increases penalties 25-50%)
- Filing incomplete Certificates of Correction (DOB rejects; delays resolution)
- Starting remedial work before permit approved (compounds violation)
- Not using licensed professionals for certifications (DOB may not accept)
- Assuming DOB will close violation after cert filed (must follow up)
- Continuing unpermitted work while permit is pending (criminal exposure)
