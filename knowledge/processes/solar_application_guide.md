# Solar Application Approval Guide

## Overview
Solar installations in NYC require coordination across multiple agencies: Department of Buildings (DOB), Fire Department of New York (FDNY), utility providers (Con Edison), and potentially the Landmarks Preservation Commission for historic properties. This guide outlines the approval pathway for residential and commercial solar photovoltaic (PV) systems.

## 1. Types of Solar Installations

### Rooftop PV
- Traditional roof-mounted photovoltaic panels
- Mounted on structural racking systems
- Most common installation type in NYC
- Requires roof structural capacity analysis

### Building-Integrated Photovoltaic (BIPV)
- Solar panels integrated into building envelope
- Replaces traditional building materials (roofing, facade, skylights)
- Treated as envelope modification
- May require more extensive architectural documentation

### Canopy Systems
- Freestanding or semi-integrated canopy structures
- Typically installed in parking areas, loading docks, or exterior spaces
- Requires structural design and wind load analysis
- Separate foundation/anchorage requirements

## 2. Permit-Exempt vs DOB Filing Requirements

### 1 RCNY 101-14 Exemptions
Solar installations may be permit-exempt under 1 RCNY 101-14 if meeting ALL criteria:
- Maximum 50 kW capacity (residential) or [VERIFY specific commercial threshold]
- Roof-mounted to existing roof structure with minimal modifications
- Non-structural connection points
- No structural alterations to building
- Complies with all applicable codes (electrical, energy, fire)

**Note:** Permit-exempt status does NOT eliminate other approvals (FDNY, Con Ed, LPC if applicable)

### When DOB Filing IS Required
- Structural modifications to roof system
- Systems exceeding exemption thresholds
- Building-integrated installations
- Canopy systems with new foundations
- Rooftop additions requiring structural support beyond racking
- Installations on buildings in historic districts

## 3. Filing Types

### ALT-2 (Alteration Type 2)
- Most common solar filing type
- Used for non-structural additions and modifications
- Typical for standard rooftop PV on residential or commercial buildings
- Requires Professional Certifier (PC) signature
- Faster review timeline

### ALT-1 (Alteration Type 1)
- Used when structural modifications are necessary
- Required if roof structure cannot accommodate loads without reinforcement
- Requires Licensed Professional Engineer (PE) design and signature
- Longer review timeline due to structural review

## 4. Required Documentation

### Structural Analysis
- Roof load capacity verification
- Dead load + live load calculations per NYC Building Code
- Wind load analysis (ASTM D6861 for rooftop systems)
- Racking system drawings with load ratings
- Anchor point locations and specifications
- Structural PE certification

### Electrical Plans
- Single-line system diagram
- AC/DC wiring layouts
- Inverter specifications and placement
- Disconnect switch locations
- Grounding and bonding details
- Electrical compliance with NYC and NEC standards
- Licensed electrician certification

### Manufacturer Specifications
- Panel technical data sheets
- Inverter specifications and certifications
- Racking system engineering reports
- Battery system specs (if energy storage included)
- Warranty documentation
- UL/IEC certification numbers

### Fire Safety Documentation
- Roof pathways for FDNY access (typically marked on roof plan)
- Setback distances from roof edges, skylights, penthouses
- Fire rating/separation requirements
- Smoke evacuation provisions (if applicable)
- FDNY coordination letter or pre-approval

### Additional Documents
- Property survey or site photos confirming roof condition
- Energy system performance calculation (if pursuing incentives)
- Utility interconnection application (Con Ed pre-approval)
- Property tax abatement forms (PTA-4) if claiming benefits

## 6. FDNY Coordination

### Fire Safety Requirements
- Rooftop solar arrays must not obstruct access pathways
- Maintain clear pathways at least 4 feet wide (or per FDNY approval)
- Setbacks from parapet edges: typically 6-10 feet depending on building configuration
- Setbacks from skylights and roof penetrations per FC 605

### Roof Access and Pathways
- Mark clear pedestrian pathways in red on roof plans
- Ensure pathways allow firefighter access to all roof areas
- Document pathway widths and heights in application

### Pre-Approval Letter
- Request FDNY Letter of No Objection or pre-approval letter
- Submit roof plan showing solar layout and access pathways
- FDNY typically responds within [VERIFY] business days

### Installation Phase
- Contractor must maintain FDNY access requirements during and after install
- FDNY may perform inspection during or after installation
- Final certificate of completion confirms compliance

## 9. Inspection Requirements

### DOB Inspection Points
- Foundation and anchor point installation (rooftop systems)
- Electrical connections and terminations
- Final system configuration and capacity
- Inspector verifies compliance with filed plans

### FDNY Inspection
- Roof access pathway clearance
- Fire safety compliance
- Smoke evacuation (if applicable)

### Utility Final Inspection
- Con Ed may require final inspection before Permission to Operate
- Verifies utility-side connections and metering equipment
- Confirms system compliance with interconnection agreement

### Timeline
- Initial inspection request submitted after installation phase complete
- DOB typically inspects within [VERIFY] business days of request
- Deficiency letters issued if non-compliance noted
- Final approval after all inspections pass

## 10. Common Objections and Issues

### Structural Concerns
- **Objection:** Building structure cannot support additional weight
- **Resolution:** Provide PE-stamped structural analysis with adequate load-bearing verification or reduce system size

### Fire Safety Objections
- **Objection:** Roof pathways blocked or inadequate for FDNY access
- **Resolution:** Revise system layout to maintain required clearances; obtain FDNY pre-approval letter

### Historical Preservation
- **Objection:** Landmark building or historic district restrictions
- **Resolution:** Seek LPC approval for rooftop installation; may require less visible positioning or design modifications

### Utility Interconnection Delays
- **Objection:** Con Ed requests modification due to feeder capacity or distribution limits
- **Resolution:** Reduce system capacity, explore alternative distribution points, or add battery storage

### Electrical Code Violations
- **Objection:** Plan review identifies NEC or NYC electrical code issues
- **Resolution:** Revise electrical design with licensed electrician; resubmit for plan examination

### Title/Ownership Issues
- **Objection:** Question regarding property ownership or solar rights
- **Resolution:** Provide deed, corporate authorization, or tenant consent documentation as applicable

## 12. Best Practices and Tips

- **Early coordination:** Contact FDNY and Con Ed before finalizing designs to identify potential issues
- **Staging documentation:** Have all required documents ready before submitting to accelerate review
- **Professional support:** Use PE/PC familiar with NYC solar approvals to reduce revision requests
- **Manufacturer selection:** Specify equipment with NYC Building Code and FDNY approvals to ease permitting
- **Incentive optimization:** Coordinate NYSERDA application timeline with DOB filing for maximum benefits
- **Maintenance documentation:** Retain all permits, certifications, and inspection reports for future reference
