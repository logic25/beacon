# Items Required to Review: Request for Access Pursuant to RPAPL §881

This checklist is used when a property owner receives a request for access from an adjacent property owner/developer who needs to enter their property for construction work. RPAPL §881 governs the right of access to adjoining property for construction purposes.

## Documents Required from Requesting Party

1. **Complete scope of work**
2. **Schematic drawings**
3. **Contract with general contractor and all sub-contractors**
4. **Architectural drawings / Renderings / Engineers' plans**
5. **All permits** with all governmental/municipal/local agencies
6. **Any violations** issued against the requesting party's property regarding the need for access
7. **Cost and payment schedule** for the work, plus timeline from commencement to conclusion
8. **Specific description of where access is required** on the adjacent property (roof, walls, yard, scaffolding, etc.)

## Insurance Requirements

1. **Full insurance policy** for the requesting party's property (not just an Acord Evidence of Insurance — the actual policy)
2. **All insurance policies** (not Acord) of all contractors and subcontractors to be employed
3. Insurance must **name the property owner, its agents, individual member(s) and employees** as additional insured

## After Review of Items 1-8 and Insurance

The property owner's attorney will advise on:

1. **Specific insurance requirements** not already in policies:
   - Workers' compensation
   - Fire casualty
   - Loss of business
   - Sufficient third-party liability coverage
   - Any additional insurance as needed

2. **Potential effect** of work on owner's use of premises, effect on commercial and/or residential tenants, and steps to ameliorate same — plus required **license fee**

3. **If owner requires engineers/architects** to review plans — cost to be paid by requesting party

4. **Less intrusive methods** — if owner's professionals suggest alternatives, parties agree to negotiate in good faith

## License Agreement

Once all items above are reviewed and agreed upon:
- Requesting party **pays reasonable attorney's fees** for the license agreement
- License agreement is then proposed and negotiated

## Practical Notes for GLE

- This checklist is useful when GLE's clients need access to adjacent properties for construction
- Also useful when GLE's clients ARE the adjacent property owner receiving an access request
- The insurance requirements are strict — Acord certificates are NOT sufficient, full policies required
- License fees and attorney's fees are standard costs the requesting party should budget for
- This process can take weeks to months — factor into project timelines
- RPAPL §881 provides a court remedy if the adjacent owner unreasonably refuses access
