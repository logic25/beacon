# 2022 NYC Building Code - Chapter 9: Fire Protection and Life Safety Systems

## Section 901: General Requirements

### 901.1 Scope
Chapter 9 establishes the general requirements for the design, installation, maintenance, testing, and inspection of fire protection and life safety systems in buildings and structures. These systems include:
- Automatic sprinkler systems
- Standpipe and hose systems
- Fire alarm and detection systems
- Emergency alarm systems
- Smoke control systems
- Fire command centers

### 901.2 Fire Protection Systems Installation and Standards
All fire protection systems shall be installed in accordance with:
- The 2022 NYC Fire Code
- Referenced NFPA standards and other applicable consensus standards
- Regulations established by the Fire Department of New York (FDNY)
- NYC Department of Buildings (DOB) requirements
- Local amendments and guidelines issued by DOB and FDNY

### 901.6 Inspection, Testing, and Maintenance
All fire protection systems shall be:
- Inspected, tested, and maintained in accordance with NFPA standards and NYC regulations
- Subject to periodic inspection by DOB and FDNY
- Records of all inspections and tests must be maintained and made available upon request
- Maintenance contracts must be filed with DOB for systems in commercial buildings
- Defects must be corrected within timeframes specified by DOB/FDNY

## Section 903: Automatic Sprinkler Systems

### 903.1 General
Automatic sprinkler systems shall be installed per:
- NFPA 13 (Standard for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems) - most common in NYC
- NFPA 13D (Standard for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems in One- and Two-Family Dwellings and Manufactured Homes)
- NFPA 13R (Standard for the Installation of Sprinkler Systems in Residential Occupancies up to and Including Four Stories above Grade Plane)

### 903.2 Where Required

**NOTE: NYC 2022 requirements are MORE restrictive than the base IBC model code in several areas.**

#### 903.2.1 Group A - Assembly Occupancies
Automatic sprinkler systems required throughout:
- **All assembly occupancies regardless of occupant load** (NYC amendment significantly more restrictive than IBC)
- Theaters, cinemas, auditoriums, concert halls
- Conference and convention centers
- Restaurants, cafeterias, bars, nightclubs, lounges
- Banquet halls and catering facilities
- Places of worship, community centers
- Sports arenas, gymnasiums
- Dance halls, nightclubs

**Exemption Exception:** Limited exemptions exist for certain low-hazard uses per DOB interpretation (requires verification with specific DOB project).

#### 903.2.3 Group E - Educational Occupancies
Automatic sprinkler systems required throughout:
- Schools (kindergarten through 12th grade)
- Universities and colleges
- Vocational and technical schools
- Training facilities
- School assembly halls and auditoriums

#### 903.2.6 Group I - Institutional Occupancies
Automatic sprinkler systems required throughout:
- Hospitals and medical care facilities
- Nursing homes and skilled nursing facilities
- Assisted living facilities
- Group homes
- Correctional facilities and jails
- Mental health and psychiatric facilities
- Detention facilities

#### 903.2.7 Group M - Mercantile Occupancies
Automatic sprinkler systems required where:
- Fire area exceeds 12,000 SF on a single floor, OR
- Any story above or below grade exit discharge exceeds 2,500 SF, OR
- [VERIFY] Certain hazardous materials storage areas within mercantile occupancies

Specific applications:
- Department stores
- Shopping centers and malls
- Supermarkets
- Retail stores with specific size thresholds

#### 903.2.8 Group R - Residential Occupancies
**NYC 2022 requires sprinklers in substantially ALL new residential occupancies - most stringent in the nation.**

- All multiple-unit residential buildings (R-2) - **NO SIZE EXEMPTION**
  - Apartment buildings
  - Cooperative buildings
  - Condominium buildings
- Hotels and motels (R-1)
- Residential care facilities
- Buildings with Group R occupancy above or below other groups

**NYC Amendment Significance:** The 2022 NYC BC requires sprinklers in all residential buildings, regardless of size or number of stories. This differs significantly from base IBC which does not require sprinklers in all residential buildings.

#### 903.2.9 Group S - Storage Occupancies
Automatic sprinkler systems required where:
- Fire area exceeds 12,000 SF, OR
- Specific hazardous material storage per Section 907.2.10

#### 903.2.11 NYC-Specific Height-Based Requirements
Automatic sprinkler systems required in:
- **Buildings exceeding 100 feet in height** (measured from lowest fire department vehicle access point to roof)
- **Buildings where the highest occupied story floor level is 75+ feet above lowest fire department vehicle access point** (defines NYC "high-rise" for certain purposes)
- All buildings converted from manufacturing to residential or educational use (loft conversions, artist lofts)
- Buildings with occupied basement levels

### 903.3 Installation Requirements

#### 903.3.1 Applicable Standards
- **NFPA 13** for most occupancies (standard in NYC commercial and high-rise buildings)
- **NFPA 13R** for residential occupancies (R-2) up to 4 stories above grade plane - requires lower design density
- **NFPA 13D** for single-family and two-family dwellings (low hazard)

#### 903.3.2 Design and Installation
- Design must be prepared by licensed Professional Engineer (PE) in New York State
- Plans must be submitted for DOB review and approval
- Installation must be per approved plans
- Third-party inspection/testing agency typically required during installation
- As-built documentation required upon completion

#### 903.3.5 Water Supply for Sprinkler Systems
Water supply systems shall be provided per:
- NFPA 13 standards (minimum pressure and volume requirements)
- NYC Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) requirements for municipal water connections
- FDNY requirements for water pressure and volume (minimum 20 PSI residual pressure typically)
- Private water supply options including:
  - Rooftop gravity tanks (elevated storage)
  - Underground storage tanks with booster pumps
  - Direct connection to municipal supply with pressure maintenance (requires FDNY approval)

**DOB Filing Requirements:** Water supply calculations and documentation required in permit application.

#### 903.3.7 Inspection Tags
- New York State Department of Labor (DOL) inspection and certification required for completion
- Certificate of Fitness (C of F) holder required for system acceptance testing

## Section 905: Standpipe and Hose Systems

### 905.1 General
Standpipe systems shall be designed, installed, and maintained per NFPA 14 (Standard for the Installation of Standpipe and Hose Systems). These systems provide water for both fire department operations and building occupant use.

### 905.2 Standards and Installation Requirements
All standpipe systems must comply with:
- NFPA 14 (current edition as adopted in NYC Fire Code)
- Local amendments as established by FDNY and DOB
- Design and construction by licensed PE

### 905.3 Where Required

#### 905.3.1 Building Height Requirements
Class III (wet) standpipes required in buildings where:
- The floor level of the highest occupied story exceeds **30 feet above the lowest fire department vehicle access point**
- Required from lowest level to roof level
- Accessible connections required on each floor via hose stations in stairwells

**NYC Clarification:** Measurement is from lowest fire department vehicle access point (typically street level), not from finished grade at all sides.

#### 905.3.2 Occupancy-Specific Requirements
- **Group A occupancies:** Standpipes required in buildings exceeding 30 feet per 905.3.1
- **Group E occupancies:** Standpipes required in buildings exceeding 30 feet
- **Group I occupancies:** Standpipes required in buildings exceeding 30 feet
- **Group M occupancies:** Standpipes required in buildings exceeding 30 feet
- **Group R occupancies:** Standpipes required in buildings exceeding 30 feet (hotels, apartments)
- **Group S occupancies:** [VERIFY] Standpipes required where building exceeds 30 feet

#### 905.3.4 Stage Standpipes
Class III standpipes (or hose connections) required at each side of stages in assembly occupancies per NFPA 14.

#### 905.3.6 Marinas and Boatyards
Class III standpipes required as applicable to occupancy use and hazards.

### 905.4 Design and Standards

#### 905.4.1 Class III Standpipes
- Most common type in NYC buildings
- Designed for both occupant and fire department use
- Minimum 2.5-inch connection at base for fire department
- Hose stations (65 mm [2.5 inch] connections) on each floor

#### 905.4.2 Water Supply
- Connected to building water supply (municipal or private)
- Pressure maintenance device required
- Booster pump required where municipal pressure insufficient
- Backup water supply required in certain applications (roof tank or pressure tank)

### NYC-Specific Standpipe Requirements
- Combined standpipe/sprinkler systems are standard practice and accepted in NYC buildings
  - Single water supply source acceptable under NFPA 14 guidelines
  - Reduces redundancy while maintaining fire protection
- Siamese (fire department) connections required per FDNY specifications for fire department access to standpipe system
- All connections must be accessible from street level or fire department apparatus access area
- Hose connections must be located in exit stairwells (accessible to building occupants)
- All dry, wet, and combined systems common in NYC; wet systems preferred in buildings where freezing is not a concern

## Section 907: Fire Alarm and Detection Systems

### 907.1 General
Fire alarm and detection systems shall be designed, installed, maintained, inspected, and tested per NFPA 72 (National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code). These systems provide automatic notification of fire to building occupants and the fire department.

**Key Components:**
- Initiation devices (detectors, manual stations)
- Control and signaling equipment
- Notification devices (audible and visible)
- Power supplies (including emergency backup)
- Interconnection (where required)

### 907.2 Where Required

#### 907.2.1 Group A - Assembly Occupancies
Manual and/or automatic fire alarm systems required in:
- Theaters and movie cinemas
- Concert halls and auditoriums
- Lecture halls and conference centers
- Banquet halls and public dining areas
- Night clubs and bars
- Restaurants with certain occupant loads
- Other assembly uses exceeding specified occupant loads per IBC Table 907.2

**Notification Requirements:** Occupant notification system required; voice evacuation systems preferred in large venues.

#### 907.2.3 Group E - Educational Occupancies
Fire alarm systems required in:
- All schools at primary, secondary, and post-secondary levels
- Universities and colleges
- Vocational and technical schools
- Training facilities and educational institutions
- Typically includes automatic detection and manual stations

#### 907.2.6 Group I - Institutional Occupancies
Automatic fire alarm system with automatic smoke detection required in:
- Hospitals and medical care facilities
- Nursing homes and assisted living facilities
- Correctional facilities and jails
- Mental health and psychiatric facilities
- Group homes (certain types)

**Special Requirements:** Manual alarm stations must be accessible; communication with fire department essential.

#### 907.2.8 Group R-1 - Hotels and Motels
Automatic fire alarm system with automatic smoke detection required:
- Manual alarm stations in corridors (guest room floors)
- Smoke detectors in guest rooms and common areas
- Occupant notification system (voice evacuation preferred)
- Fire department notification

#### 907.2.9 Group R-2 - Multiple-Unit Residential (Apartments)
Per NYC Local Law and Building Code requirements:
- Smoke detectors required in each dwelling unit (bedrooms, living areas, kitchens)
- Manual alarm stations in common areas for buildings with fire alarms
- Interconnected detectors in buildings with automatic sprinkler systems (wireless interconnection acceptable)
- [VERIFY] Fire alarm system may be required in certain circumstances (building size, specific hazards)

#### 907.2.10 Single- and Two-Family Dwellings
Smoke alarms required per NYC Administrative Code:
- Interconnected alarms in bedrooms and living areas
- Carbon monoxide alarms where applicable (near sleeping areas if gas heat/fireplace present)
- Battery backup required for all alarms

### 907.3 Automatic Smoke Detection
Automatic smoke detection required in:
- Occupancies where specifically required by occupancy section (per 907.2.x above)
- Buildings with enclosed occupant corridors (hallways, exit corridors)
- Underground portions of buildings
- High-rise buildings (buildings exceeding 75 feet above lowest fire department vehicle access) per NYC amendments
- Atriums
- Enclosed parking garages
- Electrical, mechanical, and telecommunications rooms
- Elevator shafts (per NFPA 72 and building code)

**Detection Types:** Ionization and photoelectric detectors; combination detectors acceptable per NFPA 72.

### 907.4 Manual Fire Alarm Stations
Manual alarm stations (pull stations) required in:
- Assembly occupancies
- Educational occupancies
- Institutional occupancies
- Hotels and residential buildings with fire alarm systems
- Located no more than 5 feet from exits (per NFPA 72)
- Clearly visible and easily accessible

### 907.5 Occupant Notification Devices
All fire alarm systems must include appropriate notification:
- **Audible devices:** Meeting NFPA 72 requirements (typically 70-90 dB depending on occupancy and ambient noise)
- **Visible notification devices (strobes):** Required for occupancies with hearing-impaired occupants; increasingly common requirement in NYC for ADA compliance
- Minimum sound pressure levels as specified in NFPA 72
- Notification must be automatically activated upon system alarm

### 907.6 Voice/Emergency Communication Systems
Where required in certain occupancies:
- High-rise buildings (per NYC amendments)
- Large assembly occupancies
- Institutional occupancies
- Allows for announcement of evacuation instructions
- Assists fire department in directing occupants
- [VERIFY] Specific requirements vary by occupancy

### 907.7 Emergency Alarm Systems

Emergency alarm systems are distinct from fire alarm systems and serve specific purposes:

#### 907.7.1 General Requirements
Emergency alarm systems are required in certain occupancies and provide for communication of occupants with emergency personnel or personnel trained to respond to emergencies.

#### 907.7.2 Manual Emergency Alarm Stations
Manual emergency alarm (e.g., "Call for Help" buttons) systems required in:
- Group I occupancies (hospitals, nursing homes, correctional facilities) - patient/occupant communication system
- Senior living facilities
- [VERIFY] Other institutional occupancies

**Functionality:** Activation sends signal to designated monitoring station or internal monitoring facility; allows building occupants to summon emergency assistance.

#### 907.7.3 Monitoring
Emergency alarm systems must be monitored by:
- Central station (contracted monitoring service), OR
- On-site monitoring facility with continuous staffing, OR
- Direct connection to FDNY (in some cases, per FDNY approval)

### 907.9 NYC-Specific Fire Alarm Requirements for High-Rise Buildings

High-rise buildings (exceeding 75 feet above lowest fire department vehicle access) must include:

#### Voice Communication/Announcement Systems
- Central voice communication capability
- Allows Fire Department to communicate with building occupants
- Evacuation instructions can be broadcast
- Two-way communication capability in certain areas

#### Two-Way Emergency Communication Systems in Stairwells
- Phones or intercoms located in stairwells at specific intervals
- Allows occupants to communicate with fire command center or emergency personnel
- Battery backup power required

#### Fire Command Center Integration
- Fire alarm system monitoring
- Video display of fire detection points and sprinkler activation
- Direct communication with FDNY
- Emergency power backup (required)
- Specialized voice alarm requirements per NYC Fire Code amendments

#### Elevator Recall Systems
- Automatic elevator recall to ground floor upon fire alarm activation
- Integration with fire alarm system

## Section 909: Smoke Control Systems

### 909.1 Scope and Purpose
Smoke control systems shall be installed where required by provisions of the building code for the purpose of:
- Maintaining tenable conditions in occupied spaces during fire emergency
- Facilitating life safety and orderly evacuation
- Aiding fire department operations
- Preventing smoke migration to adjacent fire compartments

**Definition:** Smoke control is the method of limiting smoke spread by mechanical or passive systems.

### 909.2 Where Smoke Control Systems Are Required

#### 909.2.1 Atriums
Smoke management systems required in atriums to:
- Prevent smoke from entering adjacent areas
- Maintain visibility in atrium for evacuation
- Mechanical or natural ventilation acceptable per design
- System activation upon smoke detection

**NYC Application:** Many NYC buildings with atriums require sophisticated smoke control (mechanical ventilation preferred in urban areas with limited natural draft potential).

#### 909.2.2 Underground Buildings
Mechanical smoke control required in:
- Basements or below-grade occupied spaces
- Subway/underground commercial areas
- Underground parking with adjacent occupied spaces
- Systems designed to remove smoke from occupied areas and exhaust outside

#### 909.2.3 High-Rise Buildings (NYC Amendment)
Buildings exceeding 75 feet above lowest fire department vehicle access require:
- **Stairway pressurization systems per NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code)** - primary requirement in NYC
- Designed to maintain positive pressure differential in exit stairwells (typically 0.02 to 0.05 inch of water column)
- Prevents smoke infiltration into stairwells during fire
- Emergency power supply to maintain operation during fire conditions (typically 4-hour backup)
- Activation by fire alarm system or manual override

#### 909.2.4 Residential Buildings
[VERIFY] Certain residential buildings may require stairway pressurization per NYC amendments, particularly high-rise apartments.

#### 909.2.5 Enclosed Parking Structures
[VERIFY] Enclosed parking structures may require smoke exhaust systems, particularly if connected to or beneath occupied spaces.

### 909.3 Design and Installation Standards
- Smoke control systems must be designed by licensed Professional Engineer
- NFPA 101 (Life Safety Code) referenced for design parameters
- Includes mechanical systems, ducting, fans, dampers, controls
- Activation by:
  - Automatic smoke detection (preferred in many cases)
  - Manual controls accessible to Fire Department
  - Fire alarm system integration

### 909.4 Testing and Approval
- Systems must be tested upon completion
- DOB approval required prior to occupancy
- FDNY approval required (smoke control is critical for fire operations)
- Annual testing typically required per NYC Fire Code
- Documentation of testing maintained by building owner

## Section 910: Emergency Voice/Alarm Communication Systems

### 910.1 General Requirements
Emergency voice/alarm communication systems serve to:
- Provide voice alarm (evacuation instructions) capability
- Provide two-way communication between fire command center and occupied areas
- Support FDNY operations during fire emergency

### 910.2 Where Required
[VERIFY] Specific occupancies and building heights requiring these systems per 2022 NYC BC Chapter 9; common in high-rise buildings and certain institutional occupancies.

### 910.3 System Components
- Central voice communication system (typically integrated with fire alarm)
- Speakers in exit stairwells, elevators, lobbies, and occupied areas
- Microphones at fire command center
- Two-way communication devices (phones/intercoms) in stairwells
- Battery backup power

## Section 912: Fire Department Connections (Siamese Connections)

### 912.1 General Requirements
Fire department connections (Siamese connections) required for:
- Sprinkler systems where required (Section 903)
- Standpipe systems where required (Section 905)
- Combined sprinkler/standpipe systems

### 912.2 Location and Accessibility
Fire department connections shall be located:
- Per NYC Fire Department specifications
- Accessible from fire apparatus access road (required minimum 20-foot-wide road)
- At grade level when possible
- Clearly identified with appropriate signage
- At least 18 inches above finished grade (per NFPA 13)
- Not within 10 feet of windows to prevent smoke/water backup into building

### 912.3 Connection Design
- Two separate 2.5-inch connections OR one 4-inch connection per FDNY requirements
  - NYC Fire Department may specify configuration based on building and water supply
- Approved caps and plugs required (no exceptions)
- Clear spacing from obstructions (minimum 36 inches clearance recommended)
- Easy access for FDNY connection of equipment (truck-mounted hoses)

### 912.4 NYC-Specific Requirements for Siamese Connections
- Connections must be inspected and tested by third-party certification agency
- Each connection must be capped when not in service
- Valve boxes or visual indicators required to show connection operational status
- Plaque or sign identifying system (SPRINKLER, STANDPIPE, or COMBINED)
- Annual certification by licensed contractor required in some cases
- [VERIFY] DOB may require specific FDNY-approved connection designs

## Section 913: Fire Command Centers

### 913.1 General and Where Required
Fire command centers are control centers where:
- Fire alarm system status is displayed and monitored
- Building emergency operations are coordinated
- Fire Department can monitor and control certain building systems

**Required in:**
- All high-rise buildings (exceeding 75 feet above lowest fire department vehicle access) per NYC 2022 BC
- Large assembly occupancies with complex fire protection systems
- Certain institutional occupancies (hospitals, nursing homes)

### 913.2 Location Requirements
- Located on ground floor or in immediate lobby area
- Accessible to Fire Department at all times
- Easy identification and access
- Not located in storage areas or mechanical rooms
- [VERIFY] Specific location requirements per NYC DOB

### 913.3 Equipment and Features in Fire Command Center

#### 913.3.1 Fire Alarm Display
- Central monitoring and display of:
  - Zone status (zones with smoke/heat detection)
  - Alarm points and their locations
  - Sprinkler valve status (where monitored)
  - Elevator recall status
  - Stairway pressurization system status
- Visual display system (typically graphic display panel showing building layout)

#### 913.3.2 Communication Equipment
- Telephone connection to Fire Department
- Two-way voice communication with building occupants (via intercom or PA system)
- Connection to emergency voice/alarm communication system
- Notification bell or light indicating alarm condition

#### 913.3.3 System Controls
- Manual override controls for:
  - Smoke control systems (stairway pressurization, mechanical exhaust)
  - Emergency voice/alarm communication
  - Elevator recall (if applicable)
  - Lighting systems (egress lighting)
- Emergency shutdown switches for certain building systems
- [VERIFY] Additional controls based on occupancy type

#### 913.3.4 Power Supply
- Emergency power (battery backup) required
- Typically 4-hour minimum backup capacity
- UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) common in modern systems
- Connection to building emergency power system

#### 913.3.5 Security and Access
- Locked when not staffed (key access for Fire Department)
- Key box or similar device allowing FDNY access during emergencies
- FDNY master key system integration

### 913.4 Documentation and Records
- Operating manuals for all systems displayed/controlled
- Building layout and floor plans showing fire protection systems
- System contact information (fire protection contractors, maintenance providers)
- Emergency procedures posted in command center

## NYC-Specific Amendments and Differences from 2022 IBC Base Code

### Summary of Key NYC Changes in 2022 Edition

#### Sprinkler Requirements
- **NYC requires ALL new residential buildings to have sprinklers** (R-2 occupancies with NO SIZE EXEMPTION)
- Applies to all new apartment buildings regardless of size, height, or number of stories
- Significant difference from base IBC which does not mandate sprinklers in all residential buildings
- Commercial occupancies also more restrictively regulated than IBC (Assembly, Educational, Institutional)

#### High-Rise Definition and Requirements
- **NYC threshold is 75 feet above lowest fire department vehicle access** (lower than standard IBC which uses 75 feet measured differently)
- Results in more buildings classified as "high-rise" requiring enhanced protection
- Requires voice alarm/communication systems (not just fire alarm detection)
- Stairway pressurization mandatory in all high-rise buildings
- Fire command center required in all high-rise buildings

#### Combined Systems Standard Practice
- Combined standpipe/sprinkler systems are standard practice in NYC
- Single water supply source acceptable under NFPA 13 and 14 guidelines
- Reduces redundancy and cost while maintaining protection
- Two-way fire department connections standard

#### Loft Conversions and Manufacturing-to-Residential Conversions
- Manufacturing to residential conversions require sprinklers throughout per 903.2.11
- Common application in lower Manhattan, Williamsburg, Long Island City, and other industrial neighborhoods
- Significant cost impact on conversion projects

#### Two-Way Emergency Communication in Stairwells
- Two-way communication systems (phones/intercoms) required in stairwells of high-rise buildings
- Allows occupants to communicate with fire command center
- NYC-specific amendment enhancing life safety

#### Emergency Voice/Alarm Communication
- Voice evacuation systems increasingly required in NYC buildings
- Allows fire department to provide evacuation instructions to building occupants
- Critical in complex buildings or those with hearing-impaired occupants

#### Smoke Detection in High-Rise Buildings
- Automatic smoke detection required throughout high-rise buildings (per NYC amendment)
- Not just in specific occupancy areas, but more comprehensive than IBC base code

#### Standpipe System Requirements
- More buildings classified as high-rise due to lower 75-foot threshold
- Results in standpipes required in more buildings than under standard IBC
- Combined standpipe/sprinkler systems reduce overall system complexity

### Jurisdictional Authorities
- **DOB (Department of Buildings)** has primary building code authority
  - Plan review and approval
  - Inspection at various stages (foundation, rough-in, final)
  - Certificate of Compliance issuance
- **FDNY (Fire Department of New York)** has primary fire code and operations authority
  - Approval of fire protection system designs and layouts
  - Inspection of completed systems
  - Ongoing compliance and maintenance monitoring
  - Approval of siamese connection locations and design
- **Both agencies must review and approve fire protection systems**
  - Plans submitted to DOB; sometimes copy to FDNY for complex systems
  - Final approval and ongoing inspection responsibility shared
  - FDNY may impose additional requirements beyond code minimum
